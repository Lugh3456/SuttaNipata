第三品·第八经 · 箭经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第三品·第七经 目录 Contents 第三品·第九经 →

人的寿命是不确定的，无法知晓——生命是困难的，短暂的，充满苦恼的。 没有任何方法使出生的人不死——老年来临，然后死亡。 就如熟透的果实早早面临掉落的危险，同样，出生的众生总是面临死亡的危险。 就如陶器师傅制作的陶器最终都会破碎，人的生命也是如此。 年轻者与老年者，愚蠢者与智慧者——所有人都被死亡征服，所有人终将走向死亡。 当他们被死亡所征服，离开这个世界而去——父亲也不能保护儿子，亲属也不能保护亲属。 看！亲属在哭泣，却无力阻止——一个接一个地，像牛被带去屠宰。 这样，世间被老年与死亡所苦恼——因此，智者不悲伤，了知了世间的本质。 你不知道来者的道路，也不知道去者的道路——没有看见两端，你徒劳地哭泣。 哭泣有什么用？如果哭泣能产生任何利益，智者也会哭泣——但哭泣只会令自己疲惫。 他不会因为哭泣而使死去的人回来——悲恸对自己只有害处。 不能使死者回来——只是使自己更加痛苦。 了解失去挚友时的无可救药，智者不悲伤——那是世间的法则。 他不止息燃烧的悲伤——只有导向更大的痛苦。 见到死亡的不可抗拒，心无悲恸的人——他正确地拔出了那支射入自己心中的箭——忧愁。 拔出了箭的人获得了平静——不再哭泣，不再悲伤，已得安稳。

逐句解释

就如熟透的果实早早面临掉落的危险，同样，出生的众生总是面临死亡的危险。就如陶器师傅制作的陶器最终都会破碎，人的生命也是如此。

箭经以一系列无常与死亡的比喻开篇，在大品中是罕见的直接面对死亡的经典。两个比喻精准而不夸张：熟果必落，是自然规律，不是灾难；陶器必碎，是物质的本质，不是缺陷。将人的生命比作这两者，不是贬低生命，而是如实描述它的本质——它从出生的那一刻起，就在走向终结的路上。这种如实的描述，是经文随后关于「如何面对死亡」的讨论的基础：先接受事实，才能讨论如何回应。

As a ripe fruit faces the constant danger of falling, so those who are born always face the danger of death. As pottery made by a potter all comes to breaking, so too is human life.

The Salla Sutta is the Sutta Nipata's most direct contemplation of death and grief. The opening similes — ripe fruit that must fall, pottery that must break — are not morbid but descriptive: they name what is simply true about organic and manufactured things. The application to human life is not a diminishment but an act of honest recognition. The fruit does not resist its ripeness; the potter does not make pots hoping they will be eternal. Only humans — knowing their mortality — attempt to live as if death is not real. The sutta's entire argument rests on this first foundation: see clearly what is true.

当他们被死亡所征服，离开这个世界而去——父亲也不能保护儿子，亲属也不能保护亲属。看！亲属在哭泣，却无力阻止——一个接一个地，像牛被带去屠宰。

死亡的孤独性：在死亡面前，一切社会关系与保护网络都失效。父母无法保护子女，子女无法保护父母，亲属无法保护亲属——死亡是每个人必须单独面对的事。「像牛被带去屠宰」是一个残酷的比喻，但它描述的是每个人都知道的现实：我们看着他人一个接一个地离去，却无力阻止，甚至不知道自己会是下一个。这种明知必然却无法阻止的旁观，是悲恸的核心结构。

When they are overcome by death and leave this world — a father cannot protect his son, relatives cannot protect relatives. Look! Relatives weep, powerless to stop it — one by one, like cattle being led to slaughter.

Death's absolute isolation is the subject of these verses: at the moment of death, every social bond and protective relationship is rendered useless. The cattle simile is stark but not cruel — it names the experience of watching others die in sequence, knowing one is in the same queue, without the power to stop or reverse any of it. The helplessness of relatives is not a failure of love but a structural feature of mortality: each person dies alone, and the witnessing of this reality, repeated death by death, is the ground from which grief grows.

你不知道来者的道路，也不知道去者的道路——没有看见两端，你徒劳地哭泣。哭泣有什么用？如果哭泣能产生任何利益，智者也会哭泣——但哭泣只会令自己疲惫。

经文的核心论点：哭泣（悲恸）不能使死者回来，不能改变死亡的事实，只会令哭泣者自己更加痛苦。「你不知道来者的道路，也不知道去者的道路」——死前从哪里来，死后去往何处，我们都不知道。在这种不确定性中，哭泣是对未知的反应，而不是对已知的事实的适当回应。这不是对悲痛的冷酷否定，而是对哭泣的功用性质疑：如果哭泣能有帮助，智者也会哭——但它不能，所以哭泣只是徒劳的自我消耗。

You do not know the path of one coming, nor the path of one going — seeing neither end, you weep in vain. What is the use of weeping? If weeping produced any benefit, a wise person would weep — but it only exhausts oneself.

The argument is not a dismissal of grief but a functional analysis: weeping that accomplishes nothing is self-harm. The 'paths not seen' refer to the unknown origins and destinations of the dying — the rebirth cosmology in which death is not an absolute end but a transition whose direction is unknown. Within this framework, extreme grief over a transition whose nature is uncertain is epistemically as well as practically confused. The concession — 'if weeping produced benefit, a wise person would weep' — shows this is not the teaching of coldness but of discernment: the question is always 'what actually helps?'

见到死亡的不可抗拒，心无悲恸的人——他正确地拔出了那支射入自己心中的箭——忧愁。拔出了箭的人获得了平静——不再哭泣，不再悲伤，已得安稳。

箭经的名称与核心意象：悲恸（soka，忧愁）被比喻为一支射入心中的箭（salla）。「见到死亡的不可抗拒」——如实接受死亡的必然性——是拔箭的动作：不是情感的压抑，而是对现实的接受使悲恸失去了其扎根的幻象（「如果我足够悲伤，事情会改变」）。拔出了箭（悲恸被理解为非必要的痛苦），就获得了平静——不是麻木，而是不再被无效的痛苦所占据。「已得安稳」（upasammati，平息了）：这是经文给出的最终状态，不是冷漠，而是从徒劳的苦中解脱出来的宁静。

One who sees the irresistibility of death, with a mind free of grief — having correctly drawn out the arrow — sorrow — that has been shot into their heart. With the arrow drawn out, one attains peace — no longer weeping, no longer grieving, one has become secure.

The sutta's central image: sorrow (soka) is an arrow (salla) lodged in the heart. 'Correctly drawing it out' is not suppression but recognition — seeing that death is irresistible and that grief does not change this, one withdraws the investment of pain from the false hope that feeling bad enough will somehow alter reality. When this is genuinely understood, the sorrow dissolves not because feeling is deadened but because the cognitive error that sustained it (that grief is appropriate, useful, or inevitable given the facts) has been corrected. The result — upasammati, peace — is the natural condition of a mind no longer expending itself on an unresolvable project.

总结 · Summary

箭经是佛教文学中最直接面对死亡与悲恸的经典之一。经文以自然比喻（熟果必落、陶器必碎）建立死亡不可避免的基础，随后论证哭泣（悲恸）的无用性：它无法使死者回来，无法改变死亡的事实，只会使自己更加疲惫。经文的核心意象是「箭」（salla）：悲恸是一支射入心中的箭，而「见到死亡的不可抗拒」是拔出这支箭的动作。拔出了箭，就获得了平静——不再哭泣，不再悲伤，已得安稳。这首经不是对死亡的冷漠，也不是对悲恸的否定，而是对「如实见死亡」这一修行的肯定：只有真正接受死亡的必然性，才能从徒劳的苦中解脱，获得安稳。

The Salla Sutta is the Sutta Nipata's most direct meditation on death and grief. Beginning with natural similes (ripe fruit falls, pottery breaks), it establishes death's inevitability as simple fact. It then argues against grief not as a moral failing but as a practical error: weeping that cannot help the dead, cannot reverse death, and only exhausts the living is a form of self-harm. The central image — sorrow as an arrow lodged in the heart — frames the practice: seeing death clearly and accepting its irresistibility is the act of drawing the arrow out. The peace that follows is not coldness or indifference but the natural condition of a mind freed from investing itself in an impossible project. The sutta does not deny that loss is real; it teaches how to meet it without the added suffering of grief that accomplishes nothing.
