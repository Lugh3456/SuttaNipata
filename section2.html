第一品·第二经 · 陀尼耶经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第一品·第一经 目录 Contents 第一品·第三经 →

陀尼耶牧人说：饭已炊熟，乳已挤净，我与家人，同住摩醯河畔；茅屋已盖，火已燃起——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。 世尊回应：我无嗔怒，亦无固执，在摩醯河畔一夜止宿，心已解脱，无所依附；茅屋敞开，火已熄灭——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。 陀尼耶说：蚊虻皆已散去，牛群在茂草中游走，雨来亦能承受——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。 世尊说：我扎好木筏，渡过洪流，抵达彼岸，筏已不再需要，筏已放下——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。 陀尼耶说：我妻温顺，不轻浮，可爱而长相守，我未闻有何过失——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。 世尊说：我心温顺，善加修习，早已解脱，调御已久，我自身无有过失——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。 陀尼耶说：我以自力养活自己，儿女皆健康无病——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。 世尊说：我不以任何人为雇主，以所得为足，四处游方而无病苦——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。 陀尼耶说：我有壮牛，有怀胎母牛，有统领牛群的公牛——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。 世尊说：我没有牛，没有牛犊，我已渡过洪流——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。 陀尼耶说：木桩已深深钉入，新绳无法被牛拉断——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。 世尊说：如公牛断绳，如象断葛藤，我不会再回到胎母之腹——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。 于是大雨倾盆而下，将天地都淹没。陀尼耶闻此，如此说道： 陀尼耶说：我们得到莫大利益，见到了世尊。具有神通、慧眼的您，我们归依！请做我们的师父吧，大牟尼。妻与我皆温顺，愿在正觉者座前修梵行。 魔王说：有子者因子而喜，有牛者因牛而喜，执取带来喜悦——无执取者，必无有喜。 世尊说：有子者因子而忧，有牛者因牛而忧，执取带来忧苦——无执取者，必无有忧。

逐句解释

【陀尼耶】饭已炊熟，乳已挤净，我与家人同住摩醯河畔；茅屋已盖，火已燃起——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。

牧人陀尼耶以自信的语气报告他的一切安排妥当：食物备好，家人安顿，居所齐全，烈火燃旺。他邀请天降大雨——因为他已万事俱备，无所畏惧。

Dhaniya the herdsman: "My rice is cooked, my milk is drawn; I dwell with my family on the banks of the Mahi. My hut is thatched, my fire is lit — if you wish to, sky, then rain."

Dhaniya speaks with the contentment of a man who has arranged his world well. He has food, family, shelter, warmth — the four essentials of a comfortable life. His invitation to the rain is a boast: come what may, I am ready. The Buddha will answer each of these boasts not by undermining them but by pointing to a deeper readiness that no material provision can supply.

【世尊】我无嗔怒，亦无固执，在摩醯河畔一夜止宿，心已解脱，无所依附；茅屋敞开，火已熄灭——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。

佛陀以同样的节律回应，但内容截然不同：他的安稳不来自物质准备，而来自内心的彻底解脱。茅屋敞开——无所隐藏，无需防护；火已熄灭——烦恼之火已息。

The Blessed One: "I am free from anger, free from stubbornness; I dwell one night on the banks of the Mahi, released, without clinging. My shelter is open, my fire is quenched — if you wish to, sky, then rain."

The Buddha matches Dhaniya's metre and refrain but transforms every image. Where Dhaniya's fire is lit for warmth and cooking, the Buddha's fire is quenched — the fires of greed, aversion, and delusion. Where Dhaniya's hut is thatched and sealed, the Buddha's shelter is open — no fixed identity to defend, nothing to protect from exposure. The one-night dwelling underscores non-attachment to place.

【陀尼耶】蚊虻皆已散去，牛群在茂草中游走，雨来亦能承受——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。

陀尼耶继续炫耀：困扰已除，牛群无忧，环境舒适——他有充分的外部条件，足以从容面对风雨。

Dhaniya: "Gnats and flies are not to be found; cattle graze in the watered meadow. They could endure the rain if it came — if you wish to, sky, then rain."

Dhaniya's world is functioning as it should: pests removed, livestock thriving, the environment managed. This is the picture of a competent, prosperous householder. The Buddha's reply will show that external comfort, however well arranged, leaves the deeper problem untouched.

【世尊】我扎好木筏，渡过洪流，抵达彼岸，筏已不再需要，筏已放下——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。

佛陀用筏喻作答：修行之道犹如木筏，帮助渡过轮回的洪流；一旦抵达彼岸，筏本身也应放下，不再执取。

The Blessed One: "My raft is well-made and well-crossed; the flood is crossed and the far shore reached, with no further need of the raft. The raft has been released — if you wish to, sky, then rain."

The raft simile is one of the most famous in the Canon: the Dhamma is like a raft, used to cross the flood of conditioned existence, then set down rather than carried on one's head. The Buddha has not only built the raft — he has crossed, arrived, and released even the means of crossing. Where Dhaniya manages things to be safe, the Buddha has arrived at a safety that needs no further management.

【陀尼耶】我妻温顺，不轻浮，可爱而长相守，我未闻有何过失——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。

陀尼耶以妻子为傲：她贤良、忠诚、可爱。这是家庭生活中最令人满足的一面——良伴在侧，无有纷争。

Dhaniya: "My wife is compliant, not wanton, pleasant, long-dwelling together; I hear of no fault in her — if you wish to, sky, then rain."

Dhaniya's third boast moves from material comfort to human relationship — his marriage is harmonious, his wife is virtuous. This is a genuine human good. The Buddha does not dismiss it; he offers its counterpart at the level of mind rather than household.

【世尊】我心温顺，善加修习，早已解脱，调御已久，我自身无有过失——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。

佛陀以自心作答：经过长期修行，心已温顺、解脱、无过。这是比良妻更深的安顿——一颗与自己彻底和解的心。

The Blessed One: "My mind is compliant, well-released, long-trained; no fault is to be found in me — if you wish to, sky, then rain."

The parallel is exact: Dhaniya praises his wife; the Buddha praises his mind. But the mind, unlike a spouse, is the practitioner's own responsibility. A well-trained mind is a companionship that cannot be lost to illness, estrangement, or death. 'No fault to be found' echoes the arahant's freedom from the ten fetters — nothing binding, nothing obscuring.

【陀尼耶】我以自力养活自己，儿女皆健康无病——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。

陀尼耶以自力更生为傲，并以子女的健康作为生命圆满的佐证。这是世俗成功最完整的图景：自给自足、子孙兴旺。

Dhaniya: "I support myself by my own earnings; my children are healthy, no illness among them — if you wish to, sky, then rain."

Self-reliance and healthy children: two of the most universally valued goods in any culture. Dhaniya's world is enviably complete. The Buddha's response will not deny these goods but will show what remains unaddressed even within them.

【世尊】我不以任何人为雇主，以所得为足，四处游方而无病苦——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。

佛陀不依赖任何人，随所获而知足，自由游方，身心无病。这是比世俗自立更彻底的自由——不为任何人或事所驱役。

The Blessed One: "I work for no master; what I find I take as sufficient; I wander freely and am without illness — if you wish to, sky, then rain."

Dhaniya's self-support is still embedded in the economy of the household: earnings, children, the structure of family life. The Buddha's freedom is of a different order — no employer (no craving driving his actions), no fixed address, contentment with whatever comes. This is the freedom of non-dependence, not the security of provision.

【陀尼耶】我有壮牛，有怀胎母牛，有统领牛群的公牛——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。

陀尼耶以畜牧业的兴旺为傲：有力壮的耕牛，有繁殖能力的母牛，有领导牛群的公牛。这是农牧社会财富的象征。

Dhaniya: "My cows are mature, my heifers are with calf; the bull is master of the herd — if you wish to, sky, then rain."

In the agrarian economy of ancient India, cattle were wealth. Dhaniya has not just cattle but the full productive capacity of a herd: mature cows, pregnant heifers, and a dominant bull. His prosperity is generative, self-sustaining. The Buddha's response strips away all of this — not as loss but as freedom.

【世尊】我没有牛，没有牛犊，我已渡过洪流——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。

佛陀没有牛、没有牛犊——也没有任何财产可以系缚他。无所拥有，正是他已渡过轮回洪流的标志。

The Blessed One: "I have no cows, I have no calves; I have crossed the flood — if you wish to, sky, then rain."

The starkness is deliberate. Dhaniya has; the Buddha has not — and this 'not-having' is the condition of crossing. Possessions anchor: cattle need pasture, heifers need protecting, the herd needs managing. What we own, owns us in return. The Buddha's 'having crossed the flood' is the payoff of complete non-possession.

【陀尼耶】木桩已深深钉入，新绳无法被牛拉断——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。

陀尼耶最后的自豪：他的牛群被牢固地拴住，不会逃脱，不会散失。他的世界是安全的、可控的。

Dhaniya: "The stakes are well-driven in; the new grass ropes cannot be pulled loose by the cows — if you wish to, sky, then rain."

Dhaniya's final boast is about control: everything is staked down, nothing can slip away. This is the householder's ultimate aspiration — a world secured against loss. But stakes and ropes are exactly what the Buddha's response will refuse. The image of binding is about to be turned on its head.

【世尊】如公牛断绳，如象断葛藤，我不会再回到胎母之腹——天神啊，若你欲降，请降吧。

佛陀如猛牛断绳，如大象挣脱葛藤，彻底斩断了轮回的系缚——不再回到任何存有的胎中，永不再生。

The Blessed One: "Like a bull I have broken the bonds; like an elephant I have torn through the creeper; I shall not return to the womb again — if you wish to, sky, then rain."

The two images — bull breaking rope, elephant tearing creeper — are images of overwhelming, irresistible release. Where Dhaniya stakes things down, the Buddha breaks free. Where Dhaniya's ropes hold cattle, the Buddha has snapped the bonds of becoming entirely. 'No return to the womb' signals final liberation: the conditions for rebirth have been uprooted.

大雨倾盆而下，将天地淹没。

对话结束，天遂人愿，大雨倾盆而下。陀尼耶早有准备，安然无恙；而佛陀，更是早在另一个层次上做好了准备。

Then the sky rained down, filling the hollows and the heights.

The rain arrives — Dhaniya's hut holds, his cattle are tethered, his provisions are safe. He was ready, as he said. But the exchange has shown a second kind of readiness, operating at a different level entirely: not the readiness of having made adequate arrangements, but the readiness of having let go of the need for arrangements.

【陀尼耶】我们得到莫大利益，见到了世尊。请做我们的师父！妻与我皆温顺，愿在正觉者座前修梵行。

听完对话、经历大雨之后，陀尼耶深受触动，与妻子一同归依佛陀，请求成为在家弟子，在佛陀座前奉行清净之道。

Dhaniya: "We have gained a great good, having seen the Blessed One with the divine eye. Be our teacher, great Sage — my wife and I are compliant; we will live the holy life in the presence of the Awakened One."

Dhaniya's transformation is sudden but not inexplicable. He is a man who has always known what readiness looks like — he has practised it in his household his whole life. Now he recognises a deeper version of it. His request to 'live the holy life' (brahmacariya) as a householder means taking up the precepts and spiritual practice within his existing life, not abandoning it.

【魔王】有子者因子而喜，有牛者因牛而喜，执取带来喜悦——无执取者，必无有喜。

魔王（波旬）趁机插话，试图为执取辩护：拥有才有喜悦，无所拥有则无喜。这是世间常见的逻辑，也是系缚众生的根本幻觉。

Māra: "One with children rejoices because of children; one with cattle rejoices because of cattle. Clinging is a man's delight — one with no clinging has no delight."

Māra's intervention is perfectly timed and perfectly argued — from the perspective of conditioned existence, he is right. Children do bring joy. Cattle do bring satisfaction. Clinging does produce pleasure. What Māra does not say — what he cannot say — is that it also produces grief, fear, and loss in equal measure. The Buddha's reply completes the equation.

【世尊】有子者因子而忧，有牛者因牛而忧，执取带来忧苦——无执取者，必无有忧。

佛陀将魔王的话逐字翻转：执取不只带来喜悦，也带来忧苦。无执取者，不是没有喜悦——而是没有忧苦。这是解脱之乐，超越了得失的循环。

The Blessed One: "One with children grieves because of children; one with cattle grieves because of cattle. Clinging is a man's sorrow — one with no clinging has no sorrow."

The Buddha does not deny Māra's premise — he extends it. The same children who bring joy are the children whose illness, failure, or death will bring grief. The same cattle that bring pride are the cattle whose loss will bring ruin. Every object of clinging is simultaneously an object of potential loss. Freedom from clinging is not the absence of joy but the absence of the sorrow that is joy's inseparable shadow.

总结 · Summary

陀尼耶经以对话体展开，是《经集》中结构最生动的经典之一。牧人陀尼耶六度自夸其外部安顿——食物、家庭、妻子、财产、牛群、牢固的系缚——佛陀六度以内心的自由作答。每一对回应，都揭示世俗的安全与解脱的自由之间的根本差异：前者依赖拥有，后者来自放下。经文末尾，魔王以「执取带来喜悦」挑战，佛陀以「执取带来忧苦」作答，将二者合并为一个完整的真理：执取既是喜乐的来源，也是痛苦的根源；无执取者，超越了这整个循环。

The Dhaniya Sutta is a dialogue of perfect formal symmetry: six boasts from the herdsman, six responses from the Buddha, each using the same refrain to invite the rain. Dhaniya's world is not bad — it is a genuinely good human life, well-arranged and prosperous. The Buddha does not criticise it. Instead, he mirrors each of Dhaniya's images at a deeper level: where Dhaniya has a thatched hut, the Buddha has an open shelter; where Dhaniya's fire is lit, the Buddha's is quenched; where Dhaniya's cattle are staked, the Buddha has broken every bond. The sutta's final exchange with Māra crystallises the teaching: clinging brings both joy and sorrow simultaneously. To be free from clinging is not to be free from experience but to be free from the grief that clings to every object of attachment like its shadow.
