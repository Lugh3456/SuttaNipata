第二品·第九经 · 何戒经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第二品·第八经 目录 Contents 第二品·第十经 →

【天神】持什么戒律，行什么行为，培养什么业——人可以被正确地建立，并获得最高的成就？ 【世尊】尊重长者，不嫉妒，知道听法的时机，渴望听闻善说之法——适时前往领受教导。 喜爱寂静与调御，在法中修行——以真理为喜，以善说为依——这是获得成就之道。 愉快地居于独处，善加禅修，培育对身心的观察——善友的陪伴，明辨善知识。 慈爱心性、良好行为，博学多闻，欢喜于法，精进于道——这是获得成就之道。 在遇到障碍时不失望，在顺境时不自满——以坚忍、智慧与禅定，远离懈怠、精进修行。 渴望禅那，精进专注，以念住修行，对粗语与散漫的语言感到羞耻——这是获得成就之道。 有信心、不执着、不骄慢、不依赖，不贪图财物，渴望出离——这是获得成就之道。 了解四圣谛，知道断灭之道——这是获得成就之道。 行于世间不执取，不受苦受乐所驱，保持平静——这是获得成就之道。

逐句解释

尊重长者，不嫉妒，知道听法的时机，渴望听闻善说之法——适时前往领受教导。

何戒经以问题开篇——持什么戒（kiṃsīla）、行什么行为、培养什么业，才能获得最高成就？佛陀的第一个回答从社会品格开始：尊重长者（thera-sammāna，礼敬有经验的修行者）、不嫉妒（anussūyaka）、知道何时适合听法（kāla-ññū）。这几个品质共同构成「可学习性」——一个修行者必须先具备能够接受教导的内在姿态，才能使任何外在的修行有效。

Respecting elders, not envious, knowing the time for hearing the Dhamma, desirous of well-spoken teachings — approaching at the right time to receive instruction.

The sutta answers a question about the conditions for highest attainment by beginning not with meditation but with social and relational virtues. Respecting those with more experience, freedom from envy (which closes the mind to others' progress), and the skill of knowing when to approach for instruction — these constitute the ground of learnable character. In early Buddhism, the spiritual life is not a solo project; it is embedded in relationships of teaching and reception. Envy of other practitioners' attainments is specifically named as an obstacle, because it turns others' progress into a source of suffering rather than inspiration.

喜爱寂静与调御，在法中修行——以真理为喜，以善说为依——这是获得成就之道。

第二颂转向修行的内在姿态：喜爱寂静（viveka-piya，喜爱独处与静默）与调御（saṃyama，自律）。「以真理为喜」（sacca-rata）：不是以娱乐或感官满足为喜，而是以真实、诚实为喜。「以善说为依」（subhāsita-āsaya）：以善加说出的话为内心依托——这可以指善言，也可以指对善言的内化。整颂描述的是一种向内转的品格，从外在刺激的寻求转向对真理与寂静的亲近。

Loving solitude and restraint, practising in the Dhamma — delighting in truth, resting in well-spoken words — this is the path to attainment.

Viveka — seclusion, solitude — is a recurring prerequisite for deep practice in the Pali Canon. Not withdrawal from all contact but the capacity to be in one's own company without filling the silence with distraction. Saṃyama (restraint) modifies this: solitude without inner restraint is merely isolation. 'Delighting in truth' (sacca-rata) names honesty as a source of pleasure — the practitioner who genuinely loves truth rather than merely respecting it as an obligation. 'Resting in well-spoken words' suggests that teachings that have been heard and understood become a kind of inner ground — a place to return to.

在遇到障碍时不失望，在顺境时不自满——以坚忍、智慧与禅定，远离懈怠、精进修行。

这一颂描述的是修行者在困难中的品格：面对障碍时不失望（aniggaha，不被打倒）；面对顺境时不自满（anunaya，不顺流而漂）。「坚忍、智慧与禅定」三者配合：坚忍（khanti）在面对困难时保持稳定，智慧（paññā）看清情况，禅定（jhāna）提供内在的平静基础。「远离懈怠」（ālasiyaṃ pahāya）：懈怠是修行中最常见的障碍，这里与精进（ātāpin，热诚精进）并列，相互平衡。

Not despairing when obstacles arise, not complacent in favourable conditions — with patience, wisdom and meditation, abandoning sloth, striving ardently.

Equanimity under both adverse and favourable conditions is presented as a single capacity with two expressions: not crushed by difficulty, not lulled by ease. Both are opportunities for the practitioner to fail — difficulty through discouragement, ease through complacency. The three companions — khanti (patience/endurance), paññā (wisdom), and jhāna (meditative absorption) — together form the toolkit that makes practice sustainable across varying conditions. Sloth (ālasiya) is specifically named as what must be abandoned; ardour (ātāpin) is its replacement, not aggressive striving but sustained, warm engagement with practice.

有信心、不执着、不骄慢、不依赖，不贪图财物，渴望出离——这是获得成就之道。

这一颂以六个配对的品质描述修行者的内在姿态：信心（saddhā，对三宝的信任）、不执着（anupādāna，不抓取）、不骄慢（amāna，无慢）、不依赖（anissita，无依赖）、不贪图财物（alola，不迷溺），以及渴望出离（nekkhamma-abhiratā，喜爱出离）。「不依赖」与「渴望出离」是这组品质的核心：正确的修行是从外在依赖中逐渐解脱的过程，而不是用新的依赖取代旧的依赖。

With faith, without clinging, without conceit, without dependence, without greed for gain, desirous of renunciation — this is the path to attainment.

The cluster of negative qualities to abandon — clinging, conceit, dependence, greed — map directly onto the conditions that make liberation impossible. Each of these is a form of self-referential investment: clinging to outcomes, conceit investing in self-image, dependence requiring external conditions to be a certain way, greed orienting practice toward acquisition rather than relinquishment. Against these, faith (saddhā) and the love of renunciation (nekkhamma) orient the practitioner toward the actual direction of the path — not accumulation but release.

行于世间不执取，不受苦受乐所驱，保持平静——这是获得成就之道。

最后一颂以简洁的方式描述成就的顶点：在世间行走而不执取（anupādāna，无执取）；不被苦乐两极所驱动（dukkha-sukha na niyyati，不被痛苦与快乐所牵引）；保持平静（upekkhā，舍，平静的观察）。这三个品质合在一起，描述了一种完全参与世间经验却不被它带走的状态——这正是解脱者在世间行走的方式，也是整首何戒经所有修行积累的最终果实。

Moving through the world without grasping, not driven by pain or pleasure, remaining equanimous — this is the path to attainment.

The final verse describes the destination rather than another stage of the path: moving through the world (loke caraṃ) without the constant impulse to grasp what is pleasant or resist what is unpleasant. 'Not driven by pain or pleasure' does not mean anaesthesia — it means that pain and pleasure no longer function as the steering mechanism of behaviour. Upekkhā (equanimity) is the stable ground from which experience is met: aware, responsive, but not carried away. The entire sutta moves from relational virtues to inner qualities to meditative practice to this final freedom — a progressive deepening toward unshakeable ease.

总结 · Summary

何戒经以「持什么戒律、行什么行为」的问题开篇，佛陀的回答构成了一幅完整的修行品格图景：从尊重长者、不嫉妒等社会美德，到喜爱寂静、以真理为喜等内在姿态，到在困难中不失望、在顺境中不自满的坚忍，到信心、无执、无慢、渴望出离，最终到在世间行走而不被苦乐驱动的平静。这条路径的独特之处在于：它将社会品格与冥想实践、出世的渴望与在世的平静，整合在同一个修行框架中。最高成就不在于离开世间，而在于以无执的方式完全临在于世间。

The Kiṃsīla Sutta answers a question about the conditions for highest attainment with a comprehensive portrait of the practitioner's character at every level: social virtues (respecting elders, freedom from envy), inner orientation (love of solitude, delight in truth), resilience (not despairing in difficulty, not complacent in ease), and the qualities of active practice (faith, non-clinging, non-conceit, love of renunciation). The arc culminates in equanimity in the midst of the world — moving through experience without being driven by pleasure or pain. The path described is holistic: it integrates social character, inner disposition, meditative practice, and final freedom, showing them as stages of a single continuous development rather than separate concerns.
