第一品·第十一经 · 胜利经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第一品·第十经 目录 Contents 第一品·第十二经 →

行走时或站立时，坐下时或卧倒时，身体弯曲或伸展——这是身体的动作。 身体由骨骼与筋腱连接，以皮肤覆盖，外表并不真实地显现。 身体充满了肠胃、肝脏、膀胱、心脏、肺脏，以及肾脏、脾脏、黏液、鼻涕、汗水、脂肪、血液、关节液、胆汁与脂肪。 此外，从九个开口，总是流出污秽之物：眼睛分泌物、耳垢、从鼻子流出的鼻涕、从口中吐出的痰液与胆汁。 从身体发散的还有汗水与污垢。头颅之中充满了脑浆——愚痴者受美丽幻象所迷惑。 若人如此观察身体，日以继夜，无论从哪个方面看，他都会厌离身体，没有理由对它生起骄慢之心。 如此，比丘应以此正念，于此处去除对身体的贪欲——白昼与黑夜，他应观察此身，了解此身。 无论他所经历的是什么，都应以此正念，对身体的渴欲消退，比丘将在此得到解脱，了知涅槃，如实而行。

逐句解释

行走时或站立时，坐下时或卧倒时，身体弯曲或伸展——这是身体的动作。

经文从身体的四种基本姿势开始观察：行、立、坐、卧，以及弯曲与伸展。这是观身（kāyānupassanā）修习的入手处——从最简单、最明显的身体现象开始，培养对身体的如实观察。

Whether walking or standing, sitting or lying down, bending or stretching — these are the body's movements.

The sutta begins at the most basic level: the four postures that cover all physical activity. This is the starting point of body contemplation (kāyānupassanā) as taught throughout the Pali Canon — begin with what is most obvious and direct. The four postures are not abstract; they are the immediate, undeniable reality of this body right now, in whatever posture is being held as one reads.

身体由骨骼与筋腱连接，以皮肤覆盖，外表并不真实地显现。

皮肤覆盖了真实的身体，使它看起来平滑、美丽、令人欲望——但这是一种视觉上的幻象。如果皮肤揭去，所见到的完全是另一回事。这就是「外表并不真实地显现」的含义：表面的美丽是一种遮蔽。

Joined by bones and sinews, plastered over with skin — the body does not appear as it truly is.

The skin is the primary source of the body's attractiveness — smooth, coloured, warm, pliable. But it is a covering, not the thing itself. The body as it 'truly is' — the bones, sinews, and what the following verses describe — is concealed by this surface. The teaching is not that the body is disgusting and should be hated, but that its apparent beauty is a surface phenomenon that does not disclose its actual nature.

身体充满了肠胃、肝脏、膀胱、心脏、肺脏、肾脏、脾脏、黏液、鼻涕、汗水、脂肪、血液、关节液、胆汁。

经文系统地列举了身体内部的组成成分——这些成分在单独呈现时，没有人会认为它们是美丽的或令人渴望的。然而正是这些构成了我们如此贪恋的身体。观察这一点，不是为了厌恶，而是为了如实地看见。

Inside: intestines, stomach, liver, bladder, heart, lungs, kidneys, spleen, mucus, phlegm, sweat, fat, blood, synovial fluid, bile.

The inventory of internal organs is a classic meditation object in the Theravāda tradition — the thirty-two parts of the body. None of these items, presented separately, would be considered attractive. Yet they constitute the body we dress, groom, protect, and identify with as 'myself.' The contemplation is not designed to produce revulsion but to break the spell of the body's surface presentation and see what is actually present.

从九个开口，总是流出污秽之物：眼睛分泌物、耳垢、鼻涕、痰液与胆汁；汗水与污垢；头颅中充满脑浆。

身体的九个开口（眼、耳、鼻、口、生殖器、肛门各两处或一处）持续地向外分泌各种液体。这是对身体另一个层面的如实观察：它不仅内部充满了上述内容，还不断向外流出这些东西。身体需要持续的清洁，正是因为它持续地产生这些分泌物。

From the nine openings, impurities constantly flow: eye secretions, ear wax, mucus from the nose, phlegm and bile from the mouth, sweat and dirt — and the skull is full of brain.

The nine orifices of the body are continuously producing secretions. The body is not a sealed, static object but a dynamic system of intake and output, constantly processing and excreting. The brain — often regarded as the seat of intelligence and self — is here named simply as another organ filling a cavity, composed of the same grey matter as everything else. The contemplation levels all hierarchies of the body's parts.

愚痴者受美丽幻象所迷惑；若人如此观察身体，日以继夜，他都会厌离身体，没有理由对它生起骄慢之心。

「美丽幻象」指的是对身体外表的错误认知——将表面的呈现误认为身体的本质。如实观察身体（而非仅看其表面）的结果，是厌离（nibbidā）——不是憎恨，而是不再被迷惑、不再追逐幻象的清醒。骄慢（māna）以身体为基础之一；见到身体的实质，骄慢便失去其依据。

The fool is deluded by the beautiful appearance. Seeing the body thus, day and night, one becomes disenchanted — there is no basis for conceit regarding it.

Nibbidā — disenchantment or dispassion — is not revulsion or self-hatred. It is the natural result of seeing through a spell: the body was attractive because it appeared other than it is; seeing it as it is, the spell breaks. Conceit (māna) relies on the body as a basis — 'my body is beautiful, young, strong.' When the body is seen accurately, this conceit loses its footing. The teaching liberates rather than depresses.

如此，比丘应以此正念，于此处去除对身体的贪欲；对身体的渴欲消退，比丘将在此得到解脱。

经文以实际的修行指导作结：以正念如实观察身体，目的是去除对身体的贪欲（kāmacchanda）。当贪欲消退，解脱自然出现——不是通过强制压制欲望，而是通过如实看见欲望所依附的对象，令欲望失去其根基。

Thus a monk with mindfulness should remove desire for the body here. With craving for the body abandoned, the monk is freed here, knowing Nibbāna.

The sutta closes with the practical goal: liberation through the removal of bodily craving. The method is mindfulness — not force, not disgust, but clear seeing. When desire is deprived of its object (the illusion of the body's beauty and permanence), it naturally subsides. What remains is not a cold, bodyless existence but liberation — Nibbāna — while still inhabiting this body, understood now as it actually is.

总结 · Summary

胜利经是《经集》中最具身体观察色彩的经典，系统地引导修行者从外到内地如实看见这个身体：从四种姿势，到皮肤的遮蔽，到内脏的填充，到九个开口的持续分泌，最后到颅中的脑浆。这不是一部仇恨身体的经典，而是一部「去幻」的经典：身体的迷人之处，来自它表面的呈现；如实观察它的内容，魔法自然消散。厌离（nibbidā）由此生起——不是憎恶，而是清醒；不是逃避，而是解脱。对身体的贪欲一旦消退，比丘便在此当下得到解脱，如实了知涅槃。

The Vijaya Sutta is the Sutta Nipata's most sustained body-contemplation text, working systematically from the body's postures through its skin, its organs, its secretions, and its cranial contents. The purpose is not self-hatred — the text does not say 'the body is disgusting and should be despised.' It says: 'see it as it actually is, not as it appears.' The spell of bodily beauty depends on the skin's concealment of what lies beneath; once the concealment is seen through, the compulsive craving that drives so much of human behaviour loses its grip. Disenchantment (nibbidā) arises — a clear-eyed freedom from the delusion of the body as a reliable source of lasting satisfaction. In that disenchantment, liberation is found.
