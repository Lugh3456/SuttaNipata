第四品·第十六经 · 舍利弗经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第四品·第十五经 目录 Contents 第五品·序偈 →

以下经文据汉译《义足经》（T0198，支谦译，三国·吴）

【舍利弗】 如此之人——明智善思惟—— 初出家离俗，往佛处求道—— 我今请问佛，彼应如何行？ 【世尊答】 不应以恶语，不应以恶意—— 不应言语乱，应节制饮食—— 知足少欲者，此人得安乐。 若人于世间，无事不卷入—— 于诸有为中，见过已离之—— 此名为出家，勿追求欲乐。 彼渴爱已枯，无依无所著—— 不为痴所盖，不为慢所动—— 彼无嗔无恚，无傲慢无曲—— 此名为比丘，无污染清净。 莫人轻蔑他，于何处莫骗—— 以嗔恚憎恨，不欲他苦恼。 比丘于世间，任何处无取—— 渡越诸苦难，不复苦恼缠。 诸佛所赞叹，此修行者行—— 无取无著者，此名真解脱—— 大师如是说，舍利弗听之。

逐句解释

不应以恶语，不应以恶意——不应言语乱，应节制饮食——知足少欲者，此人得安乐。

舍利弗经（Sāriputta Sutta）是八偈品的最后一经，以舍利弗代表「即将出家者」提问，佛陀给出新出家弟子的基础教导。第一颂覆盖三个领域：语（不恶语、不乱语）、意（不恶意）、身（节制饮食、知足少欲）——这是戒（sīla）的基础，也是修行起步的三个支柱。「知足少欲者，此人得安乐」：出离的起点不是严苛的苦行，而是对基本需求的知足——这种简朴生活本身就带来安乐（sukha），为更深的修行提供基础。

One should not speak harshly, should not act with malice — should not speak in confusion, should moderate eating — the one who is content with little has happiness.

The Sāriputta Sutta closes the Aṭṭhakavagga with a teaching addressed to the newly going-forth — basic foundations before the philosophical heights. The three domains of the first verse (speech, intention, food) correspond to the foundational triad of ethical discipline: right speech, right intention, and the material simplicity that reduces the occasions for craving. 'Content with little has happiness' — the positive framing is important: simplicity is not deprivation but a condition that generates wellbeing, providing the stable platform for deeper practice.

若人于世间，无事不卷入——于诸有为中，见过已离之——此名为出家，勿追求欲乐。彼渴爱已枯，无依无所著——不为痴所盖，不为慢所动。

「无事不卷入」（anāvilo，不搅动）与「于诸有为中，见过已离之」：出家的深义不只是剃度离俗，而是「不卷入世间的搅动」，并且对一切有为法（saṅkhāra，条件构成的事物）见到其过失（ādīnava，过患）而自然离开。「彼渴爱已枯」（taṇhā kīṇa，渴爱消尽）——这不是出家初期的状态，而是修行成熟的结果：渴爱不再给存在「灌水」，无依无著、不为痴慢所动，这是八偈品描述的解脱境界在「出家教导」语境中的重现。

If a person in the world is not drawn into agitation — seeing the danger in all conditioned things and leaving them — this is called going forth; do not pursue sensual pleasures. With craving exhausted, with no resting-place and nothing clung to — not covered by delusion, not shaken by conceit.

The deeper meaning of 'going forth' (pabbajjā) is articulated here: not the outer act of shaving and robing but the inner quality of not being drawn into the world's agitating currents, and of seeing the danger (ādīnava) in conditioned things. 'Craving exhausted' is the mature fruit of practice — the Aṭṭhakavagga's characteristic description of complete liberation reappearing in the frame of advice to the newly ordained. The sutta thus compresses the entire arc from beginning practice (right speech, moderation) to full liberation (craving exhausted, no resting-place).

诸佛所赞叹，此修行者行——无取无著者，此名真解脱——大师如是说，舍利弗听之。

八偈品以「无取无著者，此名真解脱」作最终的收束——这与八偈品每一首经的结颂主题完全一致，也是整个Aṭṭhakavagga的核心命题的最后一次宣说：解脱的本质是无取无著（anupādāparinibbāna，无执取的般涅槃）。「诸佛所赞叹，此修行者行」：这条道路不是一时一人的教导，而是所有觉悟者所共同称赞与行走的道路。「大师如是说，舍利弗听之」——以师弟的名字作结，有一种亲密的人间感：这是对具体一个人的具体教导，在整个八偈品高密度哲学分析的最后，以最朴素的方式作结。

What all Buddhas praise, what the practitioner walks — the one with no grasping and nothing clung to: this is called true liberation. The great teacher spoke thus; Sāriputta listened.

The Aṭṭhakavagga closes with its central formula one final time: 'no grasping, nothing clung to — this is true liberation.' The naming of all Buddhas as endorsing this path gives the teaching a trans-historical weight: this is not one teacher's innovation but the perennial discovery of all who awaken. The closing 'Sāriputta listened' is humanly touching — after sixteen suttas of dense philosophical analysis, the section ends with a named person in a specific moment of reception. The teaching has been heard; now it must be walked.

总结 · Summary

舍利弗经是八偈品（Aṭṭhakavagga）的终章，以舍利弗为新出家弟子代问，佛陀给出从基础（不恶语、节食、知足）到成熟（渴爱已枯、无依无著）的完整修行弧线。「无取无著者，此名真解脱」——这是整个八偈品十六首经的终结句，也是其核心命题最后一次宣说。「诸佛所赞叹，此修行者行」：不是一时一人的教导，而是所有觉悟者共同走过的道路。八偈品至此完整结束：从欲经（欲望的分析）到舍利弗经（新出家者的基础教导），以及贯穿始终的核心主题——无取无著，不立见，不争论，彻底的非执取，就是解脱。

The Sāriputta Sutta closes the Aṭṭhakavagga with an address to the newly going-forth — framing the section's dense philosophical analysis as a complete arc of practice from its foundation (right speech, moderation, contentment) to its fruit (craving exhausted, no resting-place, nothing clung to). 'No grasping, nothing clung to — this is true liberation' is the Aṭṭhakavagga's final statement of its central claim, spoken for the last time. 'All Buddhas praise this' gives the teaching its widest frame: the path of non-clinging is not an innovation but the perennial discovery. The section ends with a person receiving it: Sāriputta listened. Now it must be walked.
