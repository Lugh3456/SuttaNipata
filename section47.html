第四品·第九经 · 摩犍提经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第四品·第八经 目录 Contents 第四品·第十经 →

以下经文据汉译《义足经》（T0198，支谦译，三国·吴）

【摩犍提】 见此端正女，以女奉牟尼—— 若不受此女，以何法得定？ 【世尊】 见彼女形色，欲心不起者—— 彼见爱欲故，以慧截断之。 我见渴爱魔，贪欲爱恚痴—— 我不欲与汝，战汝身力微—— 宁死战场上，不欲受彼女。 【摩犍提】 若人如是说，七仙各有道—— 以戒以苦行，以持以学法—— 彼此或得道？于我汝何说？ 【世尊】 若执于己见，谓此是最上—— 世间中无二，余者皆卑劣—— 如此说者，彼见为争端。 若人无所住，不立此彼岸—— 诸有为皆见，不取不著者—— 我说彼清净，不依他清净。 依见依戒禁，依闻或依行—— 依彼诸清净，未脱于依处。 若人脱依处，于世间无取—— 不著于见者，此名为清净。

逐句解释

【摩犍提将女儿奉给佛陀】见此端正女，以女奉牟尼——若不受此女，以何法得定？【世尊】见彼女形色，欲心不起者——彼见爱欲故，以慧截断之。

摩犍提经（Māgandiya Sutta）以一个著名的戏剧性场景开场：婆罗门摩犍提（Māgandiya）将自己美貌的女儿献给佛陀（这是古印度最高礼遇的形式之一），佛陀拒绝。摩犍提问：若不接受此女，你以何法「得定」（得到满足/安定）？佛陀的回答揭示了欲望的认知结构：「见彼女形色，欲心不起者」——不是说女性不美，而是欲心不被触发——「彼见爱欲故，以慧截断之」：以智慧直接看见欲望的本质（渴爱、贪、瞋、痴），而将其截断。这个场景有别于简单的禁欲教导：佛陀不否认美的存在，而是描述了一种不被美触发欲望的认知方式。

Māgandiya offers his beautiful daughter: 'Seeing this fine woman, I offer her to the sage — if you do not accept her, by what teaching do you find contentment?' The Blessed One: 'One who sees that form without desire arising — having seen desire itself, having cut it through with wisdom.'

The Māgandiya Sutta's opening confrontation — a brahmin offering his beautiful daughter, the Buddha declining — is one of the Pali Canon's most dramatic scenes. The Buddha's answer is cognitive rather than ascetic: it does not deny that the woman is beautiful but describes a quality of perception in which beauty does not trigger craving. 'Having cut through desire with wisdom' is the liberating move: not suppression but direct seeing of desire's nature — its arising, its dependent character, its emptiness of anything that warrants the clinging it generates.

我见渴爱魔，贪欲爱恚痴——我不欲与汝，战汝身力微——宁死战场上，不欲受彼女。

佛陀以第一人称宣告：「我见渴爱魔」——在遇到摩犍提女儿时，他「看见」的是渴爱（taṇhā）、贪（rāga）、瞋（dosa）、痴（moha）的魔力（māra，障碍），而非一个令人渴望的对象。「战汝身力微」：这些魔力（欲望的化身）是软弱的——对一个已看穿欲望本质的人而言。「宁死战场上，不欲受彼女」：这是以斗争语言呈现的意志声明，但其底层不是苦行的压制，而是基于洞见的不动摇：欲望的本质既已洞见，就没有什么值得用「接受」来换取的。

I see the demon of craving — desire, passion, aversion, delusion — I do not wish to contest with you; your power is weak. Better to die on the battlefield than to accept that woman.

The martial imagery — seeing desire as a demon, refusing to engage its 'weak' power, preferring death to acceptance — communicates the absolute quality of the Buddha's refusal while grounding it in perception rather than will: the demon's power is 'weak' not because the Buddha is suppressing it by force but because it has been seen through. Desire that is clearly seen as the arising of craving, aversion, and delusion loses its capacity to compel. The 'prefer death' formula is a classical declaration of uncompromising commitment, not advocacy for martyrdom.

若人无所住，不立此彼岸——诸有为皆见，不取不著者——我说彼清净，不依他清净。依见依戒禁，依闻或依行——依彼诸清净，未脱于依处。若人脱依处，于世间无取——不著于见者，此名为清净。

经文后半段从具体情节转向八偈品的核心哲学：「若人无所住，不立此彼岸」——真正清净的人没有任何「依处」（āssaya，依靠、寄托、基础）。「依见依戒禁，依闻或依行——依彼诸清净，未脱于依处」：凡是「通过X来达到清净」的框架（X可以是见解、戒律、修学、行法），都是一种「依处」——一个被执取的支柱。真正的清净是「脱依处」——从所有依处中解脱出来，包括从「这个方法能给我清净」的执取中解脱。「不著于见者，此名为清净」：与清净八偈的结颂呼应，再次将真清净定义为无取无著。

One who has no resting-place, who does not set up this shore or the far shore — seeing all conditioned things, grasping and clinging to nothing: I call this one pure — purity does not depend on another. If one depends on views, precepts, hearing, or practices for purity — one has not escaped from dependency. One who is free from dependency, who takes nothing in the world — not clinging to views: this is called purity.

The philosophical conclusion of the Māgandiya Sutta links directly to the Suddhaṭṭhaka's teaching on purity: genuine purity cannot be achieved through any method that one holds as a dependency (āssaya — a resting-place, something one leans on). Every 'through X I become pure' formulation is itself a form of clinging — a position one inhabits. Freedom from all such resting-places, including the practice one follows — not abandoning practice but releasing the identity-claim 'through this I am purified' — is the Aṭṭhakavagga's account of genuine purity.

总结 · Summary

摩犍提经以一个戏剧性场景开场：婆罗门将美貌女儿献给佛陀，佛陀拒绝，并宣告见到的不是美女而是渴爱、贪、瞋、痴的魔力。这个拒绝不是苦行压制，而是基于对欲望本质的直接洞见。经文随后转入八偈品的哲学核心：真清净不依赖任何「依处」——不依见、不依戒、不依闻、不依行。凡是「通过X达到清净」的框架都是一种执取的依处，尚未真正从依处中解脱。「脱依处，于世间无取，不著于见者，此名为清净」——这与清净八偈的结论形成回响，将真清净定义为彻底的无依无著。

The Māgandiya Sutta opens with one of the canon's most striking confrontations — a beautiful woman offered, declined — and grounds the refusal not in ascetic will but in direct cognitive seeing: the form triggers awareness of craving rather than craving itself. The philosophical conclusion extends the Suddhaṭṭhaka's teaching: no method held as a 'dependency' (something one leans on for purity) can deliver genuine purity, because the dependency itself is clinging. Freedom from all such resting-places — not abandoning practice but releasing the identity-claim attached to it — is the Aṭṭhakavagga's most complete description of what purity actually consists of.
