第三品·第十经 · 瞿迦梨经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第三品·第九经 目录 Contents 第三品·第十一经 →

比丘瞿迦梨（Kokāliya）来到世尊处，礼拜后坐在一旁，对世尊说： 「世尊，舍利弗与目犍连的欲望是邪恶的，他们被恶欲所控制。」 世尊说：「瞿迦梨，不要这样说！对舍利弗与目犍连保持信心——他们是善行之人。」 第二次与第三次，瞿迦梨仍坚持这样说。世尊再次以同样的话回应。 瞿迦梨随即离开。不久，全身长满了芥子大的疮疤，然后大如豆子，最后大如石头——他死后堕入莲花地狱（Paduma naraka）。 【梵天罗迦（Sahampati）下降至世尊处，告知此事，并说了以下的偈颂。】 在人死去以后——就像真实所是的那样——那个毁谤圣者的人，将在地狱中受苦。 说谎的人堕入地狱，做了坏事而说「我没做」的人也如此。两者在来世都是同等的——在死后都是低劣的人。 人出生时，口中有一把斧头——愚者用它砍伐自己，说出了不适当的话语。 赞美不值得赞美的人，或谴责值得赞美的人——选择了不幸——他不会在那不幸中得到幸福。 在赌博中失去财富是小不幸；所有一切都包括在内——伴随自心去往恶趣，对那些抱有恶意对善人的人，这是更大的不幸。 一百年加上三十六五年——那是说话中伤圣者者在地狱中经历的年数。 【以下是各地狱名称与年数的枚举，从阿浮陀到莲花地狱的时间单位计算，层层递增，最后描述瞿迦梨所堕入的莲花地狱（Paduma）之苦。】

逐句解释

比丘瞿迦梨来到世尊处，说：「世尊，舍利弗与目犍连的欲望是邪恶的，他们被恶欲所控制。」世尊说：「瞿迦梨，不要这样说！……他们是善行之人。」第二次与第三次，瞿迦梨仍坚持。

瞿迦梨经是经集中少数以具体历史情境与道德警示为主要内容的经典之一。瞿迦梨（Kokāliya）是提婆达多的追随者，对舍利弗与目犍连——佛陀的两位首席弟子——抱有嫉妒与恶意。他来到佛陀处，三次坚持指控两位阿罗汉长老「欲望邪恶」，三次被佛陀拒绝（「对他们保持信心——他们是善行之人」），仍不罢休。这三次坚持是重要的：第一次可能是误解，第三次则是固执的恶意——他在佛陀的当面纠正下仍坚持虚假的指控。

Kokāliya said: 'The desires of Sāriputta and Moggallāna are evil — they are overcome by evil desires.' The Blessed One: 'Do not say this, Kokāliya — have faith in them, they are good in conduct.' Three times, Kokāliya persisted.

Kokāliya was a follower of Devadatta, the Buddha's cousin who attempted a schism in the Saṅgha. His accusation targets the two greatest disciples — Sāriputta (chief in wisdom) and Moggallāna (chief in supernormal powers) — with the most damaging possible charge for a monk: corrupted desire. The three-fold structure is legally and morally significant: one repetition after correction might be error; three repetitions after three corrections constitutes deliberate false witness. The Buddha's triple response demonstrates both his care for Kokāliya (offering correction three times) and the inexorability of the consequence once the false witness is confirmed as deliberate.

人出生时，口中有一把斧头——愚者用它砍伐自己，说出了不适当的话语。

这一颂是整部经集中最著名的语言比喻之一：人出生时，口中已有一把「斧头」——言语的力量。「愚者用它砍伐自己」：不善的语言——尤其是对善人的诽谤与虚假指控——不是伤害对方（善人如舍利弗与目犍连，实际上是无法被诽谤所伤的），而是伤害说话者自己。这个比喻的深刻之处在于它指出了恶语的主要受害者：不是被诽谤的人，而是说出诽谤之语的人，因为这些话语积累为导向苦的业（kamma）。

When a person is born, an axe is born in their mouth — the fool cuts themselves down with it, speaking words that are not fitting.

The 'axe in the mouth' is one of the Pali Canon's most striking images for the power of speech. The fool who speaks unworthy words does not primarily harm those spoken of — arahants like Sāriputta and Moggallāna are impervious to false accusation — but harms themselves. The axe metaphor positions harmful speech as self-destruction: the words are the instrument and the speaker is the one who is cut. This teaching on the karmic boomerang of speech is especially pointed in the context of deliberate false witness against accomplished practitioners: the very strength of the target's virtue is what makes the attack so damaging to the attacker.

赞美不值得赞美的人，或谴责值得赞美的人——选择了不幸——他不会在那不幸中得到幸福。在赌博中失去财富是小不幸；伴随自心去往恶趣，对那些抱有恶意对善人的人，这是更大的不幸。

经文以两种「不幸」作对比：赌博失去财富是小的不幸——是世俗意义上的损失；「伴随自心去往恶趣」——对善人怀抱恶意，以此业力堕入恶道——是更大的不幸，因为它是跨越多生的长期损失。这个对比的要点是：世俗的失败（财富的丧失）是可以弥补的，道德的堕落（对善人的系统性恶意）则带来长期的精神损失，远比财物损失严重。「伴随自心」（attānuvādā，自己的心）：业的审判不是外部强加，而是自心的延伸。

Praising one unworthy of praise, or censuring one who is praiseworthy — choosing misfortune — one does not gain happiness in that misfortune. The misfortune of losing wealth at gambling is small; the greater misfortune, for those who harbour ill-will toward the good, is going with one's mind to an evil destination.

The two misfortunes establish a scale of harm: gambling away material wealth is a finite, recoverable loss — the body of losses that constitute worldly misfortune. 'Going with one's own mind toward an evil destination' is the karmic consequence of sustained ill-will toward accomplished practitioners — a loss that spans multiple lifetimes. 'With one's own mind' (saha attānā) is the key: the karmic consequence is not an external punishment but the direction one's own character takes through the accumulation of deliberate harmful intention. The 'misfortune' of hostility toward the good is the mind that is shaped by it.

一百年加上三十六五年——那是说话中伤圣者者在地狱中经历的年数。

经文随后以详细的地狱时间计算描述瞿迦梨所堕入的果报，从阿浮陀（Abbuda）到莲花地狱（Paduma），层层递增，时间单位极度巨大（一颗芥子大小的种子，四万年才能倒出一粒）。这种时间计算的极度夸大，在巴利文中有时具有特别的修辞效果：它不是字面意义上的精确数字，而是表达「后果的极度严重性」——对正在修行的圣者怀抱恶意并公开诽谤，是修行世界中最严重的罪行之一，其后果在时间长度上是人类无法想象的。

One hundred years plus thirty-six five — that is the number of years in hell experienced by the one who speaks ill of a sage.

The elaborate time calculations for the various hells (Abbuda, Nirabbuda, etc., each measured by impossibly large seed-counting operations) serve a rhetorical purpose beyond literal precision: they communicate the incalculable severity of the consequence. In a culture without long written number systems, these compounding astronomical calculations were the standard way of expressing 'beyond human reckoning.' The teaching is that deliberate, repeated, false witness against an arahant — especially in the face of correction — generates consequences whose duration dwarfs ordinary conceptions of time. The purpose is not to terrify but to proportionately weight the moral gravity of attacking the spiritually accomplished.

总结 · Summary

瞿迦梨经是经集中风格最独特的经典之一——以具体历史事件（比丘瞿迦梨三次向佛陀诽谤舍利弗与目犍连）为框架，通过其惨烈的果报（疮遍全身、堕入莲花地狱）来阐明一个根本原则：对圣者的恶意诽谤是最严重的精神危害之一，其后果远超世间任何世俗损失。经文中最著名的意象是「口中的斧头」：言语是一种力量，愚者用它砍伐自己。赞美不值得赞美的，谴责值得赞美的——这种颠倒的语言，带领自心走向恶趣。这首经是对语言道德（正语）与尊重圣者的最强烈警示，也是经集中少数以反面教材为主要内容的经典。

The Kokāliya Sutta is the Sutta Nipata's most cautionary text — a concrete historical episode (a monk who three times falsely accuses the Buddha's two greatest disciples despite correction) used to illustrate a fundamental moral principle: deliberate false witness against accomplished practitioners is among the most severe spiritual harms, with consequences that dwarf any worldly misfortune. The 'axe in the mouth' image is the sutta's central teaching on speech: language is a power that the foolish turn against themselves. Praising the unworthy or censuring the praiseworthy directs one's own mind toward harm — the karmic consequence is not external punishment but the direction of one's own accumulated intention. The sutta is the Sutta Nipata's strongest statement on right speech and reverence for those who have genuinely attained.
