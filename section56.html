第五品·第一经 · 阿耆多经

经集 · Sutta Nipata · 彼岸道品

← 第五品·序偈 目录 Contents 第五品·第二经 →

【阿耆多问】 世界被什么遮蔽了？世界为何不能照耀？ 什么污染了它？什么是世界的大恐惧？ 【世尊答】 世界被无明遮蔽，因贪欲与懈怠而不能照耀—— 渴爱污染了它，苦是世界的大恐惧。 【阿耆多问】 四方奔流，什么能阻止它们？ 洪流的舍离是什么？什么能封堵这些洪流？ 【世尊答】 四方奔流，正念可以阻止它们—— 洪流的舍离，我说是智慧； 靠正念与智慧，这些洪流可以被封堵。 【阿耆多问】 智慧与正念，名色——告诉我这是在哪里止息的？ 【世尊答】 你所问的这个问题，我来解答： 识的止息处，就是名色在那里止息的地方—— 识止息，名色亦止息。 【阿耆多问】 那些已经了知的人，和那些还在修学的人—— 告诉我他们的行为方式，智者！ 【世尊答】 比丘不应贪著于欲乐，内心不散乱—— 善巧于一切法，应正念而行。

逐句解释

世界被无明遮蔽，因贪欲与懈怠而不能照耀——渴爱污染了它，苦是世界的大恐惧。

阿耆多经（Ajita Sutta）是彼岸道品的第一问，也是十六经中最宏观的开场——「世界被什么遮蔽？」这个问题触及整个佛法的元问题。答案的四层结构：无明（遮蔽）→贪欲与懈怠（不照耀）→渴爱（污染）→苦（大恐惧）。这个四层分析将「世间苦」的结构完整呈现：遮蔽（无明）是认知层面，贪欲（贪）与懈怠（pamāda）是心理层面，渴爱（taṇhā）是深层驱动，苦（dukkha）是现象层面。彼岸道品以这个宏观问题开场，奠定整品「如何渡越苦海」的基调。

The world is covered by ignorance; because of greed and negligence it does not shine — craving pollutes it; suffering is its great terror.

The opening exchange of the Pārāyanavagga sets the widest possible frame: what covers the world? The answer maps the entire liberating analysis onto the question — ignorance (the cognitive covering), greed and negligence (the psychological conditions that prevent clear seeing), craving (the deep driving force of conditioned existence), and suffering (the experienced reality that these conditions produce). The metaphor of the world 'not shining' is unusual and evocative: the natural luminosity of mind (traditionally compared to a lamp) is obscured, not destroyed, by these coverings — implying that the task is uncovering rather than creating.

四方奔流，正念可以阻止它们——洪流的舍离，我说是智慧；靠正念与智慧，这些洪流可以被封堵。识的止息处，就是名色在那里止息的地方。

「四方奔流」（cattāro oghā，四洪流）是彼岸道品最重要的意象之一：欲流（kāmogha）、有流（bhavogha）、见流（diṭṭhogha）、无明流（avijjogha）——这四股洪流将众生冲刷在轮回中。「正念可以阻止，智慧可以封堵」：正念（sati）与智慧（paññā）是抵御这四股洪流的两大力量，相辅相成。「识的止息处，名色亦止息」——这个回答点明了名色（nāmarūpa，心与物质）止息的根本：识（viññāṇa）的止息，而这正是缘起链中识（识→名色）这一环的逆向——切断识的流续，名色就无从延续。

The four floods — mindfulness can restrain them. Wisdom is the renouncing of floods; through mindfulness and wisdom these floods can be blocked. Where consciousness ceases is where name-and-form ceases.

The four floods (oghā) — of sensuality, becoming, views, and ignorance — are the Pārāyanavagga's central metaphor for the forces sweeping beings through conditioned existence. Mindfulness arrests the momentum; wisdom cuts the current at its source. The question about where name-and-form ceases receives an answer from the dependent origination chain: consciousness (viññāṇa) is the condition for name-and-form — where consciousness ceases (in the cessation of craving and clinging, ultimately in Nibbāna), name-and-form ceases with it. The question and answer together trace a path from observable suffering back to its conditioned root.

总结 · Summary

阿耆多经以「世界被什么遮蔽？」开场，是彼岸道品最宏观的问道之一。答案呈现为四层结构（无明→贪欲懈怠→渴爱→苦），将整个佛法的苦与苦因分析压缩为四行偈颂。四洪流（欲、有、见、无明）需要正念来阻止、智慧来封堵——这两者是整个修行的核心双翼。最后的问题（名色在哪里止息？）与缘起论呼应：识止息，名色亦止息——指向苦的彻底消灭。阿耆多经是彼岸道品的哲学序章，将后续十五经的个别问道统摄在一个最大的框架下。

The Ajita Sutta opens the Pārāyanavagga with the widest possible philosophical frame: what covers the world, what are the floods sweeping beings, how are they crossed? The four-layer analysis (ignorance → greed and negligence → craving → suffering) compresses the entire liberating diagnosis into four verse lines. The four floods — of sensuality, becoming, views, and ignorance — require mindfulness to restrain and wisdom to cut. The closing exchange on where name-and-form ceases connects the analysis to the dependent origination chain: consciousness's cessation is name-and-form's cessation. The Ajita Sutta serves as the Pārāyanavagga's philosophical overture, establishing the frame within which the fifteen subsequent dialogues each explore a particular facet.
