第四品·第十三经 · 大阵列经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第四品·第十二经 目录 Contents 第四品·第十四经 →

以下经文据汉译《义足经》（T0198，支谦译，三国·吴）

各执自见而诤者——诸见皆如实否？ 若皆如实，各皆真实——彼者何者劣？ 若各各以此见为真，余者以彼见为真—— 彼此各说己为真，彼此论何由定？ 若不依他而自知，若自见不依他者—— 诸见皆成正，皆成为清净—— 彼等无有一愚者。 非我所说为清净，亦非我说他见胜—— 各持己见甚坚固，由此生诤论。 牟尼无诤不说见，此是牟尼之所行—— 见闻觉知之所取，牟尼不以此为胜。 牟尼不以见净，不以闻净—— 于世间已无所取，不著此彼岸—— 此名为真牟尼。 执见而相诤者，言语种种异—— 若无有执见者，则彼等无诤论。 是故善了知，此为见之患—— 以见而诤故，见为诤之因—— 脱此见之患，于世无所著。

逐句解释

各执自见而诤者——诸见皆如实否？若皆如实，各皆真实——彼者何者劣？非我所说为清净，亦非我说他见胜——各持己见甚坚固，由此生诤论。

大阵列经（Mahāviyūha Sutta）延续小阵列经的分析，进一步追问：如果各方的见解「皆如实」（都是真实的），那就没有「劣见」，争论就无从解决；如果只有一方真实，那需要一个独立标准来判断——但这个标准本身又将成为争论的对象。「非我所说为清净，亦非我说他见胜」——这是一个关键的自我说明：佛陀或圣者不宣称「我的说法给你清净，我的说法比你的更高」。这与最上八偈中「贤圣不立最上见」的精神完全一致。

Those who contend while each holding their view — are all views really as they truly are? If they all truly are, each one being true — then which of them is inferior? 'It is not that what I say gives you purity, nor do I say my view is superior' — each holds their view very firmly; from this arise contentions.

The Mahāviyūha continues the Cūḷaviyūha's analysis. If all views are 'truly as they are,' none is inferior and dispute is unresolvable. If only some are true, an adjudicating criterion is needed — which itself becomes another contested view. The critical self-declaration — 'not that my saying gives you purity, not that my view is superior' — is the Aṭṭhakavagga's most explicit claim about its own teaching: it does not present itself as the new 'highest view' that replaces all previous highest views. This is consistent with the Paramaṭṭhaka's demonstration that any 'highest view' is self-contradicting.

牟尼无诤不说见，此是牟尼之所行——见闻觉知之所取，牟尼不以此为胜。牟尼不以见净，不以闻净——于世间已无所取，不著此彼岸——此名为真牟尼。

「牟尼无诤不说见，此是牟尼之所行」——牟尼的特征在大阵列经中得到最完整的正面描述：不诤（无争论）、不说见（不以「这是我的见解」的方式发言）。「见闻觉知之所取，牟尼不以此为胜」：通过眼、耳、感觉、思维所感知到的任何事物，牟尼都不将其作为「我的胜处」——不以任何感知作为自我优越感的依据。「不著此彼岸」：与洞穴八偈和恶意八偈的结颂呼应，牟尼不以涅槃作为新的依处。「此名为真牟尼」：这四个否定（无诤、不说见、不以感知为胜、不著此彼岸）共同构成真牟尼的完整图像。

The sage has no contention and does not proclaim views — this is the sage's way of going. Whatever is taken through seeing, hearing, perceiving, and knowing — the sage does not claim superiority through any of it. The sage is not purified through views, not through hearing — having nothing taken in the world, not clinging to this shore or the far shore — this is called the true sage.

The Mahāviyūha's portrait of the true sage is the Aṭṭhakavagga's most complete positive description — given entirely through negations. No contention, no proclaiming of views, no claiming superiority through any perceived or known object, no view-based purity, no taking in the world, no clinging to either shore. This four-fold negation is not a description of cognitive blankness but of a quality of engagement that leaves no residue of identity-claim: the sage knows, perceives, hears — but holds none of it as 'mine, superior, the ground of my purity.' This is the sage the entire Aṭṭhakavagga has been pointing toward.

执见而相诤者，言语种种异——若无有执见者，则彼等无诤论。是故善了知，此为见之患——以见而诤故，见为诤之因——脱此见之患，于世无所著。

大阵列经以一个精炼的总结作结：「执见而相诤者……若无有执见者，则彼等无诤论」——诤论的充分必要条件是执见（view-grasping），而非见解本身。「是故善了知，此为见之患」：「见之患」（diṭṭhiparāmāsa，见的危害/见的执取）是必须被了知的——不是「见解是坏的」，而是「以见解执取为自我」是有害的。「脱此见之患，于世无所著」：从见的执取中解脱，就是从世间的一切执著中解脱——因为见的执取是所有社会性争论与精神性系缚的共同根源。

Those who contend while clinging to views speak in many different ways — if there were no view-clinging, there would be no contentions among them. Therefore know well: this is the danger of views — because of views one contends, views are the cause of contention — freed from the danger of views, one takes nothing in the world.

The closing verses give the Aṭṭhakavagga's final formulation of its central diagnosis: contention requires view-clinging as a necessary and sufficient condition. Without view-clinging, the mechanism of contention cannot engage. 'The danger of views' (diṭṭhiparāmāsa — the harm of grasping views as 'mine, true, the ground of purity') is the specific pathology the Aṭṭhakavagga has been treating across all sixteen suttas. Freedom from this danger is freedom from taking in the world — the two are equivalent because what one takes in the world is always, at root, a view held as identity.

总结 · Summary

大阵列经是八偈品的哲学总结之一，与小阵列经合称，给出了对见争全景的最完整分析。经文提出大阵列经独有的自我说明：「非我所说为清净，亦非我说他见胜」——佛法的教导本身不以「最高见解」的姿态出现。真牟尼的图像在此最完整呈现：无诤、不说见、不以感知为胜、不以见净、不著此彼岸——全部是否定，全部指向同一个彻底无依无著的状态。经文以「见之患」（见的执取作为诤论的根源）作结：脱此见之患，于世无所著——见的执取与世间执著是同一件事，解脱它们也是同一步。

The Mahāviyūha completes the Aṭṭhakavagga's treatment of contention and view-clinging, providing the section's most complete positive portrait of the sage — given entirely through negations. The self-declaration that the Buddha's teaching does not present itself as a 'highest view' displacing all others is the Aṭṭhakavagga's most explicit meta-comment on its own teaching. The 'danger of views' (diṭṭhiparāmāsa) is identified as the root of all contention: view-clinging is the necessary and sufficient condition for the adversarial dynamic. Freedom from view-clinging and freedom from worldly taking are identical. The sage who holds nothing is not cognitively empty but structurally outside the contest — a different mode of being in the world.
