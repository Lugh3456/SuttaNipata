第二品·第三经 · 羞耻经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第二品·第二经 目录 Contents 第二品·第四经 →

无羞耻心，厌恶同伴，说「我是你的朋友」——但不参与可做之事，应知他不是我的伙伴。 甜言蜜语、对朋友友好——但不执行者，智者了知他只是空谈，不是行动的人。 常常寻找裂缝的人，不是伙伴；在这里，他只是在探找错误。他坐在众人中，但他的坐并不是伙伴。 一个拥有孩子的人，拥有妻子，拥有财产，依赖他人的财产——这不是朋友的特征，拥有这些并不能让他成为伙伴。 【具有以下特征的人才是真正的朋友：】 为朋友着想，渴望朋友的善——他们两者之间有联系，他不是寻求分裂，而是寻求亲近。 如同母亲对待她唯一的儿子，给予保护——对朋友也应如此，依照这个原则，了解朋友的需要，他是真正的朋友。 具有羞耻之心的人，带着品格高尚的人的共同点——他来带来了欢乐，驱散了黑暗，带来了光明——这样的人，可以被称为朋友。 在这个世界上，背负重担者获得了休息，就如旅人获得荫凉处——智慧的人得到这样的良友，为朋友施好处，因为良师是难得的。 谁能胜任这样的善知识，就获得了幸运中的幸运——在这个世界上，称赞者与被称赞者因此而欢喜结合，两者都得到喜悦。

逐句解释

无羞耻心，厌恶同伴，说「我是你的朋友」——但不参与可做之事，应知他不是我的伙伴。

「羞耻」（hirī）是巴利语中的重要道德概念，指对不善行为的内心羞耻感与自我尊重——有别于「惭愧」（ottappa，对他人谴责的畏惧）。缺乏hirī的人对自己的行为没有内在的道德约束，只会口头宣称友情，却不真正参与朋友的事务。「可做之事」指一个真朋友应当为对方做的种种事情；言而无行，是友谊的第一个检验。

One without moral shame, who dislikes companions, who says 'I am your friend' but does not engage in what can be done — know that he is not a companion of mine.

Hirī — moral shame or conscience — is one of the foundational virtues in early Buddhist ethics. It refers to internal moral sensitivity: the discomfort one feels when contemplating an unworthy act, based on self-respect rather than fear of others' censure (which is ottappa). A person without hirī has no internal brake on their conduct; their claims of friendship are empty because they are not backed by the disposition to act. The sutta opens by identifying this gap between profession and practice as the primary marker of false friendship.

甜言蜜语、对朋友友好——但不执行者，智者了知他只是空谈，不是行动的人。

第二个虚假友谊的标志：言辞上温暖友好，但当需要实际行动时，便无所作为。「智者了知」指具有辨别力的人能够透过表面的甜言看见实质。早期佛教对「言语与行动的一致」有持续的关注——言说若不由行动支撑，便是一种虚伪；而虚伪，在经集的语汇中，是一种深刻的道德缺陷。

One who speaks sweetly, who is agreeable to friends — but who does not carry things through, the wise person knows him as a talker, not a doer.

Pleasantness in speech without corresponding action is the second marker of false friendship. The wise person (paṇḍita) sees through the pleasant surface to the absence of follow-through. Early Buddhist ethics is consistently suspicious of the gap between speech and conduct; in the Sutta Nipata's vocabulary, consistency between word and action is a marker of integrity, and its absence is a form of deception — even if the deception is not consciously intended.

常常寻找裂缝的人，不是伙伴——他只是在探找错误，坐在众人中，但他的坐并不是伙伴。

「寻找裂缝」（sandhi·chidda-pariyesī，寻找缝隙）是一个生动的比喻，原指在墙上或防御工事中寻找弱点；用于人际关系，指的是那些总是寻找对方的缺陷、弱点、过失，以便加以利用的人。这种人身处群体之中，外表像伙伴，实际上是一直在监视、寻机的人。「坐在众人中」是外在形式，「寻找裂缝」是内在态度——二者分离，友谊便是虚假的。

One who is always searching for cracks is not a companion — he is only looking for faults. He sits among people, but his sitting is not that of a companion.

The image of 'searching for cracks' (sandhicchidda) comes from the metaphor of probing a wall or fortification for weakness. Applied to friendship, it describes the person who is always scanning for the friend's flaws, vulnerabilities, and mistakes — not to help but to exploit or to judge. Physical proximity (sitting among people) is entirely compatible with this adversarial inner orientation; the sutta insists that true companionship is an attitude, not a location.

如同母亲对待她唯一的儿子——对朋友也应如此，依照这个原则，了解朋友的需要，他是真正的朋友。

「母亲对待她唯一的儿子」是经集（以及整个巴利文学）中最核心的慈悲比喻之一，在慈经（Sn 1.8）中已有出现。在那里，它描述对一切有情的普遍慈悲；在这里，它描述对具体朋友的深切关怀。唯一的孩子得到母亲最全面、最无保留的保护与关怀——真正的友谊应当具有同样的品质：全心全意地为对方的利益着想，了解并回应对方的需要。

As a mother tends her only child — so too a friend, by this principle, knows the needs of the friend. He is a true companion.

The mother-and-only-child simile recurs across the Pali Canon as the paradigm of unconditional, protective love — most famously in the Metta Sutta (Sn 1.8), where it describes the ideal orientation toward all beings. Here it is applied specifically to friendship: a true friend attends to the other's wellbeing with the thoroughness and whole-heartedness that a mother gives to her only child. 'Knowing the needs of the friend' implies attentiveness — real friendship requires perception of what the other actually needs, not just projection of what seems convenient to give.

具有羞耻之心的人，带着品格高尚的人的共同点——他来带来了欢乐，驱散了黑暗，带来了光明——这样的人，可以被称为朋友。

「羞耻之心」（hirī）在此成为真正朋友的核心特质，与经文开头的「无羞耻心」形成对照，构成全诗的呼应结构。具有hirī的人有内在的道德约束，不会对朋友做出不善之事；「品格高尚的人的共同点」（sappurisa-saddhamma）指善人所共同具备的品格。「驱散黑暗，带来光明」是一个常见的比喻：真朋友是照明者，帮助我们看清而非使我们迷惑。

One with moral shame, with the qualities shared by persons of integrity — he comes bringing joy, dispelling darkness, bringing light — such a person can be called a friend.

The sutta closes its portrait of true friendship with hirī as the central virtue — forming a ring composition with the opening verse's identification of its absence as the marker of false friendship. The person of hirī has an internal standard of conduct that makes them reliable; their presence 'dispels darkness and brings light' — a recurrent Pali image for wisdom and clarity. The 'qualities shared by persons of integrity' (sappurisa-saddhamma) is a cluster of virtues including faith, virtue, generosity, learning, and wisdom that the Buddha often attributes to good persons. True friendship involves being brought into contact with these qualities.

在这个世界上，背负重担者获得了休息，就如旅人获得荫凉处——智慧的人得到这样的良友，为朋友施好处，因为良师是难得的。

以两个具体的感官比喻来捕捉真友谊的体验：背负重担的人终于可以放下，旅人在烈日下终于找到荫凉。这两个比喻都强调解脱与休息——真正的友谊给人带来的是一种可以放下防卫、不必伪装的安全感。「良师是难得的」（kalyāṇamitto dullabho）是早期佛教对善知识的一贯评价，呼应经文最后的结语：获得这样的友人，是真正的幸运。

In this world, the person carrying a burden finds rest, as a traveller finds shade — the wise person who finds such a good friend benefits the friend, for a good companion is rare.

Two sensory images make the quality of true friendship tangible: the relief of setting down a heavy burden, the relief of finding shade on a hot journey. Both capture the same experience — in the presence of a true friend, one can stop performing, stop defending, stop pretending. The weight lifts. The heat is bearable. The rarity of such a companion (kalyāṇamitta, 'good friend' — one of the most important terms in Pali for spiritual friendship) is named explicitly: not a complaint but an instruction in how to value what has been found.

总结 · Summary

羞耻经（Hirī Sutta）以「羞耻」（hirī，道德上的自尊与羞耻感）为轴心，展开了一份关于真假友谊的辨别清单。虚假的友谊有三种标志：无行动支撑的言语（空谈）、无内在道德感的关系（无hirī）、以寻找对方弱点为目的的陪伴（寻找裂缝）。真正的友谊则以母亲对待独子为典范，全心了解并回应对方的需要，其存在本身便是光明与解脱——如重担得以放下，如旅人找到荫凉。经文将hirī（内在羞耻感与自尊）定位为真正友谊的核心美德，不是因为朋友需要互相审判，而是因为具有hirī的人有内在的道德约束，不会对朋友做出不善之事。良友是稀有的，经文以此提醒：遇到这样的人，是值得珍视的幸运。

The Hirī Sutta uses moral shame (hirī — the inner sense of self-respect that recoils from unworthy conduct) as the central virtue around which to build a taxonomy of true and false friendship. Three marks identify false friendship: words without actions, apparent goodwill without follow-through, and companionship that is actually surveillance — always looking for cracks. True friendship is modelled on the mother with her only child: whole-hearted attention to the other's actual needs, with hirī as the internal guarantee of reliability. Such a friend 'dispels darkness and brings light' — their presence is itself a kind of liberation, like setting down a heavy load or finding shade. The sutta's implicit argument is that moral character is the foundation of genuine relationship: a friend without inner ethical standards cannot be trusted, however pleasant they may seem. And the rarest, most precious thing one can find in this world is a true companion.
