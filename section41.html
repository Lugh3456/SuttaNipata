第四品·第三经 · 恶意八偈

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第四品·第二经 目录 Contents 第四品·第四经 →

以下经文据汉译《义足经》（T0198，支谦译，三国·吴）

有人恶心说，有人善心说—— 牟尼不受二，无取亦无舍。 执取自所见，谓此为真实—— 若人固执见，与人多争论。 所见各不同，争论无有休—— 若无己之见，何以起争端？ 眼见耳闻等，所触而受知—— 若人清净者，不计此等相。 计相则著相，著相则有见—— 有见则争论，此愚者之道。 无见则无著，无著则无争—— 无争则寂静，此智者之道。 不以见求见，不以闻求闻—— 清净无戒禁，无取无所著。 彼不著此岸，亦不著彼岸—— 无取无所著，此名为解脱。

逐句解释

有人恶心说，有人善心说——牟尼不受二，无取亦无舍。执取自所见，谓此为真实——若人固执见，与人多争论。

恶意八偈（Duṭṭhaṭṭhaka，「恶意·八偈」，duṭṭha = 恶意/腐败）的主题是：恶意的根源不在于说了什么，而在于对自己所见的固执执取。「牟尼不受二」——圣者不接受「恶心」与「善心」的二元框架，对两者都不执取也不排斥。「执取自所见，谓此为真实」：将自己的见解（diṭṭhi）视为绝对真理——「这才是事物的真实面貌」——是一切争论（vivāda）的根源。这个教导的深度在于：它不说「你的见解是错的」，而说「对任何见解的固执执取本身就是问题所在」。

Some speak with malice, some with goodwill — the sage accepts neither, neither grasping nor rejecting. One who clings to what one has seen, calling it the truth — one who holds to views rigidly — has many disputes with others.

The Duṭṭhaṭṭhaka's title suggests 'corrupt eight-verses' but the corruption it addresses is the corruption of view-clinging: the rigid grasping of one's own perceptions as absolute truth. The sage's non-acceptance of both 'malicious speech' and 'well-meaning speech' as categories to grasp is the opening move: categorising others' speech is already a form of view-taking. The root of contention is not that people have different views but that they hold their views with the specific quality of 'this is reality, this is true, this is how things are' — converting a perspective into a defended identity.

所见各不同，争论无有休——若无己之见，何以起争端？眼见耳闻等，所触而受知——若人清净者，不计此等相。

「若无己之见，何以起争端？」——这是八偈品中最犀利的论证之一：争论（vivāda）只有在对自己见解的执取中才能存在。没有「这是我的见解，我要捍卫它」，就没有争论的基础。这不是无知论（说所有见解都无所谓），而是对见解执取机制的分析：争论不是由不同意见引起的，而是由将意见与「我」绑定引起的。「眼见耳闻等，所触而受知」：感官知觉是真实的——「若人清净者，不计此等相」：清净的人（suddha，纯净的）不以感知到的相（nimitta，标志、特征）作为建构固定见解的材料。

What is seen differs from person to person, disputes never cease — if there were no view of one's own, what would give rise to contention? What is seen, heard, and contacted is known through the senses — the pure one does not calculate from these signs.

The argument about the origin of contention is precise: disagreements exist, but contention (vivāda — the adversarial holding of one's view against another's) requires the specific additional step of binding one's view to oneself as 'mine' or 'true.' Without this binding, perceptual differences do not escalate to contention. The 'pure one' who does not 'calculate from signs' (nimittaggāhī — grasping at marks/characteristics) is one who receives perceptual experience without immediately constructing it into fixed views that then require defence.

计相则著相，著相则有见——有见则争论，此愚者之道。无见则无著，无著则无争——无争则寂静，此智者之道。

这两颂以对比结构呈现了愚者道与智者道的完整因果链：计相→著相→有见→争论（愚者）；无见→无著→无争→寂静（智者）。「无见」（diṭṭhiṃ na gaṇhāti）不是字面上的「没有任何见解」，而是「不执取见解」——不把任何感知或理解转化为「这是真理，我要坚持它」的身份认同。这种「无见」的寂静，是八偈品所描述的解脱者的核心特质：不是无知，而是感知而不执取。

Calculating signs leads to clinging to signs; clinging to signs gives rise to views; views give rise to contention — this is the path of the fool. Without views there is no clinging; without clinging there is no contention; without contention there is stillness — this is the path of the wise.

The two causal chains set the sutta's whole argument in symmetrical opposition. The fool's path: perceiving → marking/categorising → clinging to the category → view-identity → contention. The wise person's path runs in reverse. 'Without views' does not mean perceptual blankness but the absence of the grasping that converts perceptions into defended positions. This is the Aṭṭhakavagga's central practical teaching: stillness (upasama) is not an absence of engagement with the world but the quality of engagement that does not generate identity-positions requiring defence.

彼不著此岸，亦不著彼岸——无取无所著，此名为解脱。

与洞穴八偈的结颂呼应：解脱不是「到达彼岸并在那里定居」，而是「不著此岸，亦不著彼岸」。这个「不著彼岸」的教导是八偈品中最具挑战性的：解脱（vimutti）不是对涅槃的执取，不是「我已解脱，我已到达涅槃」的新身份认同。真正的解脱是无取无著的——包括对「解脱」这个概念本身的无取无著。这是八偈品独特的、超越「得到涅槃」框架的教导。

One does not cling to this shore, nor to the far shore — with no grasping, nothing clung to: this is called liberation.

The closing verse echoes the Guhaṭṭhaka's conclusion: liberation is not attachment to Nibbāna as a new shore to inhabit. The Aṭṭhakavagga consistently refuses to let liberation become a new identity — 'I am the one who has attained, I am the one who is free.' This refusal is not a denial of liberation's reality but a description of its quality: genuine release has no remainder that clings to the fact of its own release. The Duṭṭhaṭṭhaka thus closes with the same paradox as the Guhaṭṭhaka: freedom from both shores, not as a nihilistic rejection of both but as the actual content of non-clinging.

总结 · Summary

恶意八偈是八偈品对「见争」（vivāda，因见解执取而起的争论）分析最系统的经典。其核心论点：争论不是由不同意见引起的，而是由对自己见解的固执执取（「这是真实，我要捍卫它」）引起的。感官知觉是真实的，但清净的人不以感知到的相作为建构固定见解的材料。愚者的因果链是：计相→著相→有见→争论；智者的道路是：无执取→无争→寂静。经文以八偈品的标志性结尾收束：解脱不是执取「彼岸」（涅槃）作为新的居所，而是不著此岸、亦不著彼岸——无取无著，这才是解脱的真实内容。

The Duṭṭhaṭṭhaka provides the Aṭṭhakavagga's most systematic analysis of contention rooted in views. Its central argument: disputes do not arise from having different perceptions — they arise from the specific act of binding one's perceptions to a defended identity ('this is true, this is mine'). The pure one receives sensory experience without converting it into fixed positions. The fool's causal chain — marking → clinging → view-identity → contention — and the wise person's reversal — no grasping → no contention → stillness — frame the practical teaching. Liberation, as the closing verse insists, is not grasping the far shore; it is the complete absence of clinging — to this shore, to the far shore, and to liberation itself as a possession.
