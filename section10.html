第一品·第十经 · 阿罗婆经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第一品·第九经 目录 Contents 第一品·第十一经 →

如是我闻。一时，世尊住阿罗婆城，在阿罗婆夜叉的居所中。阿罗婆夜叉来到世尊处，言道：「出去，沙门！」世尊言：「好的，道友。」遂离开。「进来，沙门！」世尊言：「好的，道友。」遂进入。再度，阿罗婆夜叉言：「出去，沙门！」世尊言：「好的，道友。」遂离开。「进来，沙门！」世尊言：「好的，道友。」遂进入。第三度，阿罗婆夜叉言：「出去，沙门！」……第四度，阿罗婆夜叉言：「出去，沙门！」世尊言：「道友，我不会出去。你要怎样，就怎样吧。」 阿罗婆夜叉言：「沙门，我要问你一个问题。若你不能回答，我将扰乱你的心，撕裂你的心脏，或抓住你的双脚，将你抛过恒河彼岸。」世尊言：「道友，我在这个世界，连同天神、魔王、梵天、沙门、婆罗门的世界中，看不到任何人能扰乱我的心、撕裂我的心脏，或抓住我的双脚将我抛过恒河彼岸。但无论如何，你问吧，道友。」 阿罗婆夜叉问：对于人而言，什么是此处最好的财富？行持什么能带来幸福？什么是诸味中最甜美者？如何生活，才称为最好的生活？ 世尊答：对于人而言，信心是此处最好的财富；行持善法能带来幸福；真实是诸味中最甜美者；以智慧生活，才称为最好的生活。 阿罗婆夜叉问：如何渡过洪流？如何渡过大海？如何超越苦痛？如何得到清净？ 世尊答：以信渡过洪流；以不放逸渡过大海；以精进超越苦痛；以智慧得到清净。 阿罗婆夜叉问：如何获得智慧？如何获得财富？如何获得名声？如何系缚朋友？从此世过渡到彼世时，如何不生忧愁？ 世尊答：对善法有信心、不放逸、精进——听闻正法可以获得智慧；合宜的行为可以获得财富；真实可以获得名声；布施可以系缚朋友。在家弟子若具足这四种品质，便不会在从此世过渡到彼世时生起忧愁。 阿罗婆夜叉问：若有在家弟子来请问：世间还有什么其他值得问的吗？——应当如何回答？ 世尊答：应当如此回答：如何避免忧愁与苦痛？如何在此生中解脱一切苦？ 于是阿罗婆夜叉说：为何我过去长久以来竟如此愚痴？——在如来这里求取微薄的供养，却不知道佛陀的圆满之法。我今日才知道：在出离中有最大的利益。在村落一村落地游行时，我将追随乔达摩，因为他宣说解脱之道。

逐句解释

「出去！」「进来！」「出去！」——第四次：「道友，我不会出去。」

阿罗婆夜叉是一位凶猛的精灵，用反复驱逐与召唤来测试世尊，或纯粹出于挑衅。世尊三次顺从（不执于「我的地方」的念头），第四次则平静地拒绝——不是出于固执，而是出于清醒：无谓的顺从本身也可以是软弱，而非修行。

"Go out!" — "Very well, friend." Three times the Buddha complies. The fourth time: "Friend, I will not go out. Do what you will."

The three compliances are not weakness — they demonstrate the Buddha's absence of attachment to place and status. The fourth refusal is equally non-attached: it is not stubbornness or indignation but a clear, quiet drawing of a line. Endless compliance with pointless demands is not spiritual virtue. The Buddha's 'do what you will' is a remarkable statement of fearlessness: he genuinely has no fear of whatever Āḷavaka might do, because there is nothing in him that can be destroyed by external force.

「我将扰乱你的心，撕裂你的心脏，将你抛过恒河彼岸。」——「道友，我看不到任何能做到这些的人。」

阿罗婆的威胁三重而具体：扰乱心智、撕裂心脏、用神通将人抛过恒河。世尊的回应不是英雄式的挑战，而是平静的陈述：在整个宇宙中，他看不到任何能做到这些事的存在。这不是虚张声势，而是彻底无畏的平静表达。

Āḷavaka threatens to derange the Buddha's mind, tear out his heart, and fling him across the Ganges. The Buddha's reply: "I do not see anyone in this world who could do that."

The three threats are graduated: mental disturbance, physical harm, and supernatural power. The Buddha's response — that no such being exists in the entire world including devas, Māra, and Brahmā — is not bravado but precision. A mind without craving, aversion, or delusion cannot be 'deranged' by external pressure; a practitioner without clinging to self cannot have a 'self' torn out; and one who has crossed the flood cannot be thrown back into it. The threats target things the Buddha no longer possesses.

对于人，最好的财富是什么？行持什么能带来幸福？什么是最甜美的味道？如何生活才是最好？

阿罗婆的四个问题转向了，从威胁转为真正的问道——这本身就是经文的一个转折点。从挑战者变成求法者，正是遇见真正的觉者时常有的转变。四个问题覆盖了人类幸福的核心层面：财富、行为、品味、生活方式。

What is here the best wealth for a person? What, well practiced, brings happiness? What is the sweetest of tastes? How does one live the best life?

The shift from threats to questions marks the real beginning of the encounter. Āḷavaka's aggression was a kind of testing; now he asks genuinely. The four questions are structured around the categories of a good human life: wealth (what to accumulate), conduct (what to practise), experience (what satisfies most deeply), and lifestyle (how to organise one's existence). The Buddha's four answers redefine each category from a materialist to a Dhamma-based framework.

【世尊】信心是最好的财富；行持善法带来幸福；真实是最甜美的味道；以智慧生活是最好的生活。

四个简洁而深刻的回答：信心（saddhā）是真正的财富；善行（dhamma）是幸福的基础；真实（sacca）是所有体验中最纯粹、最令人满足的味道；以智慧（paññā）生活，是生活品质的最高标准。每一个回答都将世俗的观念升华至精神的层次。

Faith is the best wealth; the Dhamma well-practised brings happiness; truth is the sweetest taste; living by wisdom is the best life.

Four reversals of ordinary expectation. Wealth is usually thought of as gold, land, or cattle — the Buddha names faith. Happiness is usually sought through pleasant experience — the Buddha names right conduct. The sweetest taste is usually a flavour — the Buddha names truth. The best life is usually defined by comfort, status, or pleasure — the Buddha names wisdom. Each answer is a gentle shock: not a denial of the ordinary but a disclosure of what underlies and surpasses it.

以信渡洪流，以不放逸渡大海，以精进超越苦痛，以智慧得到清净。

这四句与《雪山夜叉经》中的回答完全相同，再度出现，显示这是世尊对于「如何渡越」这一问题的标准答案：信、不放逸、精进、智慧，四者各有其位，各有其所渡越之境。

By faith the flood; by heedfulness the sea; by energy suffering; by wisdom, purification.

This verse appears verbatim in the Hemavata Sutta (Sn 1.9) and recurs here, suggesting it was a well-known summary teaching. Its appearance in two successive suttas — one addressed to a yakkha seeking verification, one to a yakkha who began with threats — underscores that the path's essentials do not change based on who is asking. Faith, heedfulness, energy, and wisdom cross every obstacle regardless of context.

真实可以获得名声；布施可以系缚朋友；在家弟子具足四种品质，离世不生忧愁。

世尊再次将灵性品质与世俗利益相连：真实带来名声，布施带来友谊。并指出，具足信、精进、善法、真实这四种品质的在家弟子，在离开此世时不会有遗憾与忧愁——因为他已以正确的方式生活过。

By truth one gains fame; by giving, one binds friends. The lay disciple with these four qualities does not grieve when departing this world.

Again, as in the Hemavata Sutta, the Buddha bridges spiritual and worldly concerns. Truth and generosity are not merely virtues that produce karmic merit in some future life — they produce reputation and friendship in this one. The lay person who lives by faith, diligence, practice, and truth does not approach death with regret, because their life has been well-spent by the deepest criterion available.

【阿罗婆】为何我过去如此愚痴？——今日才知道，出离中有最大的利益。我将追随乔达摩。

阿罗婆的自我反省是真诚的：他意识到自己长久以来一直生活在愚痴之中，竟然不知道就在如来这里，有着无可比拟的法宝。他以皈依与跟随世尊作结——从凶猛的挑战者，变为真诚的追随者。

Āḷavaka: "Why was I so foolish for so long? — wandering from village to village, not knowing the Buddha's perfect teaching. Now I know: the greatest benefit lies in renunciation. I will follow Gotama."

The conversion is complete and self-aware. Āḷavaka does not simply change his mind — he diagnoses his past self as 'foolish' (bāla) and articulates what he had been missing: the complete teaching right in front of him, which he had not known how to receive. His declaration — 'I will follow Gotama from village to village' — is a commitment to the wandering life of the spiritual follower, not just intellectual agreement. The fiercest adversary becomes the most committed follower.

总结 · Summary

阿罗婆经以一场戏剧性的对峙开场：凶悍的夜叉阿罗婆三度驱逐、三度召唤世尊，世尊始终平静应对——既不执著于「我的地盘」，也不无谓地软弱退让。当威胁换来彻底无畏的平静时，阿罗婆的姿态随之转变，开始真正发问。他所问的四个问题——最好的财富是什么、如何得到幸福、什么是最甜美的滋味、如何生活最好——世尊以信、善法、真实、智慧一一作答，颠覆了世俗对这些问题的惯常理解。经文以阿罗婆真诚的悔悟与皈依结束：他从挑战者变为追随者，从愚痴者变为求法者。这种转化，正是遇见真正觉者时最自然的结果。

The Āḷavaka Sutta is a study in the transformation of aggression through fearlessness. Āḷavaka is not a skeptic or a philosopher — he is a fierce yakkha who controls territory through intimidation. The Buddha enters his dwelling, complies patiently with three rounds of eviction and re-entry, then quietly refuses the fourth. No anger, no dignity injured, no retaliation — just the stillness of someone who has nothing to defend. When threats meet this kind of complete equanimity, they lose their purchase. Āḷavaka's questions follow naturally: what does this person know that makes them so unafraid? The four questions and answers form a compact account of what matters most in human life, redefined from the ground up. The sutta closes with Āḷavaka's self-aware conversion — recognising his own past foolishness and committing to follow the one who showed him the path.
