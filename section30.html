婆罗门孙陀利婆罗堕阇正在孙陀利河边举行火祭，当他完成了祭祀仪式之后，他四处望去，寻找值得供施食物的婆罗门。 他看见了世尊坐在树下，覆盖着衣，以僧侣所见的正念坐着。 他走近，说了这些话： 【孙陀利婆罗堕阇】这位，是婆罗门吗？ 【世尊】我不称那些由出生所决定的人为婆罗门，他们不过是「这样说的人」（bho-vādī）；他是一个「有漏者」——我称那些没有执取的人为婆罗门。 谁已放弃了所有的束缚，谁不颤抖，谁已超越了执着，谁已解脱——我称那人为婆罗门。 谁已斩断了皮带与缚绳和绳索，谁已去除了大门的门闩，谁已觉醒——我称那人为婆罗门。 谁承受侮辱、打击与拘禁，而不怒、有忍耐力、有精进力——我称那人为婆罗门。 谁无愤怒，有戒律，有德行，没有渴求，自我调御，这是最后的身体——我称那人为婆罗门。 如莲花不被水所沾，如芥子不留在锥尖，不被感官欲乐所染着——我称那人为婆罗门。 谁已在此了知苦的止息，已放下负担，已解脱——我称那人为婆罗门。 谁智慧深广，了知正道与邪道，已达到最高目标——我称那人为婆罗门。 谁不与在家人或出家人混合，游行无家，很少的欲求——我称那人为婆罗门。 已放下针对有情与无情众生的暴力棍棒，不杀不令他杀——我称那人为婆罗门。 在敌对者中无敌意，在执杖者中平和，在执着者中无执取——我称那人为婆罗门。 谁的贪欲、嗔恚、慢与虚伪已落下，如芥子从锥尖上落下——我称那人为婆罗门。 谁说话温和，有益而真实，不激怒任何人——我称那人为婆罗门。 谁在世间不取长不取短，无论给予或不给予，不贪不怒——我称那人为婆罗门。 谁已超越了此世与来世，就如蛇蜕皮而去——我称那人为婆罗门。 【孙陀利婆罗堕阇】很好，乔达摩！很好！就如扶起跌倒的东西，揭开蒙盖的物，给迷失者指路，在黑暗中举起灯火，使有眼之人能看见——乔达摩已经以种种方式阐明了法，我皈依乔达摩，皈依法，皈依僧——就从今日起，我以身相依归，直至命终！

逐句解释

【世尊】我不称那些由出生所决定的人为婆罗门——我称那些没有执取的人为婆罗门。

孙陀利经与贱民经（Sn 1.7）并列，是经集中对「婆罗门」概念最彻底重新定义的经典之一。婆罗门孙陀利婆罗堕阇（Sundarikabhāradvāja）在圣河边祭祀之后寻找值得供施的真婆罗门，却发现了佛陀——一位不属于婆罗门种姓的修行者。佛陀的回应是对婆罗门身份的彻底重新定义：不是出生，不是种姓，不是仪式，而是内心的品质——无执取（anupādāna）——才是真正婆罗门的标志。

I do not call one a brahmin by birth — I call one a brahmin who has no clinging.

The Sundarikabhāradvāja Sutta is one of the Sutta Nipata's most sustained redefinitions of brahminhood. The brahmin Sundarikabhāradvāja, having completed his fire sacrifice at a sacred river, searches for a true brahmin worthy of receiving the ritual food. He finds the Buddha — not a brahmin by birth. The Buddha's response redefines the category entirely: brahmin status is not conferred by birth or maintained by ritual but is constituted by an internal quality — freedom from clinging (anupādāna). What follows is a series of portraits of the person who deserves the name 'brahmin' by this redefinition.

如莲花不被水所沾，如芥子不留在锥尖，不被感官欲乐所染着——我称那人为婆罗门。

两个著名的比喻：莲花（paduma）生于水中却不被水所沾湿，是佛教中不执着于世间的经典象征；芥子（sāsapa）在锥尖上无法停留，描述欲望在清净心中同样无法停留。「不被感官欲乐所染着」（kāmesu anupālitto）是真婆罗门的本质特征——他生活在感官世界中，接触感官经验，却不被它染污、不对它执取。这种「不染」不是冷漠，而是彻底的自由——像莲花一样，根植于泥土，却开在水面之上。

Like a lotus not wetted by water, like a mustard seed not clinging to a needle's point, unstained by sensual pleasures — I call that one a brahmin.

The lotus image is the most recognisable symbol of non-attachment in Buddhist iconography: it grows in muddy water, is nourished by it, yet its surface repels water completely — it blooms above the level it grows in. The mustard-seed-on-a-needle's-point image is its kinetic counterpart: sensual desire has no surface to grip, slips immediately away. Together, these two images describe not the avoidance of sensory experience but the quality of response in the face of it — a mind so thoroughly free from the reflex of grasping that desire cannot find purchase.

在敌对者中无敌意，在执杖者中平和，在执着者中无执取——我称那人为婆罗门。

三个对比结构描述真婆罗门在世间的处境：面对敌意不还以敌意（aniggahītena，不被压制），面对持武力者保持平和（abyāpajjhena，无嗔），面对执着者不执取（anupādiyānena，无执）。这三个品质的共同点是「不以同样方式回应」：世间的对抗、暴力与执着不能从真婆罗门身上引出同样的东西。他不是中立的旁观者，而是一种不同存在方式的体现——完全临在于冲突与执取的世界中，却不被它定义。

Without hostility among the hostile, peaceful among those who bear weapons, without clinging among those who cling — I call that one a brahmin.

The three contrasts describe the true brahmin's mode of presence in a conflicted world: hostility cannot elicit hostility, violence cannot elicit violence, clinging cannot elicit clinging. This is not passivity or indifference but a different quality of being — so thoroughly free from reactive identification that the world's adversarial energies cannot find their mirror. The true brahmin is not outside the world of conflict and attachment but is within it, fully present, while constituting a different register of response entirely. This is the meaning of non-reactivity as an ethical stance.

【孙陀利婆罗堕阇】就如扶起跌倒的东西，揭开蒙盖的物，给迷失者指路，在黑暗中举起灯火——乔达摩已经以种种方式阐明了法，我皈依三宝，直至命终！

孙陀利婆罗堕阇的皈依偈是巴利文中最美丽的皈依表达之一，以四个日常生活比喻描述佛陀的教法的效果：扶起跌倒的东西（明灯于黑暗中）、揭开蒙盖之物（揭示遮蔽的真相）、为迷路者指引方向（引导混乱中的人）、在黑暗中举起灯火（使有眼者能看见）。这四个比喻不是修辞装饰，而是对「教法对一个人内心做了什么」的精确描述：他看见了，他找到了方向，被遮盖的东西被揭开了。皈依由此不是盲目的服从，而是对已见之光的感恩与归附。

As one lifts up what has fallen, uncovers what is covered, shows the way to one who is lost, holds up a lamp in the dark so those with eyes may see — in many ways the Dhamma has been made clear. I go for refuge to Gotama, the Dhamma, and the Saṅgha — from this day forward, for as long as I live!

Sundarikabhāradvāja's refuge formula is preceded by four similes that describe what has just happened to him: something fallen has been raised (a disorientation corrected), something covered has been revealed (a concealed truth uncovered), a direction has been given (confusion resolved), a light held up in darkness (enabling sight). These are not flattery but precise descriptions of an inner event — the teaching has functioned as teaching, and something that was hidden or lost or fallen has been restored. The going for refuge that follows is therefore grounded: it is the response of someone who has genuinely seen, not merely been impressed.

总结 · Summary

孙陀利经是一部关于「谁是真正的婆罗门」的系统论述，以婆罗门孙陀利婆罗堕阇在圣河边寻找值得供施的「真婆罗门」为框架，引出佛陀对婆罗门身份的彻底重新定义。经文通过十余个「我称那人为婆罗门」（tam ahaṃ brūmi brāhmaṇaṃ）的句式，描绘真婆罗门的画像：无执取、无愤怒、忍耐调御、莲花不被水沾、在敌对者中无敌意、说话温和真实、超越此世来世。这是一幅完全以内在品格而非出生或仪式为标准的圣者图像。经文以孙陀利婆罗堕阇的三宝皈依作结——他找到了他寻找的「真婆罗门」，不在种姓之中，而在法中。

The Sundarikabhāradvāja Sutta redefines brahminhood through a systematic series of portraits: the true brahmin is identified not by birth, ritual, or caste but entirely by inner qualities — freedom from clinging, absence of anger, endurance, non-attachment to sense pleasure (like a lotus untouched by water), non-hostility among the hostile, gentle and truthful speech, and transcendence of both worlds. Each portrait verse closes with 'that one I call a brahmin' — a structural refrain that progressively builds the redefinition. Sundarikabhāradvāja, having searched for a true brahmin worthy of ritual offering, finds him in the Buddha — and his conversion is expressed through four luminous similes (the fallen raised, the covered revealed, the lost directed, the lamp in darkness) that describe exactly what the teaching has done to him. He goes for refuge having genuinely seen.
