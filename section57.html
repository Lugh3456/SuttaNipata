第五品·第二经 · 帝须弥勒经

经集 · Sutta Nipata · 彼岸道品

← 第五品·第一经 目录 Contents 第五品·第三经 →

【帝须弥勒问】 谁在这世间没有渴爱？谁没有迷惑？ 谁渡越了两端，不被困扰—— 是哪位智者，抛下了织工与织布？ 【世尊答】 在这世间，守持梵行，放弃了欲乐—— 无渴爱无迷惑，渡越了两端而不困扰—— 这样的比丘，抛下了织工与织布。 【帝须弥勒问】 你称此人为谁？如何知道他是自由的？ 「无依者」——对你所说的这人，我有此疑问， 请告诉我关于他知识的完成。 【世尊答】 他放弃了过去，没有抓住未来—— 他在当中（现在）不停留，也不思量—— 无热恼，无我慢，无嗔恚—— 婆罗门，他无烟无欲，他已到达彼岸。

逐句解释

谁渡越了两端，不被困扰——是哪位智者，抛下了织工与织布？

帝须弥勒的问题使用了一个独特的意象：「织工与织布」（tantī ca tantudharo，维持连续性的线与持线者）——这是对「识别延续的机制」的诗意表达：将存在的连续性（轮回的持续）比作织布，织工是意志/渴爱，织布是所织出的连续存在。「抛下了织工与织布」——放弃了维持存在延续的机制——正是对彻底解脱的描述。「渡越了两端」（dvayaṃ antaṃ）：渡越了「有」与「无」两端，不再被有与无的二元困扰。

Who in the world has no craving, no confusion? Who has crossed the two extremes, not troubled — which wise person has abandoned the weaver and the weaving?

The 'weaver and weaving' (tantī and tantudhara) is the Pārāyanavagga's most distinctive metaphor for conditioned existence: craving and intention are the weaver, the woven fabric is the continued existence they produce. Abandoning both the weaver and the weaving is abandoning the mechanism of becoming itself — not just refusing to act, but releasing the will that generates action in the mode of self-continuation. 'The two extremes' — existence and non-existence, or past and future — are the poles between which the unawakened mind oscillates. The crossing is not reaching one pole or the other but releasing the oscillation entirely.

他放弃了过去，没有抓住未来——他在当中（现在）不停留，也不思量——无热恼，无我慢，无嗔恚——他无烟无欲，他已到达彼岸。

世尊的回答描述了彻底的时间解脱：「放弃了过去，没有抓住未来，在现在不停留也不思量」——三个时制（过去、未来、现在）的执著都完全放下。「无热恼」（nirūpādhi，无执取的热恼）——「无烟无欲」（anāsava，无漏，字面意义「无烟」）是阿罗汉的标志：如火不烧就没有烟，心不被漏（āsava，流出）就没有「烟」（热恼的迹象）。「已到达彼岸」（pāraṃ gato）——「彼岸」的字面宣告。

He has given up the past, has not grasped at the future — in the middle (present) he does not linger, does not calculate — without fever, without conceit, without anger — without smoke, without desire, he has reached the far shore.

'Giving up the past, not grasping the future, not lingering in the present' is the Pārāyanavagga's most complete description of temporal liberation: the arahant does not engage any of the three time-frames as a basis for identity or craving. 'Smokeless' (anāsava — literally 'without outflows,' the state of one in whom the āsavā have ended) uses the fire-smoke simile: a fire that has gone out produces no smoke; a mind without the flowing outflows of sensuality, becoming, and ignorance produces no residue of suffering-generation. 'Has reached the far shore' — the Pārāyanavagga names its own destination.

总结 · Summary

帝须弥勒经（彼岸道品版）以「织工与织布」的独特意象提问：谁抛下了维持存在延续的机制（渴爱是织工，所织的是连续存在）？世尊的回答以三个时制的解脱作答：放弃过去、不抓未来、不停留现在——彻底的时间非执著。「无烟无欲，已到达彼岸」是阿罗汉的终极描述，也是整个彼岸道品「到达彼岸」主题的具体呈现。帝须弥勒经（Sn 5.3）与帝须弥勒经（Sn 4.7）同名但内容不同，前者是彼岸道品的问道风格，后者是八偈品对梵行危险的警示。

The Pārāyanavagga's Tissametteyya Sutta (distinct from the Aṭṭhakavagga's Sn 4.7) uses the 'weaver and weaving' metaphor for the mechanism of becoming: craving weaves continued existence, and liberation is abandoning both the weaver and the fabric. The answer describes triple temporal liberation — past given up, future not grasped, present not lingered in — as the structure of freedom from conditioned becoming. 'Smokeless' (anāsava) names the arahant state through the absence of the outflows. 'Has reached the far shore' is the Pārāyanavagga naming its own destination in direct terms, without metaphor.
