第五品·第十二经 · 跋陀卫经

经集 · Sutta Nipata · 彼岸道品

← 第五品·第十一经 目录 Contents 第五品·第十三经 →

【跋陀卫问】 我请求你，已出离的人， 在渴爱中——在家中——的人所执取的—— 那些执取，放弃它们—— 放弃执取，安稳—— 不再需要任何地方，不再需要任何家。 请为我说明，你是有智慧的！ 已知法的英雄， 我来问你—— 告诉我这舍弃一切的话语， 让许多来自许多地方的人 听了这句话，听了你的话后， 就在这里，放弃了一切，冷却了。 【世尊答】 跋陀卫，放弃渴爱与执取—— 上下，横竖，过去、未来、现在—— 凡人们在世间执取的—— 都放弃它， 不再执着于存在的任何模式。 如此，不再以渴爱为家的人—— 正念，已从此渡越—— 学者叫他「无所依」—— 这样的圣人不再执着于 此岸，也不执着于彼岸—— 就像莲花生于水而不沾水。

逐句解释

在渴爱中——在家中——的人所执取的——那些执取，放弃它们——放弃执取，安稳——不再需要任何地方，不再需要任何家。

跋陀卫经（Bhadrāvudha Sutta）的提问者跋陀卫来自众多弟子聚集之处，他的问题带有一种「代众人问」的性质——「让许多来自许多地方的人，听了这句话后，就在这里，放弃了一切，冷却了」。核心意象是「家」（geha）与「无家」：「在家中的人所执取的」是一种生存方式——以执取构建家园、以渴爱为居所；「放弃执取，安稳——不再需要任何家」是其对立面，「无家」不是无处可居，而是不以任何事物为渴爱的居所。

What those who dwell in craving — in a home — grasp at — abandon those graspings — abandon grasping, be secure — needing no place, needing no home.

Bhadrāvudha speaks on behalf of many students gathered from many places, asking for a statement so direct that hearing it will cause immediate release — 'they will cool right here.' The central image is 'home' (geha): the home-dweller's grasping is a mode of existence built from craving — home as shelter constructed from desire. 'Needing no place, needing no home' (anālaya) is not homelessness in the social sense but the non-dependence on craving as a dwelling-place. The liberated person has released the need for craving to function as home, as refuge, as identity-anchor.

凡人们在世间执取的——都放弃它，不再执着于存在的任何模式。学者叫他「无所依」——此岸，也不执着于彼岸——就像莲花生于水而不沾水。

「无所依」（anālaya）：这是解脱者在彼岸道品中的另一个名字——不依赖任何事物作为「家」，不以任何存在模式为依托。「不再执着于此岸，也不执着于彼岸」——这是整部彼岸道品最完整的「超越此岸与彼岸」声明：不仅放弃此岸（苦的世间），也不执着于彼岸（解脱的彼岸）——渡越本身成为完成，而非新的执取对象。「就像莲花生于水而不沾水」——莲花比喻在巴利经典中经典地描述解脱者：在世间而不被世间所染，生于水而不沾水。

Whatever people grasp at in the world — abandon all of that, do not cling to any mode of existence. The wise call such a one 'without dependence' — this sage does not cling to this shore, nor to the far shore — like a lotus born in water, untouched by water.

'Without dependence' (anālaya) is one of the Pārāyanavagga's summary terms for the liberated person — one who does not lean on craving as home, on any mode of existence as anchor. 'Not clinging to this shore, nor to the far shore' is the Pārāyanavagga's most complete statement of the sage's double non-clinging: the practice is not merely crossing from this shore to the far shore and then inhabiting the far shore — even the crossing, even the far shore, must not become a new object of attachment. The lotus simile — born in water, unstained by water — is one of the oldest and most evocative Buddhist images for liberated presence in conditioned existence: fully in the world, fully unsoiled by the world's grasping-moisture.

总结 · Summary

跋陀卫经是彼岸道品中唯一明确「代众人问」的经典——跋陀卫要求一句让众人听了就能「就在这里，放弃一切，冷却」的话语。世尊的回答以「无所依」（anālaya）为核心：放弃上下横竖、过去未来现在的一切执取，不再以任何存在模式为依托。「学者叫他无所依」——解脱者不执着于此岸，也不执着于彼岸，「就像莲花生于水而不沾水」。跋陀卫经把整部彼岸道品的修行指南浓缩为一个意象：莲花在水中，不沾水。

The Bhadrāvudha Sutta is unique in the Pārāyanavagga for its explicit communal framing: Bhadrāvudha asks for a statement powerful enough to cool many practitioners on the spot. The answer centres on anālaya — without dependence, without craving-as-home — and states the double non-clinging most completely: the sage does not cling to this shore or the far shore. The lotus simile closes the exchange with one of Buddhism's most resonant images for liberated existence: fully present in water (conditioned existence), completely untouched by water's moistening power (the staining quality of grasping). The Bhadrāvudha Sutta distils the entire Pārāyanavagga's message into a single image of unbounded but unstained presence.
