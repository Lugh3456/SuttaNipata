第二品·第四经 · 吉祥经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第二品·第三经 目录 Contents 第二品·第五经 →

【天神】如此我所闻：众多天神与梵天世界，思维吉祥之事，渴望幸福——请告知最高的吉祥。 【世尊】不亲近愚人，而亲近智者；礼敬值得礼敬者——这是最高的吉祥。 居住在适宜之地，过去曾积累功德，对自己做出正确的引导——这是最高的吉祥。 广博的学问、技艺、训练有素的戒律、说话得体——这是最高的吉祥。 奉养父母、爱护妻儿、安分守职——这是最高的吉祥。 布施、法行、照顾亲族、无可指责的行为——这是最高的吉祥。 停止并远离罪恶、戒绝饮酒、在法中不放逸——这是最高的吉祥。 恭敬、谦逊、满足、感恩、适时听闻法——这是最高的吉祥。 忍耐、易于教导、遇见沙门、适时的法谈——这是最高的吉祥。 苦行、清净的生活、见到四圣谛、证悟涅槃——这是最高的吉祥。 心对世间的接触不颤动，无忧、无尘、安稳——这是最高的吉祥。 凡行如此者，处处不被击败，无论到哪里都获得吉祥——这是最高的吉祥。

逐句解释

不亲近愚人，而亲近智者；礼敬值得礼敬者——这是最高的吉祥。

吉祥经以问答结构展开，天神询问什么是最高的吉祥（maṅgala，吉祥、善兆）。佛陀的第一个回答便颠覆了当时流行的吉祥观念：吉祥不是外在的征兆（鸟鸣、星象、吉日），而是内在的品格选择——选择谁为伴侣、选择谁为榜样。「亲近智者」（paṇḍita-sevana）是整个佛教传统最一贯的建议之一，因为我们所接近的人塑造了我们的思维方式与行为习惯。

Not associating with fools, associating with the wise; honouring those worthy of honour — this is the highest blessing.

The Maṅgala Sutta redefines 'blessing' (maṅgala) against a background of popular belief in omens, auspicious signs, and lucky days. The Buddha's first answer immediately redirects attention inward: the highest auspiciousness is not an external sign but a choice about who you spend time with. The influence of companions on character is taken with absolute seriousness in early Buddhism — the person of wisdom, through proximity, gradually shapes the orientation of those around them. Honouring those worthy of honour is the complementary practice: cultivating the right relationship of reverence rather than familiarity or dismissal.

广博的学问、技艺、训练有素的戒律、说话得体——这是最高的吉祥。

第三颂赞扬在家生活中的世俗成就：广博学问（bāhusacca，多闻，深入学习各种知识）、技艺（sippa，工艺与实际技能）、训练有素的戒律（vinaya，规范身语意的训练）以及说话得体（subhāsita，善加说话）。吉祥经的独特之处在于它同等重视在家人的世俗成就与出家人的解脱修行——两者都是「最高的吉祥」，在各自的位置上。

Broad learning, skill, well-trained discipline, well-spoken words — this is the highest blessing.

The sutta is notable for its inclusion of worldly excellence alongside spiritual attainment — both count as 'highest blessing.' Bāhusacca (wide learning) was highly valued in ancient India and in early Buddhism; the learned person can draw on accumulated wisdom. Skill (sippa) covers craft and practical competence. Well-trained discipline (vinaya) is the training of conduct — the same word used for the monastic code, but applicable to any context. Well-spoken words (subhāsita) — speech that is true, useful, timely, and kind — is throughout the Canon one of the primary expressions of wisdom in daily life.

奉养父母、爱护妻儿、安分守职——这是最高的吉祥。

这一颂专门为在家人而设，赞扬家庭与社会责任的履行：奉养父母（mātāpitu-upaṭṭhāna，服侍父母）、照顾妻儿（puttadārassa saṅgaho，摄护子女与妻子）、安分守职（anākulā ca kammantā，有秩序的工作）。佛陀将这些世俗责任明确列入「最高吉祥」，表明佛教的解脱道不要求放弃在家义务，在家人在尽责的同时，也可以走在吉祥的道路上。

Caring for one's mother and father, tending wife and children, unconfused occupations — this is the highest blessing.

The sutta explicitly includes household responsibilities among the highest blessings — a significant affirmation for lay practitioners. Caring for parents (mātāpitu-upaṭṭhāna) is a primary social virtue throughout the Pali Canon; it represents both gratitude and reciprocity. 'Tending wife and children' implies active care rather than mere provision. 'Unconfused occupations' (anākulā ca kammantā) suggests livelihood pursued with integrity and clarity, without the tangles of deception or harmfulness. The lay path is presented as fully valid and praiseworthy.

停止并远离罪恶、戒绝饮酒、在法中不放逸——这是最高的吉祥。

从世俗美德逐步转向更深的修行：主动停止并远离不善行为（āratī viratī pāpā，远离与戒除恶行）；戒绝饮酒（majjapānā ca saṃyamo，节制饮酒），这在五戒中是第五戒；在法中不放逸（appamādo ca dhammesu，在法的修持中不懈怠）。「不放逸」（appamāda）是整个巴利文学中佛陀最强调的修行品质之一，佛陀临终时的最后一句话便是「诸法无常，当不放逸精进」。

Ceasing and refraining from evil, abstaining from intoxicants, not being negligent in the Dhamma — this is the highest blessing.

The sutta moves from relational virtues toward individual practice. Refraining from evil is both a cessation (āratī — turning away) and a maintenance (viratī — ongoing restraint). Abstaining from intoxicants is the fifth precept — specifically named here, indicating that clarity of mind is a prerequisite for spiritual development. Appamāda — heedfulness, non-negligence, diligence — is one of the most insistently repeated qualities in the Pali Canon; the Buddha's last words before parinibbāna were 'All conditioned things are impermanent; strive with heedfulness.' Its presence here grounds the whole list of blessings in the quality of attention one brings to living.

苦行、清净的生活、见到四圣谛、证悟涅槃——这是最高的吉祥。

经文的高峰：苦行（tapas，在此指修行的精进与自律，而非极端苦行）、清净的生活（brahmacariya，梵行，指戒律清净的修行生活）、见到四圣谛（ariyasaccāna dassanaṃ，直接见道）、证悟涅槃（nibbānasacchikiriyā，亲证涅槃）。吉祥经在此以解脱道的终点作为「最高吉祥」的顶点，将整个从世俗美德到解脱的路径整合在同一个「吉祥」概念之下。

Austerity, the holy life, seeing the Noble Truths, realising Nibbāna — this is the highest blessing.

The sutta reaches its apex with the full path: tapas (disciplined effort — not extreme asceticism but the sustained application of energy to practice), brahmacariya (the holy life — pure conduct and celibacy in the monastic sense, or ethical integrity in the lay sense), seeing the Noble Truths directly (ariyasacca-dassana, stream-entry and beyond), and realising Nibbāna (nibbānasacchikiriyā — making Nibbāna actual through direct experience). The arc of the sutta is complete: from choosing the right companions, through family duty and social virtue, through renunciation and practice, to liberation itself — all framed as dimensions of the highest blessing.

心对世间的接触不颤动，无忧、无尘、安稳——这是最高的吉祥。

最后一颂描述解脱后的心的品质：不被世间的接触所动摇（phassā na kampati，不因感官接触而颤动）、无忧（asokā，没有悲伤）、无尘（virajā，没有染著，无烦恼之尘）、安稳（khema，平安）。这四个词描述的不是一种麻木或退缩，而是一种充分接触世界却不被它夺走平静的状态——这正是解脱者在世间行走的方式。整首经以此作结，表明最终的吉祥是内心的不动摇，而非任何外在条件的满足。

The mind that, when touched by the world's vicissitudes, does not tremble — sorrowless, dustless, secure — this is the highest blessing.

The final verse describes the quality of the liberated mind in contact with the world. The eight worldly conditions (gain and loss, fame and disrepute, praise and blame, pleasure and pain) are the constant 'vicissitudes' (phassā) that the mind meets. The liberated mind meets them without trembling — not through numbness or withdrawal but through non-attachment at the level of response. Sorrowless (asokā), dustless (virajā — without the dust of defilements), secure (khema — safe, at ease): these are not descriptions of a state achieved by avoiding experience but of a mind that moves through experience without being destabilised by it. This is the ultimate maṅgala.

总结 · Summary

吉祥经是上座部传统最广泛流传的经典之一，至今仍是东南亚佛教国家的家喻户晓的吉祥经文。其核心贡献在于彻底重新定义了「吉祥」（maṅgala）：不是外在的征兆与预兆，而是内在品格与修行的整合路径。经文从选择明智的伴侣开始，经过世俗责任（奉养父母、照顾家庭、安分工作）、道德修行（布施、远离恶行、不放逸）、精神实践（梵行、见谛、证涅槃），最终达到心的不动摇——无论世间带来什么接触，都无忧、无尘、安稳。这条从凡俗善行到解脱的完整路径，被统摄在同一个「最高吉祥」的框架下，显示出佛教伦理与解脱论的有机整合。

The Maṅgala Sutta is one of the most widely recited texts in Theravāda Buddhism, still memorised and chanted across Southeast Asia. Its central contribution is a complete redefinition of 'blessing': not external omens or auspicious signs but an integrated path of character, conduct, and realisation. The sutta moves from choosing wise companions, through household responsibilities and social virtue, through ethical training and Dhamma practice, to direct realisation of Nibbāna, and finally to the quality of the liberated mind — stable, sorrowless, untroubled by whatever the world brings. This entire arc — from the most ordinary human choices to the highest liberation — is presented as a single, coherent account of what genuine blessedness consists in. External fortune is not the target; inner unshakeability is.
