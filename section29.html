第三品·第三经 · 善说经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第三品·第二经 目录 Contents 第三品·第四经 →

【世尊】比丘们，言语有四种特征成为善说，而不是恶说——比丘们，什么是善说？ 比丘们，在这里，比丘只说善说，不说恶说——只说法，不说非法——只说令人喜悦的，不说令人不悦的——只说真实的，不说虚假的。 【万祇沙】智者只赞叹善说——说法而非非法，这被称为第一。 说令人欢喜的而非令人苦的——说真实而非虚假——比智者更聪明的人，不会因傲慢而说什么。 涅槃是善说中的第一——那是言语的最高——那些了知此法的人对彼进行分辨，他们不向着其他的方向去。 【世尊】很好，万祇沙！你正是这样理解了我的意思！ 【万祇沙】有智慧的牟尼，以善说为宝贵——令听者感受安慰和欢喜，他以这样的一句话赞美了佛陀——他是世界中最高的牟尼，他是超越一切的牟尼，他是殊胜牟尼，他是言语的最高。

逐句解释

比丘们，在这里，比丘只说善说，不说恶说——只说法，不说非法——只说令人喜悦的，不说令人不悦的——只说真实的，不说虚假的。

「善说」（subhāsita）的四个标准构成经文的核心：说善而非恶（善言）；说法而非非法（法语，而非与法相违的言辞）；说令人喜悦的而非令人苦的（柔软语，不是强制性的）；说真实而非虚假（真实语）。这四个标准相互补充：不是所有真实的话都是适当的（需要「令人喜悦」的条件），不是所有令人喜悦的话都是真实的（需要真实的条件）。四者合在一起，才是完整的善说。

Here, a monk speaks only what is well-spoken, not what is ill-spoken; only Dhamma, not non-Dhamma; only what is pleasing, not what is displeasing; only what is true, not what is false.

The four criteria for subhāsita (well-spoken speech) form an intersecting matrix rather than a simple list: good rather than harmful, Dhamma rather than non-Dhamma, pleasing rather than harsh, true rather than false. No single criterion is sufficient alone: true speech can still be harmful, pleasing speech can still be false, Dhamma speech can still be delivered harshly. The intersection of all four is what constitutes genuinely well-spoken speech — a standard that is simultaneously ethical, doctrinal, interpersonal, and epistemological. The Buddha's own speech is the paradigm for all four.

涅槃是善说中的第一——那是言语的最高——那些了知此法的人对彼进行分辨，他们不向着其他的方向去。

万祇沙（Vaṅgīsa）的诗意回应将善说的标准推向顶点：涅槃（Nibbāna）本身是所有善说中最「善」的——它是「言语的最高」（paramaṃ vacanaṃ）。这不仅是说「涅槃」这个词是最好的词，而是说指向涅槃的言语，关于涅槃的教导，是所有可能的言语中最有价值的。了知此法的人「不向着其他的方向去」——一旦了解到言语的最高表达是指向解脱的法语，其他一切言语都失去了同等的吸引力。

Nibbāna is the foremost of well-spoken speech — that is the highest of words — those who know this Dhamma distinguish it rightly, they go toward no other direction.

Vaṅgīsa's verse elevates the criterion of well-spoken speech to its ultimate expression: Nibbāna is paramaṃ vacanaṃ — the supreme word. This is not merely about the word 'Nibbāna' but about speech oriented toward liberation — teaching that genuinely points to the cessation of suffering. All other speech, however true, pleasing, or useful, is subordinate to this. Those who have understood this 'go toward no other direction' — having found what speech is ultimately for, they no longer wander through other discursive territories seeking satisfaction. The sutta thus frames the entire human capacity for language as finding its highest expression in speech that leads to liberation.

有智慧的牟尼，以善说为宝贵——令听者感受安慰和欢喜，他以这样的一句话赞美了佛陀——他是世界中最高的牟尼，他是超越一切的牟尼，他是殊胜牟尼，他是言语的最高。

万祇沙以赞叹佛陀收尾：佛陀是「世界中最高的牟尼」、「超越一切的牟尼」、「殊胜牟尼」、「言语的最高」。这一系列赞美将善说的讨论与佛陀本身联系起来：佛陀不仅教导善说，他本人就是善说的最高体现——他的每一句话都同时满足「善而非恶」、「法而非非法」、「令人喜悦」、「真实」这四个标准，并且始终指向涅槃。佛陀就是善说的活例证与其最高体现。

The wise sage values well-spoken speech — bringing comfort and joy to the listener; he praises the Buddha in this verse — the supreme sage in the world, the sage beyond all, the excellent sage, the highest of words.

Vaṅgīsa's closing praise identifies the Buddha as the living embodiment of the sutta's teaching: not merely one who teaches about subhāsita but one in whom subhāsita is completely actualised. 'The highest of words' (vacanaṃ paramaṃ) — applied to the Buddha — frames him as the fullest expression of what human speech can be: every utterance simultaneously good, Dhamma-aligned, pleasing, true, and oriented toward liberation. Vaṅgīsa, as the Canon's celebrated poet-monk, brings his craft to the service of this recognition — the sutta about speech ends in a speech act that exemplifies the highest speech.

总结 · Summary

善说经是大品（Mahāvagga）中最短小的经典，但内容精到。佛陀先定义善说（subhāsita）的四个标准：善而非恶、法而非非法、令人喜悦而非令人苦、真实而非虚假。诗人比丘万祇沙随即以诗偈回应，将这一框架推向最高——涅槃是所有善说中最「善」的，是言语的最高表达。了解此法的人不再被其他方向所吸引。万祇沙最后以赞叹佛陀收尾：佛陀不仅教导善说，他本人就是善说的最高体现——「言语的最高」。这首经将言语伦理（四个标准）与修行方向（指向涅槃）以及对佛陀的礼敬，整合在一个简洁的诗意框架中。

The Subhāsita Sutta is the Mahāvagga's briefest text but precisely crafted. The Buddha defines four criteria for well-spoken speech: good rather than harmful, Dhamma rather than non-Dhamma, pleasing rather than harsh, true rather than false. Vaṅgīsa immediately responds in verse, elevating the framework to its apex: Nibbāna is the foremost of well-spoken speech — the highest expression of what language can accomplish. Those who understand this no longer wander toward other discursive destinations. His closing praise frames the Buddha as the living embodiment of the teaching: not merely the teacher of subhāsita but its fullest realisation — 'the highest of words.' The sutta integrates speech ethics, the direction of practice, and the reverence for the teacher in a single compact poetic exchange.
