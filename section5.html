第一品·第五经 · 纯陀经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第一品·第四经 目录 Contents 第一品·第六经 →

铁匠纯陀问：我请问牟尼，具慧的善逝——世间有多少种沙门？请告诉我，世尊。 世尊答：纯陀，沙门有四种，没有第五种。我为你当面说明：道胜者、道说者、依道活者、道污者。 纯陀问：诸佛如何描述道胜者？无可比拟者又如何？请为我解说道说者，以及依道活者。 世尊答：越过了疑惑、离开了苦刺、以涅槃为喜、无有贪欲、引导世间天人——如来被智者称为道胜者。 在此界中视最上者为最上，宣说法与义、分析诸法、断除疑惑——这位牟尼被智者称为道说者。 依法而住，自制，说令人欢喜之语，不伤害他人，遵守智者的训导——这位牟尼被智者称为依道活者。 伪装为有德者，污辱家族、无礼、放肆、虚假、无有节制、喋喋不休——这样的人被智者称为道污者。 在家弟子有智慧，应了知这些情况：听闻这一切后，不因他而失去信心。怎能将不净者与净者、不修者与修者等同而观？

逐句解释

【纯陀】世间有多少种沙门？请告诉我，世尊。

铁匠纯陀（即后来为世尊提供最后一餐的那位纯陀）直接向佛陀发问：世间的沙门（出家修行者）究竟有多少种？这是一个极为实际的问题——当时印度各种沙门团体并存，令普通人无所适从。

Cunda the smith: "How many types of ascetics are there in the world? Please tell me, Blessed One."

Cunda's question is a practical one. In the Buddha's India, wandering ascetics (samaṇas) of many schools competed for lay support and devotion. A layperson genuinely wanting to support worthy practitioners needed some way to distinguish them. The Buddha does not deflect the question — he gives a clear, fourfold answer that cuts through the proliferation of competing claims.

【世尊】沙门有四种，没有第五种：道胜者、道说者、依道活者、道污者。

世尊的回答简洁而完整：沙门恰好有四种，不多不少。这四种涵盖了从最高成就到最低堕落的整个光谱——既有真实的修行者，也有打着沙门旗号的伪装者。

The Blessed One: "There are four kinds of ascetics, Cunda — not a fifth. I will explain them to your face: the path-victor, the path-teacher, the path-liver, and the path-corrupter."

The fourfold scheme is characteristically Buddhist in its precision: not a vague 'many types' but exactly four, arranged implicitly from highest to lowest. 'I will explain them to your face' (sammukha) signals directness — this is not evasion or mystification but clear instruction for a clear question.

越过了疑惑、离开了苦刺、以涅槃为喜、无有贪欲、引导世间天人——如来被称为道胜者。

道胜者（maggajina）是已彻底征服道路的人：疑惑已越过，烦恼之刺已拔除，以涅槃为真正的喜悦，贪欲已尽，能够引导人天两界的众生。这是最高的成就——佛陀本身即是道胜者。

One who has crossed over doubt, is free from thorns, rejoices in Nibbāna, is free from greed, and leads the world with its devas — the Tathāgata is called the path-victor by the wise.

Five marks of the path-victor: doubt crossed (no uncertainty about the path or the goal), free from thorns (the 'thorns' of defilement that pierce and wound the mind), Nibbāna as delight (not merely an intellectual goal but a lived joy), free from greed, and capable of guiding others — including devas, the divine beings. The last quality matters: the path-victor's attainment is not private; it radiates outward as guidance for others.

视最上者为最上，宣说法与义、分析诸法、断除疑惑——被称为道说者。

道说者（maggadesī）是能够清晰讲说道路的人：他洞见最高的真理，能够宣说法义、分析诸法、为他人断除疑惑。道说者本身已有相当深厚的修证，但其主要角色是传授与引导。

One who in this world declares the highest as highest, who teaches the Dhamma with its meaning, who analyses the phenomena, who cuts off doubt — such a sage is called the path-teacher by the wise.

The path-teacher is distinguished from the path-victor not by lesser sincerity but by scope of attainment. The teacher has genuine realisation — they see the highest as highest (no confusion about what matters most), they teach with precision (Dhamma and its meaning together, not just formulae), and they resolve doubt in others. This is the arahant-disciple or highly attained teacher who can transmit what they have received.

依法而住，自制，说令人欢喜之语，不伤害他人，遵守智者的训导——被称为依道活者。

依道活者（maggajīvī）是以法为生活方式的人：依法而住，身心自制，言语令人欢喜而不伤害人，遵守智者的训诫。他可能尚未证得最高果位，但生活的每一面都以道为依归。

One who lives by the Dhamma, restrained, who speaks words that are pleasing, who does not harm others, who follows the instruction of the wise — such a sage is called the path-liver by the wise.

The path-liver has not necessarily attained the heights of the first two — but they live the path completely. Their restraint is genuine, their speech is kind and not harmful, they take guidance from those wiser than themselves. This is the sincere, diligent practitioner who may still be on the way but is unequivocally on it. The sutta respects this category: living the path, even without full attainment, is honourable.

伪装为有德者、污辱家族、无礼、放肆、虚假、无有节制、喋喋不休——被称为道污者。

道污者（maggadūsī）是披着修行者外衣的污染者：伪装高德、败坏所依止的家族、粗鲁无礼、放肆妄为、言语虚假、毫无节制、话多而无实质。这样的人不是修行者，而是修行界的毒瘤。

One who masquerades as having virtues, who is a corrupter of families, insolent, reckless, a deceiver, without self-restraint, a prattler — such a one is called the path-corrupter by the wise.

The path-corrupter is dangerous precisely because they appear to be what the first three are. Seven characteristics mark them: false virtue (performance without substance), corruption of lay supporter families (exploiting the devotion of those who feed and shelter them), insolence, recklessness, deceit, no self-restraint, and hollow talk. Together these paint a vivid portrait of the spiritual fraud — not a rare figure in any tradition, in any age.

在家弟子有智慧，应了知这些情况；听闻这一切后，不因他而失去信心。怎能将不净者与净者等同而观？

有智慧的在家弟子应当了解这四种区别：了解之后，遇到道污者也不应对整个修行团体失去信心。不能因为有人败坏了沙门之名，就将净者与不净者一视同仁——浑水中仍有清流。

The wise lay disciple, having understood all this, should not lose faith on account of him. How could one equate the impure with the pure, the uncultivated with the cultivated?

The sutta closes with practical advice for the layperson: knowing the fourfold distinction protects against two errors. The first error is naive trust — giving support to any robed person without discernment. The second error is cynical dismissal — encountering a fraud and concluding that all monastics are frauds. The wise path between these errors is discrimination: the path-corrupter's existence does not contaminate the path-victor, any more than a counterfeit coin disproves the existence of genuine currency.

总结 · Summary

纯陀经简短而务实，为在家信众提供了一套辨别真假修行者的框架。道胜者已彻底征服道路，以引导众生为其自然功能；道说者有真实修证，善于传授与分析；依道活者以法为日常生活的准绳；道污者则以外衣遮盖内在的空虚与腐败。经文的最后一句尤其重要：不能因为有道污者的存在，就对整个修行界失去信心。区别净与不净，是在家弟子的智慧责任——而这种区别能力本身，正是一种修行的成熟。

The Cunda Sutta is one of the most practically useful texts in the collection — a clear taxonomy of spiritual practitioners designed to help lay disciples navigate a crowded field of competing claimants. The four types form a spectrum from genuine attainment to complete corruption, with the middle two categories — the path-teacher and the path-liver — covering the range of sincere practitioners at various stages. The sutta's final warning against losing faith is as important as the taxonomy itself: the existence of charlatans is not an argument against the path, only an argument for discernment. A mature layperson supports the worthy, avoids the harmful, and does not allow the two to collapse into each other. This discernment — knowing the difference — is itself an expression of wisdom.
