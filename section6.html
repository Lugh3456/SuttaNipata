第一品·第六经 · 败亡经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第一品·第五经 目录 Contents 第一品·第七经 →

如是我闻。一时，世尊住舍卫城祇树给孤独园。时，有一天神于深夜，以其光照亮整个祇园，来到世尊处，礼拜世尊，立于一侧，以偈问道： 天神问：我们来请问乔达摩——何为败亡之人？我们来请问世尊——何为败亡之因？ 世尊答：繁荣者易知，败亡者也易知——爱法者繁荣，厌法者败亡。 天神问：我们如此了知第一点了。请告诉我们第二点，世尊——败亡的原因是什么？ 世尊答：喜爱不善之人、不喜爱善人、乐于不善之人的法——此为败亡之因。 嗜眠、喜聚会、怠惰、易怒、行为粗暴——此为败亡之因。 虽有能力，却不养活年迈的父亲与母亲——此为败亡之因。 以虚假言语欺骗婆罗门、沙门或其他出家者——此为败亡之因。 拥有大量财富、黄金与食物，却独自享用——此为败亡之因。 以出身、财富、家族而骄傲，轻视自己的亲族——此为败亡之因。 好色、嗜酒、赌博，将所赚的财物挥霍一空——此为败亡之因。 不满足于自己的妻子，与娼妓厮混，与他人妻子厮混——此为败亡之因。 年岁已过，却娶回年轻妻子，因嫉妒而辗转难眠——此为败亡之因。 将权力交给耽于酒食的女人或男人——此为败亡之因。 出身名门、志向远大，实际财力却微薄——此为败亡之因。 这些便是世间败亡之因。有智慧的圣者了知这些，以圣慧观察，走向有福之界。

逐句解释

爱法者繁荣，厌法者败亡。

这是整部经文的根本原则：对法（善、正道）的态度，决定了一个人的兴衰。这不是神秘的判断，而是因果的自然法则。

One who loves the Dhamma prospers; one who hates it declines.

The foundational principle is stated before the list begins: the orientation toward the good determines one's trajectory. Everything that follows is a specific application of this. 'The Dhamma' here is not merely Buddhist doctrine but the broader principle of what is right, true, and beneficial — the grain of reality, not against it.

喜爱不善之人、不喜爱善人、乐于不善之人的法——此为败亡之因。

选择与不善者为友，远离善人，并且乐于接受不善者的影响——这三者合在一起，是走向败亡的第一步。我们选择的同伴，塑造了我们的方向。

Loving the disagreeable, not loving the good, delighting in the Dhamma of the disagreeable — this is a cause of downfall.

Association is the first cause of downfall in the list — before laziness, greed, or any specific vice. The teaching throughout the Pali Canon consistently emphasises that companionship shapes character more than almost any other factor. Choosing unwholesome associates is not a neutral act; it is a direction-setting one.

嗜眠、喜聚会、怠惰、易怒、行为粗暴——此为败亡之因。

五种个人性格上的弱点：贪睡（懒于修行与工作）、喜好无谓的社交聚会、懒散怠惰、容易发怒、举止粗鲁——这些习性合在一起，构成败亡的温床。

Being addicted to sleep, fond of gatherings, lazy, irritable, of rough conduct — this is a cause of downfall.

Five character flaws that compound each other: excessive sleep (avoiding the demands of life and practice), social idleness (gatherings without purpose), laziness (not applying oneself to what matters), irritability (reacting to friction with anger rather than patience), and rough conduct (treating others without care or dignity). None is catastrophic alone; together they ensure nothing worthwhile is built or sustained.

虽有能力，却不养活年迈的父亲与母亲——此为败亡之因。

有能力赡养父母却不赡养——这在佛教伦理中是极为严重的过失。父母是子女的「第一位老师」，不报其恩，是对基本人伦的背弃。

Though able, not supporting one's aged father and mother — this is a cause of downfall.

Filial responsibility is treated throughout the Pali Canon as a fundamental moral obligation. The qualification 'though able' matters: this is not a condemnation of those who genuinely cannot provide, but of those who choose not to. The Buddha elsewhere calls parents 'the first teachers' and 'worthy of offerings' — neglecting them is a moral failure of the most basic kind.

以虚假言语欺骗婆罗门、沙门或其他出家者——此为败亡之因。

用谎言欺骗修行者——不论是婆罗门、佛教比丘，还是其他出家人——是一种特别严重的欺骗，因为它不仅是谎言，更是对神圣关系的亵渎。

Deceiving with false words a brahmin, ascetic, or other mendicant — this is a cause of downfall.

Deceiving spiritual practitioners is singled out separately from general dishonesty because of the specific nature of the relationship. Those who seek alms have placed themselves in a position of vulnerability and trust. To deceive them is to exploit that vulnerability. The sutta does not limit this to Buddhist monks — any sincere seeker deserves honesty.

拥有大量财富，却独自享用——此为败亡之因。

财富本身并不是败亡之因；独占财富、不与他人分享，才是问题所在。在佛教经济观中，布施是将财富转化为功德的途径；囤积则是贪执的表现。

Having great wealth, gold, and food, but enjoying it alone — this is a cause of downfall.

Wealth is morally neutral in itself; what one does with it is not. The teaching is not that wealth causes downfall — it is that hoarding does. Generosity (dāna) is consistently identified in the Canon as the foundation of both worldly prosperity and spiritual progress. The person who accumulates without giving contracts their own humanity and cuts themselves off from the goodwill of the community.

以出身、财富、家族而骄傲，轻视自己的亲族——此为败亡之因。

以出身、财富或家族背景为傲，由此轻视甚至鄙视自己的亲属——这种骄傲切断了人与人之间最基本的纽带，也是傲慢之心的直接体现。

Proud of birth, wealth, or clan, and looking down on one's own relatives — this is a cause of downfall.

The three bases of pride listed here — birth, wealth, and clan — are precisely the things the brahmin tradition valued most. The Buddha repeatedly challenges this hierarchy. Pride in unchosen advantages (birth, family) is especially groundless. To use these as reasons to look down on one's own relatives is to let vanity dissolve the bonds of genuine human connection.

好色、嗜酒、赌博，将所赚的财物挥霍一空——此为败亡之因。

三种经典的自我毁灭行为：追逐女色（放纵于感官欲乐）、沉湎于酒、迷恋赌博——三者相加，足以将任何程度的积蓄彻底耗尽。

Addicted to women, wine, and gambling, one squanders all one's earnings — this is a cause of downfall.

The three vices are a recognisable cluster in virtually every culture: sexual indulgence, intoxication, and gambling. Each involves the pursuit of intense short-term pleasure at the cost of longer-term wellbeing. Together they are particularly destructive because each feeds the others — the gambler drinks, the drinker pursues sexual encounters, and all three drain the resources that might otherwise build a stable life.

不满足于自己的妻子，与娼妓及他人妻子厮混——此为败亡之因。

不满足于合法婚姻关系，转而追逐娼妓或与他人妻子私通——这不仅违反了基本的性道德，也破坏了家庭与社会的基本信任结构。

Not content with one's own wife, being seen with prostitutes and the wives of others — this is a cause of downfall.

Sexual faithfulness is identified as a cause of stability; its absence as a cause of downfall. The specific mention of prostitutes and others' wives covers two forms of sexual misconduct with different social consequences: one involving commercial exploitation, the other involving betrayal of both one's spouse and another family's bond. Both generate suffering in expanding circles.

年岁已过，却娶回年轻妻子，因嫉妒而辗转难眠——此为败亡之因。

一个年老的男人娶了年轻妻子，终日因嫉妒而不得安睡——这是一种因不切实际的欲望而自招痛苦的状态。欲求与实际能力之间的落差，带来的只有苦恼。

A man past his prime who takes home a young wife and cannot sleep for jealousy — this is a cause of downfall.

The observation is psychologically precise: the mismatch between an aging man's desire and his reality generates jealousy, anxiety, and sleeplessness rather than happiness. The teaching is not moralistic condemnation but practical observation: pursuing desires that generate more suffering than satisfaction is its own form of downfall, regardless of social convention.

将权力交给耽于酒食的女人或男人——此为败亡之因。

将财产或权力托付给沉湎于酒食、挥霍无度的人——无论男女——是将自己的资源置于不负责任之手，必然带来损失。

Placing a woman or man addicted to drink and squandering in authority — this is a cause of downfall.

Delegation of responsibility to the wrong person — regardless of their gender — is identified as a cause of downfall. The criterion is not who the person is but what they do: addiction to drink and habitual squandering disqualify anyone from being trusted with authority or resources. Poor delegation of responsibility is a form of negligence that carries real consequences.

出身名门、志向远大，实际财力却微薄——此为败亡之因。

背景显赫、抱负宏大，实际财力却与之完全不相称——在此落差之中强行维持门面，必然入不敷出，走向败亡。

Of noble birth, with great ambitions, but of meagre means — this is a cause of downfall.

The final specific cause is the gap between aspiration and capacity. A person who believes their status requires expensive living — maintaining appearances, pursuing ambitions — but lacks the resources to sustain it will inevitably fall into debt, deception, or desperation. The teaching here is about realism: align one's way of living with one's actual means, not with one's self-image.

这些便是世间败亡之因；有智慧的圣者了知这些，走向有福之界。

列举完败亡的种种原因之后，经文以积极的结语收束：了知这些的智者，正是因为这种了知，能够走向繁荣与有福之境。知道什么导致败亡，便知道如何避免它。

These are the causes of downfall in the world. The wise one, knowing them, endowed with noble insight, goes to a fortunate realm.

The sutta closes not with condemnation but with the practical value of understanding. Knowing the causes of downfall is itself protective — the person who has clearly seen what leads to ruin can orient their life away from it. 'Noble insight' is the quality that converts this list from a catalogue of failures into a map for navigating life wisely.

总结 · Summary

败亡经是《经集》中最具世俗伦理色彩的经典之一。一位天神在深夜问佛：何为败亡之人？世尊以十二条具体而实际的原因作答，涵盖了交友、怠惰、不孝、欺骗、贪财、骄傲、放纵、嫉妒、识人不明与量力而行等各个层面。这些都不是抽象的罪名，而是有据可查的生活模式——每一条都描述了一种让人走向衰落的具体行为。经文的基调不是惩罚性的，而是诊断性的：了解败亡的原因，本身就是走向繁荣的开始。

The Parābhava Sutta is a remarkably practical text — a diagnostic inventory of the behaviours that lead to personal ruin. Its scope is both social (neglecting parents, deceiving monastics, hoarding wealth, choosing bad companions) and personal (laziness, jealousy, addiction, poor delegation). What makes the list coherent is not moralism but observation: each behaviour named is genuinely self-destructive, generating consequences that compound over time. The sutta's framing — as a night-time conversation with a deity — gives it a timeless register, but its contents are grounded in the everyday realities of ancient household life. The closing verse is important: this is not a list designed to shame but to enlighten. The wise person who understands these patterns can navigate around them.
