第四品·第七经 · 帝须弥勒经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第四品·第六经 目录 Contents 第四品·第八经 →

以下经文据汉译《义足经》（T0198，支谦译，三国·吴）

【帝须弥勒问】 何者为危殆？愿尊者为说—— 独处修梵行，舍弃欲染者，何事最危殆？ 【世尊答】 若人修梵行，复著于婬欲—— 此人为不善，如御者失辔。 前行已放弃，后退又复来—— 此愚痴之人，此名为卑劣。 昔所有名誉，今皆尽消失—— 见此当精勤，莫复堕于欲。 为欲所压覆，思想多散乱—— 此人以为耻，自知行不善。 修梵行之人，若为欲所胜—— 众人皆轻蔑，此名为堕落。 是故当精勤，不著婬欲染—— 断欲修梵行，此名为清净。 空旷处独居，意定修禅定—— 若能如是行，不为欲所胜。 修行者当知，此危殆最重—— 须弥勒，知此，修清净梵行。

逐句解释

若人修梵行，复著于婬欲——此人为不善，如御者失辔。前行已放弃，后退又复来——此愚痴之人，此名为卑劣。

帝须弥勒经（Tissametteyya Sutta）是八偈品中最简短的经典之一，主题集中：对发愿独身修梵行（brahmacariyā，清净行，指出家修行）的人而言，最大的危险是什么？答案是：重堕欲染（婬欲）。「如御者失辔」——车夫失去了对马的控制，这是对内心失控的经典比喻。「前行已放弃，后退又复来」：已经放弃了世俗的欲望生活，又退回到欲望中，这种进退失据使修行者陷入最深的困境。

If a person practises the holy life but returns to sensual desire — this person is unwholesome, like a charioteer who has lost the reins. Having abandoned what came before, then retreating back again — this foolish person is called degraded.

Tissametteyya asks what is most dangerous for a celibate practitioner; the answer is sexual entanglement. The 'lost reins' simile describes the loss of self-governance: the charioteer who cannot control the horses is in greater danger than one who never mounted. The double failure of someone who renounces worldly life and then reverts is worse than never renouncing — they have abandoned the householder's legitimate satisfactions while being unable to sustain the renunciant's freedom. The sutta is uncompromising about this structural danger without moralising about desire itself.

昔所有名誉，今皆尽消失——见此当精勤，莫复堕于欲。空旷处独居，意定修禅定——若能如是行，不为欲所胜。

「昔所有名誉，今皆尽消失」——一个已放弃世俗而修梵行的人，如果重堕欲染，其精神上的名誉（作为修行者的尊严与信用）随之消失。经文的实践建议是：「空旷处独居，意定修禅定」——远离人群（远离接触的机会），在禅定中安住（有了内在的定力，欲望的冲击就减弱了）。这是实践层面的处方：不是通过意志力强行压制欲望，而是通过环境（独居）与内心（禅定）的双重支持来维护梵行。

Whatever reputation was had before has now entirely disappeared — seeing this, one should be diligent and not fall back into desire. Living alone in an open place, with a concentrated mind practising meditation — if one can do this, one will not be overcome by desire.

The practical prescription is twofold: physical seclusion (reducing the opportunities for contact that trigger desire) and mental concentration (samādhi as the positive condition that makes desire lose its grip). The loss of spiritual reputation is presented not as social shame but as a genuine description of what happens internally: the practitioner who reverts loses not just others' regard but the coherence of their own practice — the accumulated momentum of renunciation is dissipated. The remedy is structural: conditions that support the holy life rather than will-power directed against desire.

总结 · Summary

帝须弥勒经是八偈品中最简明的经典之一，以一问一答的结构呈现修梵行者最大的危险：重堕欲染。「如御者失辔」是核心比喻——已放弃世俗欲乐而修清净行的人，如果退回到欲染，其失控比从未出发的人更为深重，因为他既失去了世俗的满足，也失去了修行的清净。经文的对治是实践性的：独居僻静处，修习禅定——以环境与内心的双重支持来维护梵行，而非仅凭意志力。

The Tissametteyya Sutta is the Aṭṭhakavagga's most concise text. Its single question — what is most dangerous for a celibate practitioner? — receives a direct answer: the return to sensual desire after renunciation. The 'lost reins' simile captures the specific quality of this failure: greater than never starting. The remedy is structural — solitary dwelling and meditation — framing the preservation of the holy life as a matter of supportive conditions rather than heroic will. The sutta's brevity and directness make it one of the canon's clearest statements on the practical management of renunciant life.
