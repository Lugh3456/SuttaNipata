第二品·第七经 · 梵法经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第二品·第六经 目录 Contents 第二品·第八经 →

古代的婆罗门——他们是梵法的奉行者——他们的积累是什么？他们对感官的节制是什么？ 他们记住并保护了他们自己的界限，他们的财富是真理——我将告诉你他们的梵法，如果你问我的话。 古代婆罗门不喜爱牛，也不喜爱黄金与谷物——他们的财富是研究、护持、慈心——这些是最高的宝藏。 在门口预备好的食物，他们认为是极好的——各种各样的食物被带来服侍到来的人。 婆罗门从全国各地聚集——他们向婆罗门提供他们的女儿——这样在婆罗门与婆罗门之间和睦相处。 那时他们不借助于其他原因，他们不是因为价格而买卖，他们的婚姻是由双方同意，而不是钱财购买。 古代婆罗门守护真理，他们是梵行者，是受尊敬的，不超越界限，护持梵行。 这些婆罗门的财富是这些，在这个世界中，他们是德行高尚的，因此他们是真正值得礼敬的，他们受到人们的尊敬。 但是随着时间的推移，他们看见了国王们的财宝——美丽的女人、华丽的车乘与装饰——他们的心被这些愿望所占据。 从那时起，他们渴望财富，撰写新的咒语书，造成了从前不存在的礼仪；他们造了梵咒来增加财富。 用这样的咒术，婆罗门向国王请求财物——国王们被他们的贪婪所打动，给予了丰厚的财宝。 国王们变得更加贪婪，受到婆罗门的请求——为了争夺财富，他们在他们的国家发动了战争。 牛被杀死了，母亲死于悲痛——神祇们因此离开了——那些古代梵法的守护者。 国王与婆罗门背离了梵法——在梵法的堕落中，人们遭受了苦难。 吃好的、玩乐、享受丰厚财富——人们由此走向罪恶、邪见与短命。 邪念由此在人们中蔓延——生命变短，肤色黯淡——这是梵法堕落的结果。

逐句解释

古代的婆罗门——他们是梵法的奉行者——他们的财富是研究、护持、慈心——这些是最高的宝藏。

梵法经以一种罕见的历史叙事为框架：佛陀追溯婆罗门传统从黄金时代到堕落的历史。在古代，婆罗门的财富（dhana）不是金钱与牛群，而是三种内在的资产：研究（佛陀时代的吠陀研习）、护持（对传统与品格的守护）、慈心（metta，对众生的友善）。这是对婆罗门传统的一种批判性内部叙述——佛陀使用婆罗门自己的历史来揭示他们已经偏离了自己的原初理想。

The ancient brahmins — their wealth was study, protection, lovingkindness — these were the highest treasure.

The Brahmadhammika Sutta is unusual in the Sutta Nipata: it is a historical narrative rather than a dialogue or a direct teaching. The Buddha traces the brahmin tradition from a golden age through gradual corruption. In the ancient period, the brahmins' wealth (dhana) consisted not of cattle, gold, or grain, but of internal goods: learning (the study of Vedic knowledge), protection (guarding the tradition and one's own integrity), and lovingkindness (metta — the disposition of goodwill toward all beings). This is an internal critique: the Buddha uses the brahmin tradition's own history to show that contemporary brahmins have drifted from their original values.

古代婆罗门守护真理，他们是梵行者，是受尊敬的，不超越界限，护持梵行。

古代婆罗门的理想图像：守护真理（sacca，诚实与真实）、梵行者（brahmacārī，清净的生活）、受尊敬（sakkato，被人们尊重）、不超越界限（sīmātikkama-rahito，不越界）、护持梵行（brahmacariya-pālaka）。这些品质与佛陀弟子的修行美德高度重叠——梵法经隐含地建议：佛陀的僧团才是古代梵法的真正继承者，而不是那些已堕落为贪婪礼仪服务者的当代婆罗门。

The ancient brahmins guarded truth, were practitioners of the holy life, respected, not transgressing boundaries, protecting the holy life.

The portrait of the ideal ancient brahmin closely mirrors the Buddha's description of the ideal monastic disciple — truth, chastity, respect-worthiness, restraint, protection of the holy life. This parallel is not accidental. The Brahmadhammika Sutta implicitly argues that the Buddha's community has recovered what the brahmins have lost. The brahmin tradition began with exactly the values that the Dhamma teaches; it is contemporary brahminism that has deviated, not the Dhamma that is novel. The critique is conducted from within the tradition's own terms.

但是随着时间的推移，他们看见了国王们的财宝——美丽的女人、华丽的车乘——他们的心被这些愿望所占据。

堕落的起点：婆罗门的眼睛转向了王室的财富与奢华。「他们的心被这些愿望所占据」（taṃ disvā taṇhā paccagā，看见了，渴爱回来了）——这正是缘起论中「触→受→渴爱→执取」的序列在历史层面的展现。一个文明的道德堕落与一个个人的心理堕落，遵循着相同的机制：看见了令人渴望的东西，渴爱生起，此后便是一系列的道德妥协。

But in time, they saw the treasures of kings — beautiful women, ornate carriages — and craving arose in their hearts.

The moment of corruption follows the logic of dependent origination: seeing (contact) produces feeling (attraction), which produces craving (taṇhā). This is not merely a moral fable but a psychological account — taṇhā paccagā, 'craving arose again,' describes the reactivation of desire that had been suppressed or absent. The brahmin tradition's decline follows the same sequence that individual minds follow: encounter with desirable objects, the arising of craving, and then the progressive rationalisation of increasingly compromised choices. A civilisation falls the same way a person falls.

用这样的咒术，婆罗门向国王请求财物——国王们被他们的贪婪所打动，给予了丰厚的财宝——国王们变得更加贪婪，为了争夺财富，他们在国家发动了战争。

腐败的循环：婆罗门制造新的仪式咒术（从前不存在的）来合法化向国王索取财物；国王受到婆罗门认可的激励而给予；获得财物的婆罗门又鼓励国王更多地积累，以便能够举行更大的祭典；国王之间为财富竞争，最终爆发战争。这是一个道德腐败的系统性图示：原初的简朴被欲望取代，欲望产生新的制度与仪式来满足自身，最终导致社会暴力。

Using such spells, the brahmins requested wealth from kings — the kings, moved by their own greed, gave abundantly — and kings, becoming yet more greedy, warred for wealth across their lands.

The cycle of corruption is described systemically: brahmins create new rituals (novel, previously non-existent) to provide religious sanction for royal accumulation; kings give because giving to brahmins generates merit and legitimacy; the brahmins' needs grow with their receipts; kings compete for the resources to meet brahminical demand; competition becomes warfare. The sutta traces a clear causal chain from the initial deviation of brahmin values through to social violence — a critique of how religious institutions can become vehicles for legitimising exactly the greed they were established to restrain.

牛被杀死了，母亲死于悲痛——神祇们因此离开了——那些古代梵法的守护者。

牛的屠杀是梵法经中最有张力的意象。牛在吠陀传统中是神圣的，被称为「供给者」、「无害的」、「慈母」——屠杀牛（用于日益奢华的祭典）是对吠陀原初价值的直接背叛。「母亲死于悲痛」：牛被比喻为母亲，其死引发悲伤，这是对暴力的情感化呈现。「神祇们因此离开了」：当梵法的守护被抛弃，神祇也随之离去——仪式的外壳留了下来，但内在的神圣性已消失。

Cattle were slaughtered, mothers died in grief — the gods therefore departed — those who had guarded the ancient brahmin Dhamma.

The slaughter of cattle carries particular weight in the Vedic context: cows were sacred, described as 'givers,' 'harmless,' 'mother-like.' Their killing in increasingly elaborate sacrificial rites was the most visible symbol of the tradition's self-betrayal. 'Mothers died in grief' applies the maternal image emotionally: the cows as mothers, their deaths as a grief-inducing violence. 'The gods departed' — when the inner spirit of the tradition was abandoned, the sacred presence withdrawn. The external forms remained; the gods (symbolic of the tradition's integrity) left. The critique of ritual without inner content is one of the Buddha's most persistent themes.

总结 · Summary

梵法经是经集中少数以历史叙事为框架的经典之一。佛陀追溯婆罗门传统的兴衰：在黄金时代，婆罗门的财富是研究、护持与慈心，他们守护真理、过清净的生活；随着时间推移，他们看见了王室的财富，渴爱生起，他们开始制造新的咒术仪式来换取财物，国王们被鼓励积累财富，最终国家之间爆发战争，牛被大量屠杀，神祇离去，古代梵法的精神荡然无存，人类走向短命与苦难。这是对宗教制度腐败的系统性批判，同时也是一个隐含的论证：佛陀的僧团才是古代梵法精神的真正继承者，因为他们所坚守的——真理、清净生活、慈心——正是古代婆罗门所拥有，而当代婆罗门已经失去的。

The Brahmadhammika Sutta is a historical narrative — rare in the Sutta Nipata — tracing the rise and fall of the brahmin tradition. In the golden age, brahmins' wealth consisted of learning, integrity, and lovingkindness; they guarded truth and lived the holy life. Gradually, contact with royal wealth activated craving; they began composing new ritual prescriptions to legitimise the extraction of wealth from kings; kings were encouraged to accumulate, competed for resources, and went to war; cattle were slaughtered in elaborate rites; the ancient spirit departed. The sutta is a systematic critique of how religious institutions can become vehicles for the very greed they were established to counter — and an implicit argument that the Buddha's community has recovered what brahminism lost: the actual inner content of the ancient brahmin Dhamma.
