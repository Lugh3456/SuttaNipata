第二品·第十一经 · 罗睺罗经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第二品·第十经 目录 Contents 第二品·第十二经 →

你是否经常与智者同住？罗睺罗，你满足了吗？你是否经常坐近世尊——诸天所尊崇的导师？ 【罗睺罗】我经常与智者同住，我满足了。我经常坐近世尊——诸天所尊崇的导师。 【世尊】舍弃五欲，以正念出家，舍弃欲乐，见到苦的止息。 不与朋友交往贪欲之事，亦不与世俗的人来往——成为不放逸的比丘，不追求任何不实之事。 不依赖形色，不依赖气味，不依赖味道，不依赖接触，不依赖心识——对一切不生执着，以正念进行游行。 你对这世间的任何事物，不应当生起渴望——以正念觉知，游历于世。 觉知诸行是无常，对无常的觉知应修习——以此破除骄慢，以此终结苦的。 这样行走的比丘，以离欲达到平静，超越欲望的纠缠，在此处得到彻底的寂灭。

逐句解释

你是否经常与智者同住？罗睺罗，你满足了吗？你是否经常坐近世尊——诸天所尊崇的导师？

罗睺罗经是佛陀对自己亲生儿子的教导，这在经集中是独一无二的。罗睺罗（Rāhula）在七岁时出家，是最年轻的比丘之一，被称为「欢喜学习者」（sikkhākāma）。问句的形式（「你满足了吗？」）不是寻常的问候，而是对罗睺罗修行状态的真实关切：与智者同住、亲近导师，是最幸运的修行条件——佛陀在询问他的儿子是否真正利用了这个极为稀有的机缘。

Do you dwell often with the wise? Are you content, Rāhula? Do you often sit near the Blessed One — the teacher revered by gods?

Rāhula was the Buddha's son, ordained at seven years old, known as the 'foremost lover of training' (sikkhākāma). This sutta is addressed to him directly — one of the very few instances in the Pali Canon where the Buddha teaches his own child. The opening question — 'are you content?' — is not pleasantry but a genuine inquiry into whether Rāhula is actually using the extraordinary conditions he has: proximity to the most accomplished teacher, daily access to wisdom. The question implies that conditions alone are not enough; they must be actively engaged.

舍弃五欲，以正念出家，舍弃欲乐，见到苦的止息。

「五欲」（pañca-kāmaguṇā）是色、声、香、味、触五种感官欲乐——这是出家生活的核心放弃。「以正念出家」（sato pabbaj）：出家不是逃避，而是以清醒的觉知选择另一条道路。「见到苦的止息」（dukkhassa antaṃ karohisi，做到苦的终结）：出家的目的是明确的——不是苦行本身，而是通过离欲的训练，真正见到苦如何终结。这四句话是整个出家理由的精简陈述。

Abandoning the five sense-pleasures, going forth with mindfulness, abandon sensual delight, see the end of suffering.

The five strands of sensual pleasure (pañca-kāmaguṇā) — forms, sounds, smells, tastes, and touches — are what the monastic life structurally withdraws from as a field of training. 'Going forth with mindfulness' specifies the quality of the renunciation: it is not a flight from complexity but a deliberate, clear-eyed choice to inhabit a simplified life in order to see more clearly. 'See the end of suffering' is the purpose — not the renunciation itself but what becomes visible when the distractions of sense-pleasure are reduced.

不依赖形色，不依赖气味，不依赖味道，不依赖接触，不依赖心识——对一切不生执着，以正念进行游行。

六处（āyatana）的系统性放弃：色（形色）、香（气味）、味（味道）、触（接触）、法（心识所缘的现象）——这五项加上前面隐含的声，构成六根六境系统。「不依赖」（anissita，无依）不是不接触感官对象，而是在接触时不产生执取依赖。「以正念游行」（sato anucarāhi）：正念在此是感官接触与执取之间的间隔——使接触发生，但使反应性的执取不发生。

Not dependent on forms, not dependent on smells, not dependent on tastes, not dependent on touches, not dependent on mind-objects — clinging to nothing, wander with mindfulness.

The five (with sound implied — six in total) sense-spheres are listed as potential sites of dependence. 'Not dependent' (anissita) does not mean sensory deprivation — monks see forms, hear sounds, smell fragrances. It means that contact with sense objects does not produce the clinging and leaning that make them into requirements. Mindfulness (sati) is the quality that creates the gap between contact and clinging: the practitioner is present with what arises without the reflex of grasping. The instruction is repeated for each sense-sphere to drive home its application to each moment of sensory experience.

觉知诸行是无常，对无常的觉知应修习——以此破除骄慢，以此终结苦。

无常（anicca）的修习是佛陀给罗睺罗的核心修行指导。「对无常的觉知应修习」（aniccasaññaṃ bhāvehi，修习无常想）：这不是一次性的洞见，而是需要持续培育的观察方式——反复地将觉知指向一切现象的生灭。「以此破除骄慢」：慢（māna）的根基是对「我是」的执取；观见一切现象无常，「我是」的幻象失去依托。「以此终结苦」：苦根植于对无常事物的执取；以无常想持续观察，执取失去支撑，苦的根源随之消退。

Develop the perception of impermanence — this destroys conceit, this ends suffering.

Aniccasaññā — the sustained cultivation of the perception of impermanence — is identified here as the central practice. Not a philosophical position but a perceptual training: repeatedly directing attention to the arising and passing of all phenomena. Its two results are named: the destruction of conceit (māna — which depends on a stable sense of 'I am something') and the ending of suffering (which depends on clinging to what is impermanent). When impermanence is continuously and directly perceived, the objects of conceit and clinging are seen as they are — passing, unstable, unable to provide the ground for a permanent self.

这样行走的比丘，以离欲达到平静，超越欲望的纠缠，在此处得到彻底的寂灭。

经文以果报作结：以这种方式修行的比丘（按照上述所有指示）以离欲（virāga，无欲、离染）达到平静（santa，寂静）；超越「欲望的纠缠」（kāma-saṃyojana，欲的结缚）；「在此处得到彻底的寂灭」（parinibbuto，般涅槃）。「在此处」强调这是当下可能的——不是死后的某种状态，而是在这个生命中，在这个身心中，可以实现的寂灭。这是佛陀给儿子的最终礼物：明确的道路，与其终点。

A monk walking thus, through dispassion reaches peace, crosses beyond the entanglements of desire, and is fully extinguished right here.

The conclusion describes the arc of the path in three movements: dispassion (virāga — the natural result of clearly perceiving impermanence, as craving loses its hold), peace (santa — the quality of mind that arises when craving is absent), and complete extinguishing (parinibbuta — the full cessation of the conditions for rebirth). 'Right here' (idheva) is important: this is not a posthumous reward but a present possibility. The Buddha's teaching to his son ends where all Buddhist teaching ends — Nibbāna accessible in this life, in this body, through this practice.

总结 · Summary

罗睺罗经是佛陀对自己儿子的直接教导，在经集中具有特殊的温情与份量。佛陀询问罗睺罗是否满足于修行生活，随后给予了简洁而完整的修行指导：舍弃五欲、以正念出家；对六处的感官接触不生执取依赖；培育无常想，以此破除骄慢、终结苦。这条路径的终点是离欲、平静与当下的寂灭。经文的深刻之处在于它将出家的理由（见苦的止息）、修行的核心（对感官的正念、无常观）与果报（此处的般涅槃），整合在一封父亲给儿子的短信中——简洁、温暖、毫不妥协。

The Rāhula Sutta is the Buddha's direct teaching to his own son — one of the most personally intimate texts in the Canon. After asking whether Rāhula is genuinely using the extraordinary conditions he has been given, the Buddha delivers a compact and complete set of instructions: abandon the five sense-pleasures; go forth with mindfulness; practise non-dependence on all six sense-spheres; cultivate the perception of impermanence to break conceit and end suffering. The path's outcome is dispassion, peace, and complete extinguishing right here in this life. The sutta combines the warmth of a father addressing a son with the uncompromising clarity of a teacher delivering the full teaching — nothing softened, nothing withheld.
