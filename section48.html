第四品·第十经 · 死前经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

以下经文据汉译《义足经》（T0198，支谦译，三国·吴）

【问】何者无贪欲？何者心平等？ 何者不染欲？何者名牟尼？ 【世尊答】 身坏命终前，已无贪欲者—— 不依于过去，不思量彼彼—— 此名无贪欲，心平等安稳。 彼心无愤恚，无慢无虚伪—— 无渴爱无嗔恚，不以慢行世—— 此名心平等。 彼无所依止，如莲不著水—— 于世间无取，不依彼此岸—— 此名不染欲。 彼心无动摇，不为苦所动—— 善恶皆不著，不忧不欢喜—— 此名为寂静。 于世无依止，不以我慢行—— 诸有为中见，不著于任何—— 此名为牟尼，如是牟尼道。 彼不执於见，不以戒为胜—— 彼渡彼岸已，不复还此岸—— 此身最後有，不復生死来。

逐句解释

身坏命终前，已无贪欲者——不依于过去，不思量彼彼——此名无贪欲，心平等安稳。

死前经（Purābheda Sutta，purā = 在……之前，bheda = 分解/破坏）的主题是：在死亡发生（「身坏命终」）之前，一个人应当处于什么状态？「已无贪欲者」——在死之前就已经没有贪欲；「不依于过去」——不执取已经过去的记忆与经验；「不思量彼彼」——不在心中反复权衡、筹划、担忧。这描绘的是一种当下的完整性：不是靠压制，而是贪欲在生命中就已自然消失——死亡不是突然的考验，而是对一种已经建立的内在状态的自然延伸。

Before the body breaks apart at death, one who already has no craving — not leaning on the past, not calculating this and that — this is called having no craving, a mind that is equal and secure.

The Purābheda Sutta asks what the liberated person's inner life looks like before death — not as a deathbed achievement but as a condition already established in life. 'Before the body breaks' frames the teaching: genuine liberation is not an emergency response to dying but the quality of a life already lived without grasping. 'Not leaning on the past' (atītaṃ nānvāgameyya) is non-nostalgia — not replaying and clinging to what has already passed. 'Not calculating this and that' is non-anticipation — not the anxious rehearsal of futures that have not arrived.

彼心无愤恚，无慢无虚伪——无渴爱无嗔恚，不以慢行世——此名心平等。彼无所依止，如莲不著水——于世间无取，不依彼此岸——此名不染欲。

平等心（upekhā）的具体描述：无愤恚（无嗔恚）、无慢（māna，不以傲慢行于世）、无虚伪（māyā，不伪装）、无渴爱、无嗔恚——这六者的消失，就是「心平等」的内容。「如莲不著水」：莲花从水中生长，却不被水浸湿——这是佛教文学中描述「处世而不染世」最经典的意象之一，也是「不染欲」的最优美表达。莲花不是在远离水，而是在水中保持自己的本性——圣者也是如此：处于世间，但不被世间的渴爱所沾染。

A mind without anger, without conceit, without deceit — without craving or aversion, not going through the world with conceit — this is called equanimity. One who has no resting-place, like a lotus unsoiled by water — taking nothing in the world, not dependent on this shore or the far shore — this is called unstained by desire.

The lotus simile for non-staining is one of the Pali Canon's most resonant images: the lotus grows from mud through water to flower in the air, but the water does not cling to its petals. The sage lives in the world — in contact with it, not withdrawn from it — but the world's craving-and-aversion structure does not find purchase. The six qualities of equanimity (no anger, no conceit, no deceit, no craving, no aversion, no arrogant movement through the world) give the abstract term concrete psychological content.

彼不执於见，不以戒为胜——彼渡彼岸已，不复还此岸——此身最後有，不復生死来。

死前经以「最後有」（pacchimabhava，最后一生）作结：「不执于见，不以戒为胜」——再次强调不以见解或戒律为执取的支柱；「渡彼岸已，不复还此岸」——已渡越轮回，不再回来；「此身最後有，不復生死来」——这是阿罗汉的标志之一，巴利文称为「parinibbutā，已般涅槃者」——不再生，不再死，这一生是最后一次在轮回中现身。这个结尾将死前经定性为对阿罗汉状态（在生时已解脱，死时为「般涅槃」）的描述。

Not clinging to views, not taking precepts as superior — having already crossed to the far shore, not returning to this shore — this body is the last existence; birth and death come no more.

The closing verses name the arahant's characteristic final mark: 'last existence' (pacchimabhava) — this is the final arising in conditioned existence; there is no further becoming. 'Not returning to this shore' (na pacceti oghavattaṃ) is one of the Pali Canon's standard descriptions of full liberation: the arahant has crossed the flood of conditioned existence and will not be swept back. Not clinging to views or taking precepts as superior — repeating the Aṭṭhakavagga's central theme — confirms that even the practices that brought one to the far shore are not held as possessions or sources of superiority.

总结 · Summary

死前经描绘的是「在身坏命终之前」就已经建立的解脱状态，而非临终的紧急准备。阿罗汉的特质被逐一呈现：无贪欲（不依过去、不思量将来）、心平等（无愤恚、无慢、无虚伪、无渴爱）、不染欲（如莲不著水，处世而不染）、于世无依止（不执见、不执戒为胜）。经文以「最後有，不復生死来」收束，点明这是对阿罗汉最后一生的描述——已渡彼岸，不复轮回。死前经是八偈品中最完整地呈现阿罗汉内在状态的经典，以「在死之前已经如此」的框架，揭示解脱不是对死亡的准备，而是对生命的彻底转化。

The Purābheda Sutta describes liberation as a condition already established before death, not a deathbed achievement. The arahant's qualities are enumerated: no craving (neither nostalgic nor anticipatory), equanimity (free from anger, conceit, deceit, craving, aversion), unsoiled by desire (the lotus image — present in the world, untouched by its clinging-structure), no resting-place (no dependence on views or precepts as superior), and the mark of final liberation — 'last existence, birth and death come no more.' The sutta's frame — 'before the body breaks' — conveys that genuine liberation is not preparation for dying but the quality of a life that has already released what death could otherwise be feared to take away.
