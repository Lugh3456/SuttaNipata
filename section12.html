第一品·第十二经 · 牟尼经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第一品·第十一经 目录 Contents 第二品·第一经 →

亲密生忧患，同住生尘垢；无亲密、无同住——此乃牟尼所见。 已生之物若被截断，不再播种，不令其生长——彼被称为独行的牟尼；伟大的圣者已见到寂静之地。 探索了根基，消灭了种子，不再滋养它——彼为真正的牟尼；已见到不动之地，越过了推论，不落于任何推论之数。 了知了一切住所，不渴求任何住所——彼真实为牟尼；无贪无取，不行诡道，已抵达彼岸。 胜一切、知一切，于诸法中有智慧，不被一切所染，已放下一切，在渴爱止息中解脱——智者称之为牟尼。 具有智慧之力，具有戒德与誓愿，专注、禅定、乐于禅思、不执著、无所漏——智者称之为牟尼。 独行的牟尼、不放逸的牟尼、不被毁誉所动的牟尼——如狮子不惊于声音，如风不被网所缚，如莲花不为水所染——引导他人，而非被他人引导——智者称之为牟尼。 在他人谈论时，若有立竿见影之言——在那些人中已处于最前位、如平静之湖——不傲慢的牟尼，智者称之为牟尼。 彼漫游之处，无有不平与不正；越过了疑惑，远离了苦、乐——智者称之为牟尼。 克制自己，对轮回中的众生无有伤害，渡过了洪流，守护自己、安住于禅定而不摇摆——智者称之为牟尼。 如对岸之雨鸟，一如既往地止于此——从一切漏中解脱，无有执取——智者称之为牟尼。 强健如良驹，放弃了轻慢与傲慢，征服了生死的轮回——圆满修习者——智者称之为牟尼。 如实了知、超越于世间中者——越过了苦、乐之巅——有智慧、解脱了、无有结使——智者称之为牟尼。 见到了轮回的灭尽，洗净了一切诸漏，清净、无染、无有结使——智者称之为牟尼。 知名、知处、远离了生灭，越过了泥泞，渡过了淤泥，站立于正道——智者称之为牟尼。

逐句解释

亲密生忧患，同住生尘垢；无亲密、无同住——此乃牟尼所见。

牟尼经以一个简明的观察开篇：亲密的关系带来忧患（因为爱恋带来失去的恐惧），同住带来尘垢（因为接触带来烦恼的增殖）。真正的牟尼看见了这一点，因此不以强烈的亲密或长期的同住来维系关系。这不是冷漠，而是清醒的观察。

From intimacy, anxiety is born; from living together, dust arises. Without intimacy, without living together — this is what the sage has seen.

The sutta opens with the same observation that drives the Rhinoceros Horn Sutta: intimacy generates anxiety (the fear of loss), and close living generates the friction of accumulated grievances and dependencies. The sage does not deny these as human goods; they simply see what comes with them. This seeing is the beginning of a different relationship with the world — present, caring, but not entangled.

已生之物若被截断，不再播种，不令其生长——彼被称为独行的牟尼；伟大的圣者已见到寂静之地。

「已生之物」指的是已经生起的烦恼、渴爱与轮回的种子；「截断」则是将其根除而非压制。不再播种新的种子，不再滋养已有的种子——这样的人是真正的独行牟尼，因为他已见到寂静（涅槃）之地。

Whatever has arisen — cut off; not sowing, not letting it grow — one is called a sage who wanders alone. The great sage has seen the place of peace.

The agricultural metaphor is precise: what has already arisen (past conditioning) is cut off; no new seeds of craving are sown; the existing ground is not nourished. This three-part cessation — stopping the past, not generating the future, starving the present — describes the complete cessation of the cycle. The 'place of peace' is Nibbāna — not a location but a quality of being.

了知了一切住所，不渴求任何住所——彼真实为牟尼；无贪无取，不行诡道，已抵达彼岸。

「住所」（āyatana）指的是六处、或存有的各种依止。牟尼了解一切存有的形式，却对任何一种都不渴求——无贪、不执取、不走弯路——已抵达彼岸（涅槃）。知而不执，是智慧的完整定义。

Having understood all stations, not craving any station — truly a sage; without greed, without grasping, not practising deception, having reached the far shore.

The 'stations' (āyatana) refer to the sense bases and the various realms of existence. Understanding them fully — knowing their nature, their arising, their cessation — but craving none of them: this is the sage's complete orientation. Knowledge without craving is wisdom; craving without knowledge is bondage. The far shore, Nibbāna, is reached by this combination.

胜一切、知一切、于诸法中有智慧、不被一切所染、已放下一切——在渴爱止息中解脱。

五种胜者的特质：胜一切（征服了一切障碍）、知一切（了知一切法的实相）、于诸法中有智慧（不仅知道，而且有洞见）、不被一切所染（如莲花不为泥水所染）、放下一切（无有执取）。在渴爱的止息中，彻底解脱。

Victorious over all, knowing all, wise in all phenomena, undefiled by all, having relinquished all — freed in the cessation of craving.

Five qualities of the fully liberated sage, each using 'all' (sabba) to emphasise the totality of the achievement. Nothing is held back, nothing is left contaminated, nothing is retained. The cessation of craving (taṇhā) is the mechanism of liberation throughout the Buddha's teaching — not a single dramatic moment but the progressive and finally complete stilling of the reaching impulse that drives rebirth.

独行的牟尼——如狮子不惊于声音，如风不被网所缚，如莲花不为水所染——引导他人，而非被他人引导。

三个已在《犀角经》中出现过的意象——狮子、风、莲花——在此汇聚，定义了真正的牟尼：不受外界干扰（狮子），不被任何东西捆绑（风），不被世间污染（莲花）。他引导他人，而非被他人所引导——这是精神独立性的核心标志。

The solitary sage — like a lion not startled by sounds, like the wind not caught in a net, like a lotus not stained by water — leading others, not led by others.

The three images from the Rhinoceros Horn Sutta reappear here, now applied not to the case for solitude but to the character of the sage. What these images share is non-reactivity: the lion is not startled, the wind is not trapped, the lotus is not contaminated. The sage who embodies these qualities is naturally the one who guides others — because their direction comes from within, not from external pressure or social conformity.

克制自己，对轮回中的众生无有伤害，渡过了洪流，守护自己、安住于禅定而不摇摆——智者称之为牟尼。

四种品质：自我克制（不被冲动所驱使）、对一切轮回众生无有伤害（慈悲的根本）、已渡过洪流（四种烦恼洪流）、安住于禅定而不摇摆（心的稳定性）。这四者共同刻画出一位在行动与禅思两方面都圆满的牟尼形象。

Restrained, not harming any beings in the round of rebirth, having crossed the flood, guarding oneself, dwelling in meditation without wavering — the wise call such a one a sage.

Four qualities that span both the ethical and meditative dimensions: restraint (the self-governing quality), non-harm toward all beings in the round of rebirth (compassion extended across the full range of sentient life), crossing the flood (liberation from the four āsavas), and meditative stability. A sage who is compassionate but not stable, or stable but not compassionate, is incomplete. Both are required.

见到了轮回的灭尽，洗净了一切诸漏，清净、无染、无有结使——智者称之为牟尼。

这是对阿罗汉果的直接描述：见到了轮回（saṃsāra）灭尽的可能性，并使之实现；洗净了一切漏（烦恼污染）；清净、无染、无有结使（七结）。牟尼的最高定义，是彻底清净的解脱者。

Having seen the ending of the round of rebirth, having washed away all the taints, pure, undefiled, without fetters — the wise call such a one a sage.

The arahant is described by what has been removed: the round of rebirth (seen and ended), all taints (āsavas — the deep-seated pollutants of sense-desire, existence, and ignorance), and all fetters (saṃyojanas — the bonds that tie beings to the wheel of rebirth). What remains after these removals is not emptiness but purity — the natural luminosity of a mind thoroughly cleared of obscuration.

知名、知处、远离了生灭，越过了泥泞，渡过了淤泥，站立于正道——智者称之为牟尼。

最后一颂以旅行者的意象作结：牟尼知道道路的名称与位置，已超越了生灭的循环，越过了泥泞（烦恼的沼泽），渡过了淤泥（欲望的泥坑），站立于正道（八正道）之上。这是整部经文最具象的结语——修行是一段真实的旅程，而牟尼是已经抵达并站立于坚实之地的人。

Knowing designation and ground, having gone beyond arising and passing, gone beyond the bog, crossed the mire, standing on the path — the wise call such a one a sage.

The closing verse uses the imagery of a traveller who knows the terrain, has crossed the difficult ground, and now stands on firm footing. 'Knowing designation and ground' suggests both intellectual understanding (names, classifications) and direct experiential orientation (where one is). The bog (kappa) and mire (paṅka) are traditional images for the obstructions of craving and delusion. Standing on the path — the Noble Eightfold Path — is the stable ground from which the sage operates. The sutta ends not with transcendence but with footing.

总结 · Summary

牟尼经是《经集》第一品的最后一首，以十五颂勾勒出真正的「牟尼」（muṇi，沉默的圣者）的完整形象。经文从亲密关系带来忧患的观察出发，逐步展开：牟尼截断已生之物、不再播种、了知一切住所而不渴求、胜一切诸法而不染、在渴爱止息中解脱。反复出现的意象——狮子、风、莲花——再度汇聚，共同刻画一位不受外界扰动、不被任何事物系缚、不为世间所染的圣者形象。经文以「站立于正道」作结，这是第一品从蛇经到牟尼经所呈现的精神旅程的最终落脚点。

The Muni Sutta closes Vagga 1 with a portrait of the sage that draws together the themes of the entire chapter. Fifteen verses describe the muṇi — the silent one, the sage — from multiple angles: their freedom from the entanglements of intimacy, their cessation of the cycle of arising, their knowledge without craving, their equanimity in all conditions, their complete liberation. The lion, wind, and lotus images recur from the Rhinoceros Horn Sutta, now assembled as a composite portrait rather than a case for solitude. The final verse — 'standing on the path' — grounds the sage in the practical reality of the Noble Eightfold Path. The sage has not escaped the world; they have found their footing within it, on terrain that does not shift beneath them.
