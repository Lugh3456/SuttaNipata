第五品·第十四经 · 褒沙罗经

经集 · Sutta Nipata · 彼岸道品

← 第五品·第十三经 目录 Contents 第五品·第十五经 →

【褒沙罗问】 你了知过去，释迦族人—— 对没有形式的感知者—— 对「一切形式都已消失」的修行者—— 内部与外部—— 有疑问的人的疑问—— 你如何回应这个问题？ 【世尊答】 褒沙罗，如来了知一切感知的居所—— 了知「有存在者」—— 知道「无所有处」中有识—— 他知道那里的困难。 了知了「无所有处」—— 他以无所有处为喜悦，以之为家—— 他解脱于此。 了知这个，正念的比丘—— 以直接的知识知道它—— 他是真正了知的人—— 这个渡越对他来说是直接的。

逐句解释

对没有形式的感知者——对「一切形式都已消失」的修行者——你如何回应这个问题？

褒沙罗经（Posāla Sutta）的问题是彼岸道品中最技术性的禅定问题：「没有形式的感知者」指的是进入了「无色界」（arūpaloka）的禅定修行者——这些禅定者的感知中，物质形式（rūpa）已经消失，进入了无所有处（ākiñcaññāyatana）等无色禅境。褒沙罗的问题是：对于这样的修行者——「一切形式都已消失」的人——他们的「疑问」是什么？他们还缺少什么？这是一个专门针对深度禅定修行者的问题，询问无色禅境的局限与解脱的关系。

For one who has no-form perception — for a practitioner for whom 'all forms have vanished' — internally and externally — the question of one with a doubt — how do you respond to this question?

The Posāla Sutta poses the most technically specialised question in the Pārāyanavagga: what is the remaining problem for a meditator who has attained formless perception — specifically the sphere of nothingness (ākiñcaññāyatana), the seventh jhāna? Such a practitioner has gone beyond all form-perception; their experience is characterised by the absence of form. Posāla's question is: what is still unresolved for them? The question is aimed at advanced practitioners who may believe they have solved the problem through the formless attainments, identifying what remains.

如来了知一切感知的居所——了知「有存在者」——知道「无所有处」中有识——他知道那里的困难。了知了「无所有处」——他以无所有处为喜悦，以之为家——他解脱于此。

「如来了知一切感知的居所」：佛陀了知所有可能的感知状态，包括无色界禅定。「知道『无所有处』中有识」——关键洞见：即使在「无所有处」这个极度精微的禅定状态中，仍然有「识」（viññāṇa）存在——意识的持续是一种微细的「存在」（bhava）。「他知道那里的困难」：无所有处的困难是：禅定者可能以这个状态为「家」——以「无所有」的体验为执取对象，于是又生出一种微细的执取。「了知了『无所有处』——他以无所有处为喜悦，以之为家——他解脱于此」：这是对「识」的直接了知（而非仅仅进入该状态）导向解脱——了知识的存在与性质，不以任何禅境为最终家园。

The Tathāgata knows all the abodes of perception — knows 'those who have existence' — knows that in the sphere of nothingness there is consciousness — he knows the difficulty there. Knowing 'the sphere of nothingness' — one who takes delight in nothingness, makes it home — is liberated from it.

The critical insight is: even in the sphere of nothingness (ākiñcaññāyatana), the most refined of the formless attainments, consciousness (viññāṇa) persists — there is still a very subtle mode of existence (bhava). The 'difficulty' there is that the practitioner may settle into this attainment and mistake it for liberation, taking 'nothingness' itself as home and making it an object of subtle grasping. True liberation from this state comes not from dwelling in it but from knowing it — knowing that even here consciousness persists, even here there is a subtle 'someone who knows nothingness.' This direct knowledge of the nature of consciousness in the formless attainment cuts through the potential for mistaking refined meditative experience for awakening.

总结 · Summary

褒沙罗经是彼岸道品中最技术性的经典，针对深度禅定修行者。褒沙罗的问题：对于进入了「无所有处」（一切形式消失）的修行者，还有什么疑问？世尊的回答揭示：即使在最精微的无色禅境中，「识」仍然存在——这是那里的「困难」。真正的解脱来自对这个识的直接了知，而非以无所有处为「家」的执取。褒沙罗经对整部彼岸道品关于「无执取」的主题做出最精微的应用：即使是最高的禅定境界，也不应成为新的执取对象；解脱是了知，不是安住。

The Posāla Sutta is the Pārāyanavagga's most technically specialised dialogue, addressed to meditators in the formless attainments. The key teaching is: even in the sphere of nothingness — even where all form-perception has dissolved — consciousness persists, and this persistence is a subtle form of existence that can become an object of subtle grasping. The practitioner who settles into nothingness as home has merely shifted the location of grasping to the most refined available object. True liberation requires direct knowledge of what consciousness is doing even there, cutting through the potential confusion of refined attainment with awakening. The Posāla Sutta applies the Pārāyanavagga's central principle — non-grasping — to the most rarified meditation experience: no attainment, however refined, is immune from the possibility of becoming a dwelling-place for subtle craving.
