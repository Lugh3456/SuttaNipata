第二品·第八经 · 船经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第二品·第七经 目录 Contents 第二品·第九经 →

如果人渴求太多，渴求少于他值得的，他就不可能从别处得到——他应当带着学识接近贤者。 比丘应当放弃傲慢，以谦逊来到，知道什么时候适合——了解法与非法，用心聆听。 他应当去见那些值得见的，有学识、有戒律的人，解决疑惑，在他的提示下追求知识。 就像一艘船能运送人过河，有一位聪慧而博学的人，他在讲解方面有才能——他使那些接近他的人越过苦海。 因此，应当接近学识广博的人，如同接近智者——听从他的话语，履行梵行，了解真谛。 了解这一点的人，谦逊而智慧，渴求解脱——他了解了法的意义，就如旅人对陆地渡口的了解。

逐句解释

如果人渴求太多，渴求少于他值得的，他就不可能从别处得到——他应当带着学识接近贤者。

船经开篇讲述如何正确地接近善知识（kalyāṇamitta，善友）。「渴求太多」（atilābha-kāmo，贪求过多）或「渴求少于他值得的」——两种极端都会阻碍真正的学习：一个傲慢地期待太多，另一个则低估自己所能接受的。「带着学识接近」意味着带着准备好的心、对所受教导的认真态度，而非仅凭热情或习惯。接近善知识是一门艺术，需要恰当的姿态。

If a person desires too much, or desires less than he is worthy of, he cannot get it elsewhere — he should approach the wise with learning.

The Nāvā Sutta is a teaching on how to approach a spiritual teacher — a practical instruction in the relationship between student and guide. Both extremes of approach fail: demanding too much (setting conditions, expecting guaranteed results) or accepting too little (failing to pursue understanding to its depth). 'Approaching with learning' means coming prepared — not empty, not merely eager, but with the groundwork of prior reflection that makes a teaching receivable. The quality of reception determines the quality of transmission.

比丘应当放弃傲慢，以谦逊来到，知道什么时候适合——了解法与非法，用心聆听。

四个条件描述了向善知识请法的正确态度：放弃傲慢（māna，骄慢，执着于自己的见解与地位）；以谦逊来到（nipaca，低伏，这是巴利文中表示谦逊姿态的词）；知道什么时候适合（kāla-ññū，知时，知道何时适合提问、何时应当沉默）；用心聆听（sussūsanta，渴望听闻，主动的聆听，而非被动的在场）。这四个品质共同构成「可教性」——使自己成为真正可以接受教导的人。

The monk should abandon conceit, come humbly, knowing what is timely — understanding what is the Dhamma and what is not, listening with care.

Four qualities constitute the right orientation toward a teacher: abandoning conceit (not assuming prior knowledge trumps what is being offered); coming humbly (nipaca — the Pali word suggests a physical lowering, a posture of receptivity); knowing what is timely (kāla-ññū — when to ask, when to be silent, when to reflect rather than respond); and listening with earnest attention (sussūsanta — actively wanting to hear, not merely politely attending). These four together constitute 'teachability': the quality that makes transmission possible.

就像一艘船能运送人过河，有一位聪慧而博学的人，他在讲解方面有才能——他使那些接近他的人越过苦海。

船的比喻是经集中最简洁有力的比喻之一。善知识如同一艘能运送人渡过大河的船：渡过的不是普通的水，而是苦海（dukkha-ogha，苦的洪流）。「在讲解方面有才能」（desanākusalo）强调教导能力：不是所有有智慧的人都能将智慧传递给他人；真正的善知识既有所知，又有所教的能力。「使那些接近他的人越过」——结果取决于两方：善知识的能力，以及学生接近的正确方式。

As a boat can ferry people across a river, a wise and learned person, skilled in explanation — he brings those who approach him across the flood of suffering.

The boat simile gives the sutta its name. The image is simple and precise: the teacher carries the student across. The water is not the ocean of samsāra in the grandest sense but the dukkha-ogha — the flood of suffering that is the immediate experience of an unexamined life. The qualification 'skilled in explanation' (desanākusalo) is important: wisdom and communicative skill are distinct. A teacher who knows but cannot convey leaves students stranded at the bank. The sutta's implied argument is that seeking the right teacher, in the right way, is itself a critical act of practice.

了解这一点的人，谦逊而智慧，渴求解脱——他了解了法的意义，就如旅人对陆地渡口的了解。

最后一颂以第二个渡越比喻作结：旅人了解陆地渡口（titthaṃ，渡河处、渡口）的位置，才能安全渡过。同样，了解法义的人知道正确的「渡口」——修行的入手处与方向。谦逊（nipaka）与智慧（paññavā）在此并列：两者都是必需的——没有谦逊的智慧容易变为傲慢，没有智慧的谦逊容易流于盲从。「渴求解脱」是整个修行的动力源头，使谦逊与智慧得以有效运作。

One who knows this, humble and wise, desirous of liberation — he has understood the meaning of the Dhamma, as a traveller knows a ford on land.

The ford (tittha) image closes the sutta: a traveller who knows where the crossing is can traverse a river safely; without this knowledge, they wander the bank or plunge in at the wrong point. Understanding the Dhamma's meaning is knowing where the crossing is — the right entry point, the right direction, the destination. Humble (nipaka) and wise (paññavā) are paired as complementary qualities; neither alone is sufficient. The desire for liberation (vimutti-kāmo) is the motivating force that sets both qualities in productive motion. The three together — humility, wisdom, and the aspiration for freedom — constitute the ideal student of the Dhamma.

总结 · Summary

船经是小品（Cūḷavagga）中最短小的经典之一，但内容精练：它专注于一个具体的修行主题——如何正确地接近善知识。佛陀指出两个错误：要求太多（傲慢地期待保证）或接受太少（不充分地追求理解）。正确的接近方式是：放弃傲慢，以谦逊来到，知时适语，用心聆听，带着准备好的心与真正的渴望。具备这些品质的学生，遇到真正的善知识——那位能运送渡越的「船」，那位有讲解才能的老师——便能越过苦海，就如知道渡口位置的旅人能安全渡河。在修行的整个路径中，接近善知识是一个关键节点；而如何接近，与接近谁同样重要。

The Nāvā Sutta is among the shortest in the Cūḷavagga but tightly focused: it addresses a specific and practical question about the spiritual life — how does one approach a teacher rightly? Two failures are identified: demanding too much (approaching with pre-conditions or expectations of guaranteed results) and settling for too little (not pursuing understanding to its depth). The right approach combines the abandonment of conceit, genuine humility, a sense of timing, and earnest attention. A student equipped with these qualities, meeting a teacher who is both wise and skilled in explanation — the 'boat' of the title — can cross the flood of suffering. The ford simile closes the teaching: understanding the Dhamma is knowing where to cross; humility and wisdom together are what allow one to find and use that ford.
