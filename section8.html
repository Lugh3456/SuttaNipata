第一品·第八经 · 慈经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第一品·第七经 目录 Contents 第一品·第九经 →

善于利益之道、欲达寂静之境者，应当如此行持：能干、正直、极为正直，柔顺、温和、不骄慢。 知足、易于供养、少事少务，生活简朴、根门寂静、有智慧，不放逸、不贪着于家。 不做任何智者会谴责的微小过失，愿一切众生幸福安乐。 凡有生命者，无一例外——软弱或强壮，长或大，中或短，小或胖， 可见或不可见，住于近处或住于远处，已生或将生——愿一切众生无一例外皆幸福。 不要欺骗任何人，不要在任何地方轻视任何人；在任何状况下，不要以愤怒或恶意来使他人受苦。 如同母亲以生命保护她的独生子，对一切众生也应如此修习无边之心。 对一切世间，修习无边的慈爱之心——向上、向下、向旁，无有障碍、无有怨恨、无有敌意。 无论站立、行走、坐下，还是卧倒，在一切清醒的时刻，应当安住于此正念——此称为梵住。 不执取邪见，具有戒德，具足胜观，断除了对欲乐的渴求——此人必不再投生于胎中。

逐句解释

善于利益之道、欲达寂静之境者，应当如此行持：能干、正直、极为正直，柔顺、温和、不骄慢。

慈经以修行者应具备的个人品质开篇，而非直接从慈爱的修习说起。能干、正直（两次强调）、柔顺、温和、不骄慢——这是修习无边慈爱的土壤。没有这些个人品质作为基础，慈爱的修习只是空话。

One skilled in good, wishing to attain the state of peace, should act thus: capable, upright, and very upright, obedient, gentle, and not conceited.

The sutta begins not with love but with character. Before radiating loving-kindness outward, the practitioner must have cultivated the inner qualities that make such radiation genuine: capability (not helpless or vague), uprightness (double-emphasised — both in outer conduct and inner alignment), obedience to wise guidance, gentleness of manner, and freedom from conceit. These are the prerequisites, not add-ons.

知足、易于供养、少事少务，生活简朴、根门寂静、有智慧，不放逸、不贪着于家。

八种外在条件：知足（不贪求多余）、易于接受供养（不挑剔）、少事（不令自己和他人劳烦）、简朴生活、守护感官之门、有智慧、精进不懈、不贪恋家庭生活。这些条件共同构成了能够修习慈爱的外在环境。

Content, easily supported, with few duties, of simple livelihood, with calm senses, discreet, not impudent, not greedy among families.

Eight conditions for the outer life: contentment (not restless with what one has), easy to support (not demanding of one's benefactors), few duties (a spacious life, not crammed with obligations), simple livelihood, sense restraint, discretion in conduct, modesty, and non-greediness when visiting lay families. Each reduces the friction of daily life, freeing attention for what the sutta is about to describe.

不做任何智者会谴责的微小过失，愿一切众生幸福安乐。

「微小过失」的强调尤为重要：不是只避免大的道德失误，而是连智者会谴责的小过失也不犯。在这样的清净基础上，生起对一切众生的善愿——祝愿他们幸福与安乐。

Not doing even the slightest thing that the wise would censure — may all beings be happy and safe.

The pivot point of the sutta: the character requirements of the first two verses give way to the first expression of loving-kindness. 'Not even the slightest thing' sets a high bar — the wise are perceptive, and small violations of integrity matter. From this position of thorough moral cleanliness, the wish for all beings' happiness arises naturally, not as an external practice but as an expression of what the practitioner has become.

凡有生命者，无一例外——软弱或强壮，长或大，中或短，小或胖，可见或不可见，住于近处或住于远处，已生或将生——愿一切众生无一例外皆幸福。

这几颂以极为系统的方式，将慈爱的对象扩展至所有可能的众生类别：按体型（软弱/强壮、长/大、中/短、小/胖）、按可知性（可见/不可见）、按距离（近处/远处）、按时间（已生/将生）——无一例外，无一遗漏。

Whatever living beings there may be, without exception — weak or strong, long or large, medium, short, small, or fat, seen or unseen, those near and those far away, those born and those to be born — may all beings without exception be happy.

The enumeration is exhaustive by design. Every possible category of being is named to prevent any mental exclusion. The practitioner who lists every type — including beings not yet born, beings too small to see, beings living far away — is training the mind to hold 'all beings' not as an abstraction but as a genuine totality. No being is outside the field of loving-kindness. This is the 'boundless' (appamāṇa) quality that makes mettā one of the brahmavihāras — the divine abidings.

不要欺骗任何人，不要在任何地方轻视任何人；在任何状况下，不要以愤怒或恶意来使他人受苦。

慈爱在行为上的三个禁止：不欺骗、不轻视、不以愤怒或恶意伤害他人。这是慈爱从内心状态落实到日常言行的具体表现。真正的慈爱不会止步于良好的愿望，而会在每一个互动中体现出来。

Let no one deceive another; let no one despise any person anywhere; in anger or with ill will, let no one wish suffering on another.

After the vast opening of loving-kindness to all beings, the sutta brings it back to the immediate relational scale: individual interactions. The three prohibitions — no deception, no contempt, no ill will — are the behavioural expression of loving-kindness. You cannot genuinely wish for all beings to be happy while deceiving them, looking down on them, or wishing them harm when angry. The inner state and the outer conduct must align.

如同母亲以生命保护她的独生子，对一切众生也应如此修习无边之心。

这是整部慈经中最动人的意象：母亲对独生子的爱——最深、最无条件、甚至愿意以生命相护——就是我们对一切众生应当修习的慈爱之模板。这不是一种温和的「善意」，而是一种深切的、愿以身相护的爱。

Just as a mother would protect her only child with her own life, even so let one cultivate a boundless heart toward all beings.

The mother-child simile is the heart of the sutta. A mother's love for her only child is the most intense, unconditional, self-sacrificing love most people know. It is not abstract goodwill — it is fierce, protective, willing to give everything. This is the quality of heart the sutta asks us to extend to all beings without exception. Not watered-down sentiment, but the full force of the deepest love we know, offered without boundary.

对一切世间，修习无边的慈爱之心——向上、向下、向旁，无有障碍、无有怨恨、无有敌意。

慈爱的方向：向上（天界）、向下（地下界）、向四面八方——无所不及，无所阻碍，无有怨恨，无有敌意。这是「无边」（appamāṇa）的具体含义：没有任何空间或关系的例外。

Radiating loving-kindness toward the whole world — above, below, and across — without obstruction, without hatred, without enmity.

The three spatial directions — above, below, across — cover every possible location of every possible being. The three negatives — no obstruction, no hatred, no enmity — identify the three forms of resistance that limit loving-kindness: psychological blocks (obstruction), active ill will (hatred), and underlying animosity (enmity). All three must be absent for the radiation to be genuinely boundless.

无论站立、行走、坐下，还是卧倒，在一切清醒的时刻，应当安住于此正念——此称为梵住。

四种姿势涵盖了人的全部清醒时刻：站立、行走、坐下、卧倒。在所有这些时刻保持慈爱的正念，就是「梵住」（brahmavihāra，神圣的安住）。慈爱不是偶尔修习的功课，而是生命的基本底色。

Whether standing, walking, sitting, or lying down, as long as one is awake, one should develop this mindfulness. This is called a divine abiding here.

The four postures cover all waking experience — there is no posture excluded, no time of day when loving-kindness is off duty. 'Divine abiding' (brahmavihāra) is a significant designation: these are called divine because they are the states that devas (divine beings) are said to inhabit, and because they elevate the human practitioner to that quality of dwelling. Loving-kindness sustained across all four postures is not occasional practice but a way of being.

不执取邪见，具有戒德，具足胜观，断除了对欲乐的渴求——此人必不再投生于胎中。

慈经以解脱的标志作结：不执取邪见（邪见不缠缚心）、戒德具足、拥有深刻的洞见（胜观）、断除了对感官欲乐的渴求——这样的人不会再被带回轮回的胎中，已经走向了彻底的解脱。

Not holding to views, virtuous, endowed with vision, having removed desire for sensual pleasures — such a one is not reborn in the womb again.

The closing verse ties the mettā practice to liberation. It identifies four qualities of the one who is no longer bound to rebirth: freedom from fixed views (the flexibility that comes from genuine insight), virtue (ethical foundation), superior vision (direct seeing of the truth), and absence of sensual craving. These are the fruits of a life sustained by loving-kindness. The connection is not accidental: the person who genuinely holds all beings in boundless goodwill is progressively freed from the self-centred craving that binds them to the cycle of rebirth.

总结 · Summary

慈经是《经集》中流传最广、影响最深远的经典，至今仍是南传佛教中最常诵读的经文之一。它的结构清晰而完整：先以个人品质与简朴生活为基础，再以系统的方式将慈爱扩展至一切众生——无论体型大小、可见与否、远近已生未生，无一例外。母亲护子的意象，将无边慈爱化为人人都能理解的情感原型。经文的高潮是「梵住」：在站立、行走、坐下、卧倒的一切清醒时刻，都安住于这无边的慈爱之中。这不是一种偶尔修习的技法，而是一种彻底转化生命方向的修行——其最终的果实，是从胎中解脱、不再轮回。

The Metta Sutta is the most beloved and widely recited text in the Sutta Nipata, chanted daily in Theravāda temples across the world. Its structure is deceptively simple: establish the character and conditions for genuine practice, then open the heart to all beings without exception. The enumeration of beings — by size, visibility, proximity, and temporal existence — trains the mind against selective compassion. The mother-child simile brings the boundless back to the human scale: not abstract goodwill but the most intense love we know, extended without limit. The 'divine abiding' instruction — sustaining loving-kindness across all four postures, all waking hours — transforms mettā from a meditation technique into a way of being. The closing verse connects this practice directly to liberation: the heart that holds all beings in genuine loving-kindness is progressively freed from the self-centredness that drives the cycle of rebirth.
