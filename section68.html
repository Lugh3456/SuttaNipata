第五品·第十三经 · 优陀耶经

经集 · Sutta Nipata · 彼岸道品

← 第五品·第十二经 目录 Contents 第五品·第十四经 →

【优陀耶问】 我来问你，已禅修的人—— 坐在禅修中，已除灭渴爱， 已渡越了疑惑—— 解脱是通过知识的还是通过行为的？ 【世尊答】 优陀耶，放弃了欲乐， 两种——放弃了忧愁—— 克服了惰性与昏沉—— 阻止了悔恨。 以平静与正念的清净—— 以法的正思惟为先—— 我称之为知识的解脱， 无明的破碎。 【优陀耶问】 什么束缚了世间？ 什么是它的行走的方式？ 放弃什么——人们说——是涅槃？ 【世尊答】 以喜乐为束缚，世间束缚—— 以思惟为它的行走的方式—— 放弃渴爱——人们说——是涅槃。 【优陀耶问】 正念在什么之上行走—— 人们放弃了什么，放弃了感知？ 我来问一个问题—— 请为我解说无所有。 【世尊答】 放弃了对内部与外部的感知—— 不借助感知而知—— 正念的行走在这个中间—— 这就是无所有，就是渡越了苦。

逐句解释

解脱是通过知识的还是通过行为的？——以平静与正念的清净——以法的正思惟为先——我称之为知识的解脱，无明的破碎。

优陀耶经（Udaya Sutta）以一个哲学问题开始：解脱是通过知识（ñāṇa）还是通过行为（sīla）？这是印度宗教思想的一个古老争论。世尊的回答拒绝了这个二元对立：解脱的条件包括心理层面（放弃欲乐、克服惰性、阻止悔恨）与实践层面（平静、正念、清净），「以法的正思惟为先」——正思惟（paṭisaṅkhā）是智慧的运作。「知识的解脱」（ñāṇavimutti）：不是纯粹的行为也不是纯粹的知识，而是两者在修行中的整合——以清净的心理状态为基础，以正思惟的智慧作为破除无明的工具。

Liberation through knowledge or through conduct? — Through the purity of equanimity and mindfulness — with right reflection on the Dhamma as forerunner — I call this liberation through knowledge, the shattering of ignorance.

Udaya's question — knowledge or conduct? — poses one of Indian philosophy's oldest debates. The Buddha's answer refuses the dichotomy: liberation through knowledge (ñāṇavimutti) requires both the psychological preparation (abandoning pleasure-and-grief, overcoming sloth, stopping remorse) and the wisdom dimension (equanimity, mindfulness, right reflection on the Dhamma). Right reflection (paṭisaṅkhā) is not mere intellectual analysis but the active investigation of experience through the Dhamma lens. 'Shattering of ignorance' is the functional definition: knowledge-liberation is the moment when ignorance, which was the structural condition of all suffering, is broken apart by insight.

以喜乐为束缚，世间束缚——以思惟为它的行走的方式——放弃渴爱——人们说——是涅槃。

「以喜乐（nandī）为束缚，世间束缚」：「喜乐」这里不是一般意义的快乐，而是「对体验的喜悦与欢迎」——正是这种对体验的迎受与喜悦构成了世间的束缚。「以思惟（vitakka）为它的行走的方式」：世间的运作方式是通过思惟——概念化、分别、规划——维持其连续性的。「放弃渴爱——人们说——是涅槃」：这是对涅槃最简洁的功能性定义：不是一个地方，不是一种状态，而是「渴爱被放弃」这件事本身。

With delight as its fetter, the world is bound — with thought as its means of travelling — by abandoning craving — that, they say, is Nibbāna.

'Delight' (nandī) as the world's fetter is a precise diagnosis: it is not pleasure itself but the welcoming, the receiving-with-joy of experience that constitutes bondage. The world moves through thought (vitakka — the proliferating, planning, conceptualising activity of mind). 'By abandoning craving — that is Nibbāna' is one of the Pali Canon's most compressed functional definitions: Nibbāna is not a place or state reached after abandoning craving — it is the abandoning of craving itself. The cessation is the destination.

放弃了对内部与外部的感知——不借助感知而知——正念的行走在这个中间——这就是无所有，就是渡越了苦。

最后一个问题问的是「无所有」（ākiñcañña）——这是禅定中的「无所有处」，但在这里超越了禅定技术的含义。「放弃了对内部与外部的感知」：不执取内部感知（内在的感觉与心理状态），也不执取外部感知（对外物的感知）。「不借助感知而知」（neva saññā）：不以概念感知为认识工具——这描述的是超越概念化感知的直接了知状态。「正念的行走在这个中间」：在「内部与外部感知都放弃，但认知仍清明」的中间状态，以正念安住——这就是「无所有」，就是渡越了苦的状态。

Having abandoned both inner and outer perceptions — not knowing through perception — mindfulness walking in between — this is what is called 'having nothing,' the crossing of suffering.

'Having nothing' (ākiñcañña) here transcends the technical dhyāna meaning of the formless attainment and describes a quality of mindful presence that has released both internal perception (inner feeling-states and mental formations) and external perception (cognition of external objects) without losing clarity. 'Not knowing through perception' (neva saññā) — not using conceptual perception as the instrument of knowing — points to direct awareness prior to conceptualisation. 'Mindfulness walking in between' — the middle ground where grasping at neither inner nor outer perception is maintained through mindful awareness — is the functional description of liberation-in-presence. The Udaya Sutta closes the Pārāyanavagga's technical sequence with its most precise phenomenological description of liberated awareness.

总结 · Summary

优陀耶经是彼岸道品哲学最密集的经典之一，包含三个互相递进的问答：（1）解脱是通过知识还是行为？——两者整合，「以法的正思惟为先」的知识解脱；（2）什么束缚世间，什么是涅槃？——「喜乐」是束缚，「思惟」是世间的行走方式，「放弃渴爱」就是涅槃；（3）什么是「无所有」？——放弃内外感知，不借感知而知，正念在中间行走，这就是渡越了苦。优陀耶经是彼岸道品对解脱现象学的最精密描述，从心理准备到知识解脱到无所有的现象学，层层深入。

The Udaya Sutta is the most philosophically dense of the Pārāyanavagga's dialogues, moving through three progressive exchanges: (1) knowledge versus conduct as the route to liberation — answered by their integration in knowledge-liberation, with right reflection as the forerunner; (2) what binds the world and what is Nibbāna — delight binds, thought moves the world, abandoning craving is Nibbāna; (3) what is 'having nothing' — releasing both inner and outer perception, knowing without conceptual perception, with mindfulness in between. The final exchange is the Pārāyanavagga's most precise phenomenological description of liberated awareness: not the absence of awareness but awareness freed from conceptual-grasping, present in the middle ground between inner and outer, clear without being caught.
