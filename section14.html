第二品·第二经 · 腥臭经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

【提沙·迦叶波】米饭、米粥、豆类与蔬菜，以及合法获得的各种食物——你们吃这些，却不说谎。 提沙·迦叶波说：不吃荤腥，不用芝麻；这是婆罗门家的传统——如果你问，我会告诉你，没有荤腥的食物不会玷污他。 【世尊】鱼和肉——我不这样说是荤腥。各种精制食物、乌黑的芝麻、蜂蜜、鸡蛋——这些都不是荤腥。 杀生、打人、割割、束缚、偷盗、说谎、欺骗、巫术咒术、邪淫——这是荤腥，不是吃肉。 在这个世界上，贪求荤腥者，不节制欲望，贪著美味，信奉虚无，不公正——这是荤腥，不是吃肉。 粗野、固执、背叛、不悲悯、傲慢、吝啬——不施予朋友——这是荤腥，不是吃肉。 愤怒、骄傲、固执、对抗、虚伪、嫉妒、吹嘘、自负、亲近不善者——这是荤腥，不是吃肉。 品行不良、拒还债务、中伤、欺骗商人、假冒善人——这是荤腥，不是吃肉。 在这个世界上，那些杀生、对有情众生无悲悯心、掠夺他人、毁坏、粗暴——这是荤腥，不是吃肉。 贪婪、不节制、杀害、常行恶法、破戒失德——死后堕入地狱——这是荤腥，不是吃肉。 不是鱼、不是肉、不是斋戒、不是裸体、不是剃头、不是发髻、不是尘土、不是兽皮、不是火祭——世界上没有任何净化咒术能清除那些未渡疑惑的人的污垢。 以根守护感官，走在感官中，坚守真理、柔和、调御、不被欲染、放舍一切苦——智者不执着于所见所闻。 世尊这样宣说，以种种方式——提沙·迦叶波，明白此法的人，不依赖任何东西，以智慧了知法之根本，不求他人，恰当地行脚。 世尊这样教导，以偈颂——于是婆罗门提沙·迦叶波，以偈颂回答佛陀： 【提沙·迦叶波】我今理解，询问佛陀与瞿昙——的确，是这样。以食物净化是另一个传统，而今我问到了善的道路，彻底了知，我从迷惑中解脱出来。

逐句解释

【提沙·迦叶波】不吃荤腥，不用芝麻；这是婆罗门家的传统——没有荤腥的食物不会玷污他。

提沙·迦叶波（Tissa Kassapa）是一位坚持饮食纯净规范的婆罗门。他的立场代表了印度古代的普遍观念：饮食的纯洁（尤其是避免肉食和某些「不洁」食物）决定了人的宗教纯洁性。他来质疑佛陀：佛陀与弟子食用各种食物，如何算是清净者？经文以对话形式展开这场关于纯洁性本质的辩论。

Tissa Kassapa: Not eating what is rank, not using sesame — this is the tradition in brahmin families. One whose food is free of rank smell is not defiled by it.

Tissa Kassapa represents the brahminical position that ritual purity — especially dietary purity — determines religious standing. The word translated 'rank smell' (āmagandha) literally means 'raw smell,' the smell of raw meat; it became a technical term for ritual impurity in food. His challenge to the Buddha is implicit: if you eat anything placed in your bowl, how can you claim purity? The sutta stages this debate in order to redirect the entire category of 'impurity' away from dietary rules toward conduct.

【世尊】鱼和肉——我不这样说是荤腥。杀生、打人、割割、束缚、偷盗、说谎、欺骗——这是荤腥，不是吃肉。

佛陀的回应是彻底的概念转移：「荤腥」（āmagandha，腥臭）不指食物的种类，而指行为的品质。列举的行为——杀生、暴力、束缚、盗窃、欺骗——是真正使人「腥臭」的东西。这个立场的深刻之处在于：它不是「吃肉没关系」的宽松教导，而是将道德的标准从外在形式（吃什么）彻底转向内在实质（做什么、意图什么）。

The Blessed One: Fish and meat — I do not say these are rank. Killing, beating, cutting, binding, stealing, lying, deceiving — this is rank, not the eating of flesh.

The Buddha's move is a complete reorientation of the concept of 'purity.' The āmagandha — the rank smell of impurity — is not in food but in harmful action. This is not a libertarian statement about diet; it is a radical relocation of the moral criterion. What defiles is intention and action: violence, deception, harm. A person who eats simply but harms others constantly is far more 'rank' than one whose diet is unrestricted but whose conduct is pure. The teaching redirects attention from the outer to the inner.

粗野、固执、背叛、不悲悯、傲慢、吝啬——不施予朋友——这是荤腥，不是吃肉。

经文展开一份道德「腥臭」的详细清单，涵盖了多个层面：对他人的粗暴对待、内心的固执与傲慢、不悲悯（无慈）、吝啬（不布施）。「不施予朋友」特别值得注意：在古印度，对朋友的慷慨是基本的社会美德；吝啬不仅是个人品格缺陷，更是对关系与社群的背叛。

Harshness, stubbornness, betrayal, lack of compassion, arrogance, miserliness — not giving to friends — this is rank, not the eating of flesh.

The list broadens from violent acts to character traits: harshness in speech and manner, stubbornness (the refusal to yield even when wrong), betrayal of trust, absence of compassion, arrogance, and miserliness. The specific mention of 'not giving to friends' places the teaching in the context of lay social life — generosity toward one's circle is a baseline social virtue, and its absence is a mark of spiritual degradation. These are the 'smells' that indicate a corrupted character.

不是鱼、不是肉、不是斋戒、不是裸体、不是剃头——世界上没有任何净化咒术能清除那些未渡疑惑的人的污垢。

这一颂是经文最具张力的陈述：不仅饮食不能净化，连各种苦行与宗教仪式也无法净化——斋戒、裸体苦行（耆那教与某些婆罗门传统的修行）、剃发、积尘垢于身、兽皮衣、火祭，统统无效。真正的污垢是「未渡疑惑」（vicikicchā，对真理的根本疑惑与不确定）——只有洞见与道德品格才能净化这种污垢。

Not by fish, not by flesh, not by fasting, not by nakedness, not by shaving the head — no purification rite in the world can cleanse the filth of one who has not crossed over doubt.

The verse is the sutta's most radical statement: not only diet but the full spectrum of ascetic and ritual practices — fasting, nudity (Jain and some brahminical practice), head-shaving, smearing with dust, animal skins, fire-sacrifice — cannot purify. What cannot be removed by any of these is vicikicchā: the fundamental unresolved doubt about the nature of reality, truth, and the path. Only direct insight (paired with ethical conduct) addresses this. All external purification methods mistake the symptom for the disease.

以根守护感官，走在感官中，坚守真理、柔和、调御、不被欲染——智者不执着于所见所闻。

经文最后转向正面陈述：真正的清净是什么样的？是根律仪（守护六根，使感官接触不引发贪嗔）、坚守真理、柔和、调御身心、不被欲望染著、对所见所闻不生执着。这是修行的正面描述，与前面的「腥臭」清单形成对照：真正的清净在于内心对感官接触的回应方式，而非外在的饮食规范。

With sense-faculties guarded, walking in the senses, firm in truth, gentle, trained, not stained by craving — the wise one is not attached to what is seen or heard.

Having defined purity negatively (what it is not), the sutta offers its positive portrait: indriyasaṃvara (sense restraint — not suppressing experience but responding without reactive clinging), adherence to truth, gentleness, training, freedom from craving's stain, and non-attachment to the objects of perception. 'Not attached to what is seen or heard' does not mean sensory deprivation but the absence of clinging in the moment of contact. This is the actual practice of purity — internal, behavioral, relational — not a dietary regime.

【提沙·迦叶波】我今理解——的确，是这样。以食物净化是另一个传统，而今我彻底了知，我从迷惑中解脱出来。

提沙·迦叶波以偈颂回应，承认自己的理解已改变：「以食物净化是另一个传统」——他不否定自己原有的传统，而是承认它属于另一类规范，与佛陀所揭示的更深层真理不同。「从迷惑中解脱出来」表明他的改变不是表面的意见转变，而是内心真正的澄清。对话以转化而非失败告终——这是经集中对话型经典的典型结构。

Tissa Kassapa: I understand now — it is indeed so. Purification by food is a different tradition. Having now asked and fully known, I am freed from confusion.

Tissa Kassapa's response is dignified: he does not collapse in embarrassment but acknowledges understanding. 'Purification by food is a different tradition' — he frames his former view not as stupidity but as belonging to a different system of thought, which the Buddha's teaching has now transcended. 'Freed from confusion' echoes the sutta's implicit theme: the deepest impurity is unresolved confusion about what actually constitutes purity. The dialogue ends in genuine understanding — the sutta's teaching has accomplished exactly what it describes: clarity, not ritual performance.

总结 · Summary

腥臭经是佛陀最有力的道德重新定义之一：它将「荤腥」（āmagandha，腥臭/不洁）从饮食领域彻底转移到行为与品格领域。婆罗门提沙·迦叶波以饮食纯净的传统观念挑战佛陀，佛陀的回应是系统性地列举真正的「腥臭」：杀生、暴力、欺骗、傲慢、吝啬、不悲悯——这些才是使人真正不洁的东西，不是鱼肉，也不是任何食物。更进一步，经文宣告一切外在净化手段的无效：斋戒、裸体苦行、仪式，都无法净化「未渡疑惑」的内心。真正的清净是根律仪、坚守真理、调御身心、不被欲望染著。这是对印度传统宗教纯洁观念的根本性批判，将道德标准的中心从外在仪规转向内在品格与行为实质。

The Āmagandha Sutta is one of the Buddha's most powerful moral redefinitions: it relocates 'rank smell' (āmagandha — impurity) entirely from the dietary domain to the domain of conduct and character. The brahmin Tissa Kassapa challenges the Buddha on the grounds of food purity; the Buddha responds by systematically listing what is actually rank — killing, violence, deception, arrogance, miserliness, lack of compassion. These, not flesh or any food, are genuine impurity. The sutta then goes further: all external purification methods — fasting, asceticism, ritual — are unable to cleanse unresolved confusion about reality. True purity consists in sense restraint, adherence to truth, training, and freedom from craving. This is a fundamental critique of ritual-based religion: the criterion of moral standing is internal and behavioural, not external and ceremonial.
