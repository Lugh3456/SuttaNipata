【法迦居士】我要问您关于在家修行的事情——请告诉我，世尊：一位出家比丘，以及一位在家修行的居士，他们应该如何修行？ 【世尊】我将为你讲述在家生活的法——听我说，法迦！ 不杀生命，不取不与之物，不说谎，不饮酒，不与他人之妻或丈夫私通。 在新月日与满月日的当天，以及两个八日，应完整地守持八戒——这是在家人的戒律，带来安乐与利益。 在戒日，比丘早起——欢喜地布施食物与饮料，供养僧众。 在戒日，适当地供养在家人以供给比丘的需要——欢喜地提供所需，这是在家人的修行之道。 如此，在家人就能在此获得善报——获得幸福与快乐，死后升入天界。 对于比丘，应守持所有的戒律——断除贪欲、恶意与无明——以此安住。 远离财富的执取，远离对财宝与谷物、土地的贪欲，这对于希求解脱的比丘是适当的。 无家可归者，对感官克制，不放逸，专注，远离一切执着——这是比丘的修行。 不管在何处居住，无论在山林还是在村庄——向阿罗汉求法，住于无执取的境界中——这是比丘的道路。 如是，出家人和在家人——我都指示了正道——这两种道路，是修行的明确方向。

逐句解释

【法迦居士】我要问您关于在家修行的事情——请告诉我，世尊：一位出家比丘，以及一位在家修行的居士，他们应该如何修行？

法经以居士法迦（Dhammika）的问题开篇，这个问题具有重要的包容性：他同时询问出家比丘与在家居士的修行方式。这表明佛教从一开始就承认两条有效的道路，而不是仅为出家者提供指引。法迦来自五百名在家弟子的群体（「带着五百人」），这表明这是一个代表在家团体提出的问题——在家人需要适合自己生活情境的指导，而不仅仅是出家戒律的削减版。

Dhammika the layperson: I want to ask about practice for householders — tell me, Blessed One: how should a monk and a householder practise?

Dhammika arrives representing five hundred lay disciples — his question is a communal inquiry about the lay path. The significance of asking about both monastics and householders simultaneously is that it establishes the sutta as an account of two genuinely valid paths rather than one ideal (monastic) and one compromise (lay). The Buddha's answer — a full section for each — honours this framing. The Dhammika Sutta is the Cūḷavagga's closing text and one of the Pali Canon's most explicit treatments of the parallel validity of the two lifestyles.

不杀生命，不取不与之物，不说谎，不饮酒，不与他人之妻或丈夫私通。

在家人的五戒（pañcasīla）：不杀生（pāṇātipātā veramaṇī）、不偷盗（adinnādānā）、不妄语（musāvādā）、不饮酒（surāmeraya-majjapānā）、不邪淫（kāmesumicchācārā）。这五戒是在家佛教伦理的基础，也是整个人类社会最基本的道德规范——减少直接的伤害、欺骗与失去理智的行为。经文将此作为在家修行的起点，表明道德戒律不是压迫性的规定，而是使良善在家生活成为可能的基础结构。

Not killing living beings, not taking what is not given, not speaking falsely, not drinking intoxicants, not engaging in sexual misconduct with another's partner.

The five precepts (pañcasīla) for lay practitioners are enumerated directly and without elaboration — they are the foundational ethical structure. Their logic is social and personal simultaneously: each precept prevents a specific kind of harm (violence, theft, deception, loss of mindfulness through intoxication, violation of relational trust). The five together constitute the minimum ethical ground from which a life oriented toward Dhamma can be built. Without this ground, the other dimensions of practice — generosity, meditation, wisdom — lack foundation. The precepts are not constraints on flourishing but its prerequisites.

在新月日与满月日的当天，以及两个八日，应完整地守持八戒——这是在家人的戒律，带来安乐与利益。

「新月日与满月日」（uposatha，布萨日）以及两个「第八日」（月亮周期的第八日），共构成每月四个特别的戒律日。在这些日子，在家人守持八戒（aṭṭhasīla），相当于比丘戒律的一个简化版本，包括不吃午后食物、不使用高床、不观看歌舞等。这种分级的戒律制度允许在家人在特定的日子体验更深的出离，而无需全时间出家——是在家生活与出家修行之间的一个有意设计的连接。

On the new moon and full moon days, and on the two eighth days, the eight precepts should be kept completely — this is the lay precepts, bringing welfare and happiness.

The uposatha days — four per lunar month — are occasions for lay practitioners to observe the eight precepts, which add to the five precepts: not eating after noon, abstaining from entertainment and cosmetics, and not sleeping on high beds. This graduated system is elegantly designed: ordinary days involve the five precepts of a functional ethical life; designated days involve the eight precepts of a more intensive, monastic-adjacent practice. Lay practitioners can taste deeper renunciation without abandoning household life. The system acknowledges the reality of laypeople's lives while providing a pathway toward deeper practice.

对于比丘，应守持所有的戒律——断除贪欲、恶意与无明——以此安住。

转向比丘的修行。「守持所有的戒律」（sabbasīlesu sampanna，圆满一切戒律）——比丘戒律远比在家人的五戒或八戒复杂，具足戒（pātimokkha）包括比丘二百二十七条戒律。「断除贪欲、恶意与无明」（lobha-dosa-moha，贪嗔痴三毒）是解脱道的核心任务：三毒是一切苦的根源，戒律的持守是为了防止三毒通过行为外露，而禅修与智慧则进一步断除三毒的内在根源。

For the monk: be accomplished in all the precepts — cutting off lust, ill-will, and ignorance — abide thus.

The monastic path begins with a higher bar: 'all the precepts' refers to the full Pātimokkha — the 227 training rules of the Theravāda monk. This is not merely a matter of social ethics but a comprehensive training of body, speech, and mind through structured constraint. The three roots of suffering — lobha (greed/lust), dosa (aversion/ill-will), and moha (delusion/ignorance) — are named as what must be cut off. Precepts create the conditions (reduced external involvement, simplified living) in which the deeper work of removing these roots through meditation and wisdom can proceed.

如是，出家人和在家人——我都指示了正道——这两种道路，是修行的明确方向。

法经的最后一颂以一个明确的总结收场：佛陀宣告他已同时为出家人与在家人指示了正道。「这两种道路」（ubho pi te magge，两条道路都是）——不是「在家道路勉强可以」，而是「两种道路都是正道」。这是对在家修行的明确肯定：在家人持守五戒与八戒、布施供养，是真实的修行；出家人持戒、断三毒、远离执取，是另一条真实的修行。两条路通向同一个方向。

Thus for monastics and for householders both — I have shown the right path — these two paths are the clear direction of practice.

The closing affirmation is the sutta's most significant statement: both paths have been shown, and both are right. This is not a concession to lay practitioners ('the best you can do is…') but a positive affirmation of the dual-path structure of the Buddha's teaching. The Cūḷavagga closes with this inclusive declaration — the small chapter that began with the protective chant (Ratana), moved through ethics, friendship, urgency, and wisdom, ends by explicitly affirming that the Dhamma provides a genuine path for both those who leave the household and those who remain in it. Neither is second-class.

总结 · Summary

法经是小品（Cūḷavagga）的最后一部经典，以最明确的方式陈述了佛教双轨制修行体系的核心。居士法迦同时为出家比丘与在家居士询问修行之道，佛陀以平等的认真态度回答了两者：在家人持守五戒、在布萨日守八戒、布施供养僧众，这是真实的修行道路；出家比丘圆满戒律、断除三毒（贪嗔痴）、远离执取，这是另一条真实的修行道路。经文以「这两种道路，是修行的明确方向」作结，宣告两条路都是正道。小品以此作结，表明佛教的教法是充分包容的：无论以何种生活方式，都有一条通往解脱的真实道路。

The Dhammika Sutta closes the Cūḷavagga with the most explicit articulation of Buddhism's dual-path structure. Dhammika the layperson asks about both the monastic and lay paths simultaneously; the Buddha answers both with equal seriousness. Lay practice: the five precepts as a daily foundation; the eight precepts on uposatha days; generosity and support of the Saṅgha. Monastic practice: full precepts, cutting off the three roots of suffering, non-attachment, non-dependence. The closing verse affirms that both paths are right paths — not one ideal and one compromise. The entire Cūḷavagga — from the protective Ratana chant through ethics, friendship, urgency, grief, and wisdom — ends with this inclusive declaration: the Dhamma provides a genuine path to liberation for everyone, however they live.
