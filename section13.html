第二品·第一经 · 宝经

无论在此聚集的，是地上或空中的众生，愿所有众生都欢喜——请认真听我所说的话。 因此，诸位众生，请听从——对人类施予慈悲，日夜向他奉献的诸神与人类，请保护他们！ 在此世界或另一世界，无论何种财富，无论天界的珍贵宝石——没有什么能与如来相比。这宝在佛中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。 止息，离去，不死，无上——释迦牟尼以定所证悟的，没有任何东西与此法相比。这宝在法中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。 世尊所赞叹的清净三昧，被称为当下的果报——进入此三昧者，无以为比。这宝在法中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。 有信心的善男子赞扬了八位人，四对圣者——世尊的弟子们，值得布施——施予他们，大果报现前。这宝在僧中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。 坚定地应用自己，无有欲求——在瞿昙的教法中努力精进——他们已达到不死，获得成就，享受平静。这宝在僧中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。 如立于地中的门柱，不为四方风所动；我如此说有智慧者，观见四圣谛的人。这宝在僧中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。 深刻理解四圣谛的人，以善教者的智慧，即使有重大懈怠，也不会投生为第八生。这宝在僧中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。 随着见到，三法被舍弃：身见、疑惑、任何戒禁取——他也从三恶道中完全解脱，不能做六种根本罪恶。这宝在僧中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。 即使他做了恶行，以身、语、意——他不能隐藏，因为不能对见道者隐藏。这宝在僧中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。 如林中夏初的树林开花，他讲解了导向寂灭的最高法——同样地，世尊所教导的，带来最大利益。这宝在佛中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。 殊胜的知道，施与殊胜的，带来殊胜的果——殊胜者给予，殊胜的结果诞生。这宝在佛中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。 旧业已尽，新业不生，心对未来出生没有执着——种子已灭，欲望不再生长，智者就如这盏灯熄灭一般寂灭。这宝在僧中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。 无论在此聚集的，是地上或空中的众生，我们礼敬如来——为人天所尊崇的佛陀。愿有吉祥。 无论在此聚集的，是地上或空中的众生，我们礼敬如来——为人天所尊崇的法。愿有吉祥。 无论在此聚集的，是地上或空中的众生，我们礼敬如来——为人天所尊崇的僧。愿有吉祥。

逐句解释

无论在此聚集的，是地上或空中的众生，愿所有众生都欢喜——请认真听我所说的话。

宝经的传统背景是：毘舍离城遭受瘟疫、饥荒与非人侵扰，居民请求佛陀前往。佛陀令阿难诵此经绕城，驱散灾难。经文开篇邀请一切有情聆听——地上众生与空中众生（诸神、夜叉等），建立一个无遮的聆听场域。

Whatever beings are assembled here, whether on earth or in the sky — may all beings be happy; please listen carefully to what I say.

The Ratana Sutta was traditionally chanted during a crisis in Vesālī — plague, famine, and non-human disturbances. The Buddha sent Ānanda through the city reciting this text. The opening summons all beings, human and non-human, to attend. This inclusive address — encompassing visible and invisible realms — is characteristic of protection chants (paritta), which are intended to establish a field of truth that extends in all directions.

在此世界或另一世界，无论何种财富，无论天界的珍贵宝石——没有什么能与如来相比。这宝在佛中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。

「以此真实，愿有吉祥」（idam pi Buddhe ratanam paṇītaṃ, etena saccena suvatthi hotu）是宝经的核心结构：每一颂赞叹三宝之一（佛、法、僧），以「真实誓言」（saccakiriyā）作结。真实誓言的原理是：所说的话若绝对真实，其真实性本身便具有护持的力量。

Whatever wealth exists in this world or beyond, whatever precious jewel in the heavens — nothing is equal to the Tathāgata. This jewel is in the Buddha. By this truth, may there be well-being.

The sutta's refrain operates through saccakiriyā — the 'act of truth.' In ancient Indian thought, a statement of perfect truth has inherent power; its utterance creates a protective field. Each verse declares something true about the Buddha, Dhamma, or Saṅgha, and the act of affirming that truth is itself the source of protection. The comparison with worldly treasures establishes that the Three Jewels are the highest value available — not merely religious preference but cosmological fact.

止息，离去，不死，无上——释迦牟尼以定所证悟的，没有任何东西与此法相比。这宝在法中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。

法宝以四个词描绘涅槃：止息（khema，安全、平静）、离去（virāga，无贪）、不死（amata，不死）、无上（anuttara，无上）。这四个词不是抽象的神学概念，而是修行者直接体验的状态。释迦牟尼的「定」（samādhi）在此指他对这些状态的直接实证，使他有资格传授导向涅槃的法道。

Cessation, dispassion, the deathless, the supreme — what the Sakyan sage realised through concentration, nothing equals this Dhamma. This jewel is in the Dhamma. By this truth, may there be well-being.

Four epithets describe Nibbāna: khema (safety, peace), virāga (dispassion), amata (the deathless), anuttara (the unexcelled). These are not metaphors but descriptors of a directly attainable state. The Buddha's concentration (samādhi) is named as the means of realisation — emphasising that the Dhamma is not merely a doctrine but an experiential achievement, replicable through practice. Its incomparability to all other things is the truth that protects.

有信心的善男子赞扬了八位人，四对圣者——世尊的弟子们，值得布施——施予他们，大果报现前。这宝在僧中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。

「八位人，四对」指四果各分道果两位：须陀洹道与果、斯陀含道与果、阿那含道与果、阿罗汉道与果，共八位圣者。这是早期佛教的标准修行图谱。对这样的僧团布施，被称为「值得布施」（āhuneyyā）——不仅是施者的功德，更因为这些弟子是法的活生生的体现，将法传递到每一个接受的心中。

The persons of good faith extol the eight individuals, the four pairs — the disciples of the Blessed One, worthy of offerings. Giving to them, great fruit arises. This jewel is in the Saṅgha. By this truth, may there be well-being.

The 'eight persons, four pairs' describes the classical map of attainments: stream-entry (path and fruit), once-returning (path and fruit), non-returning (path and fruit), and arahantship (path and fruit). Each stage involves both the moment of breakthrough (magga) and its established fruit (phala). This Saṅgha — not the wider monastic community but specifically those who have attained these stages — is 'worthy of offerings' because they embody the Dhamma and transmit its reality to the world.

如立于地中的门柱，不为四方风所动；我如此说有智慧者，观见四圣谛的人。这宝在僧中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。

门柱（indakhīla）深埋地基，城门的柱石，任何风力都无法撼动。观见四圣谛的人（见道者，须陀洹以上）以同样的方式不可撼动：他们已经直接看见苦、集、灭、道，这种直接的见道（dassana）无法被取消。疑惑、身见、戒禁取已被永久舍弃——信心从此建立在无可动摇的基础上。

Like a pillar planted in the earth, unmoved by the winds from the four directions — so I say is the person of wisdom who sees the Four Noble Truths. This jewel is in the Saṅgha. By this truth, may there be well-being.

The indakhīla is the great post sunk deep into the earth at a city gate — immovable because it is anchored below the surface. The stream-enterer's certainty is of the same quality: having directly seen the Four Noble Truths, their understanding cannot be shaken by doubt, wrong view, or external challenge. The simile does not describe stubbornness but rootedness — an insight that has penetrated below the level where winds of opinion can reach.

随着见到，三法被舍弃：身见、疑惑、任何戒禁取——他也从三恶道中完全解脱，不能做六种根本罪恶。这宝在僧中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。

须陀洹果断除三结：身见（sakkāyadiṭṭhi，认为五蕴中有常一的自我）、疑惑（vicikicchā，对佛法僧的根本疑惑）、戒禁取（sīlabbataparāmāsa，执持错误的道德规范或仪式为解脱之道）。断此三结者永不堕三恶道（地狱、饿鬼、畜生），最多七次人天往返便证涅槃，且不能犯弑父、弑母、弑阿罗汉、出佛身血、破和合僧五逆罪及随顺外道。

With seeing, three things are abandoned: self-view, doubt, and any grasping at rules and rituals. He is fully freed from the four states of misery and cannot commit the six root offences. This jewel is in the Saṅgha. By this truth, may there be well-being.

Stream-entry (sotāpatti) removes three 'fetters' (saṃyojana): sakkāyadiṭṭhi (the view that there is a permanent self in or identical with the five aggregates), vicikicchā (doubt about the Buddha, Dhamma, and Saṅgha), and sīlabbataparāmāsa (the belief that ritual observance alone leads to liberation). The result is guaranteed: no more lower rebirths, no capacity for certain extreme acts. The liberation described is not yet complete but is irrevocable — once the stream is entered, the ocean is the destination.

旧业已尽，新业不生，心对未来出生没有执着——种子已灭，欲望不再生长，智者就如这盏灯熄灭一般寂灭。这宝在僧中。以此真实，愿有吉祥。

这是宝经最后一颂关于僧的赞叹，描绘阿罗汉的涅槃。旧业（过去造作的业力）已耗尽，新业（将导致再生的业）不再积累，因为对「未来出生」的执着（abhinandanti）已断。「种子」指再生的种子（bīja），欲望（taṇhā）是令种子发芽的水分；二者皆尽，就如油尽灯灭——灯没有消失到某处，只是燃料耗尽，火焰自然止息。

Old kamma is exhausted, no new kamma is produced, the mind is not attached to a future birth. Seeds destroyed, desires not sprouting — the wise one is extinguished just as this lamp is. This jewel is in the Saṅgha. By this truth, may there be well-being.

The arahant's parinibbāna is described through the lamp simile — one of the most frequent images for Nibbāna in early Buddhism. The lamp does not go anywhere when it is extinguished; the fire simply ceases because fuel is absent. Old kamma is exhausted through lived experience; new kamma is not generated because the craving and ignorance that produce it are gone; the 'seeds' of rebirth have no moisture of desire to sprout. The verse is the culmination of the sutta's praise: the highest jewel in the Saṅgha is the one who has passed fully beyond.

总结 · Summary

宝经是上座部传统最重要的护持经典之一，至今仍在寺院与家庭中持诵，用于驱邪、祈福、护持。其结构建立在「真实誓言」（saccakiriyā）的原理上：每一颂对三宝之一作出绝对真实的陈述，以「以此真实，愿有吉祥」作结——真实陈述本身便是保护的力量。经文赞叹佛陀无与伦比，赞叹法是直接体验的涅槃，赞叹僧是已实证各个阶位的圣者团体。护持的力量不来自神灵的庇佑，而来自真理本身的重量。最后一颂以油尽灯灭描绘阿罗汉的寂灭，将整部经典的赞叹引向最终的解脱——三宝的宝贵，正在于它们指向并体现了这个终点。

The Ratana Sutta is one of Theravāda Buddhism's most important protection chants (paritta), still recited in monasteries and households for blessing and protection. Its architecture rests on saccakiriyā — the act of truth: each verse makes an absolutely true statement about the Buddha, Dhamma, or Saṅgha, then closes with 'by this truth, may there be well-being.' The protective force is not supernatural intercession but the weight of truth itself. The sutta praises the Buddha as incomparable to any worldly treasure, the Dhamma as the directly realised Nibbāna, and the Saṅgha as the community of those who have attained its various stages. The final verse — the lamp extinguished when fuel is gone — brings the entire arc of praise to its destination: the Three Jewels are precious precisely because they lead to and embody liberation.
