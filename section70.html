第五品·第十五经 · 谟伽罗阇经

经集 · Sutta Nipata · 彼岸道品

← 第五品·第十四经 目录 Contents 第五品·第十六经 →

【谟伽罗阇问】 我两次问你，释迦族人—— 你没有告诉我—— 我听说，圣人， 第三次问时，你会回答—— 这个世间——另一个世间—— 梵天世界——连同神明—— 你如何看待这个世间， 使死神找不到你？ 【世尊答】 谟伽罗阇，把世间看作空的—— 以正念，随时—— 拔除对自我的见解—— 如此，超越死亡—— 这样看待世间的人， 死神见不到他。

逐句解释

我两次问你，释迦族人——你没有告诉我——第三次问时，你会回答。

谟伽罗阇经（Mogharājā Sutta）有一个独特的叙事框架：谟伽罗阇之前已问过两次，佛陀没有回答；这是第三次。「第三次问时，你会回答」——这个细节在巴利经典中罕见，暗示谟伽罗阇的问题需要特别的时机，或需要提问者达到某种成熟度。这也许意味着：这个答案太重要、太精确，不能随便给出，而要等到提问者足够准备好。谟伽罗阇的耐心与坚持本身，就是一种修行。

I have asked you twice, Sakyan — you did not tell me — I have heard, sage, that you answer on the third asking.

The narrative frame of Mogharājā is unique in the Pārāyanavagga: two previous unanswered questions, and now a third. This three-attempt structure appears elsewhere in early Buddhist literature as a marker of significance — the teaching is given only when the student has demonstrated genuine persistence and the moment is right. Mogharājā's patience and his willingness to return twice before receiving an answer is itself a form of practice. The delayed answer also sets up the received teaching as something that required preparation to receive — both in the teacher's timing and the student's readiness.

把世间看作空的——以正念，随时——拔除对自我的见解——如此，超越死亡——这样看待世间的人，死神见不到他。

「把世间看作空的」（suññato lokaṃ avekkhassu）——这一句是整部经集中最著名的句子之一，也是「空观」（suññatā）在彼岸道品中最直接的表达。「空的」：不是世间什么都没有，而是世间「空于自我」（suññaṃ attena）——没有固定的、实质的自我可以被找到。「以正念，随时」：这个空观不是一次性的哲学认识，而是随时、随地、以正念维持的观察方式。「拔除对自我的见解」（attānudiṭṭhiṃ ūhacca）：「我见」（sakkāyadiṭṭhi，身见）是轮回的核心执取——拔除它，死神就失去了追踪的目标。「死神见不到他」——没有「自我」可以被死亡抓住；「我见」被拔除，死神就无从识别你。

Look upon the world as empty — always, with mindfulness — remove the view of self — thus, transcend death — one who sees the world this way, the death-king does not see.

'Look upon the world as empty' (suññato lokaṃ avekkhassu) is one of the most famous sentences in the entire Sutta Nipata and one of the most concentrated statements of the suññatā teaching in the Pali Canon. 'Empty' here means empty of self (suññaṃ attena vā attaniyena vā — empty of self or what belongs to self): the world is not nothing, but it contains no fixed substantial self anywhere. 'Always, with mindfulness' — the suññatā perspective is not a one-time philosophical insight but a continuous quality of perception maintained through ongoing mindfulness. 'Remove the view of self' — sakkāyadiṭṭhi (identity-view, the view that there is a self in the five aggregates) is the root of all clinging and the structural condition for continued existence as a target of death. When identity-view is removed, there is no self for the death-king to track. 'The death-king does not see' — Māra operates through identity; no identity, no target.

总结 · Summary

谟伽罗阇经包含整部经集最著名的一句话：「把世间看作空的——以正念，随时——拔除对自我的见解——如此，超越死亡」。这个「空观」（suññatā）是：世间空于自我（无固定实质的自我），这个观察以正念随时维持，拔除「我见」（sakkāyadiṭṭhi），死神就找不到你——因为没有「我」可以被死亡所抓住。经文之所以在第三次问才回答，暗示这个教导的分量：它需要提问者足够成熟才能真正领受。谟伽罗阇经是彼岸道品的倒数第二经，也是整部经集中「空」的教导最直接的表达。

The Mogharājā Sutta delivers what may be the single most concentrated teaching in the entire Sutta Nipata: 'look upon the world as empty — always, with mindfulness — remove the view of self — thus transcend death.' The suññatā (emptiness) teaching here is not the full Madhyamaka emptiness of later philosophy but the emptiness-of-self that is native to the earliest strata of Pali literature: the world contains no fixed substantial self anywhere. Maintaining this perspective through ongoing mindfulness, and removing the deep-seated identity-view that is the structural condition of all clinging, the practitioner becomes literally invisible to Māra (death): there is no self for the death-king to find. The three-asking frame elevates this teaching as one requiring special readiness — and the brevity of the answer, after so much waiting, gives it its extraordinary weight.
