第二品·第六经 · 正游行经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第二品·第五经 目录 Contents 第二品·第七经 →

【天神】我要问世尊一个问题——请告诉我，牟尼：一位比丘如何正确地游行于世间，以令诸天也敬礼他？ 【世尊】谁的旗帜已倒下，谁的负担已卸下，谁没有缠缚——无论在哪里他都不执着，没有捆缚：他正确地游行。 谁已断除愤怒，已放弃慢，已超越一切的缚束——没有执取、调御、平静：他正确地游行。 谁依靠自己的力量，不执着于任何事物，全然知道法，不依靠任何东西：他正确地游行。 谁没有自以为是，谁已超越了时间，谁在世间没有停顿，谁无所有、无渴望：他正确地游行。 谁没有分别，谁洞见一切，谁在一切法中无所执着，谁已放弃一切：他正确地游行。 谁已断除有漏，谁已抛弃骄慢，谁超越一切——谁没有贪欲，调御、平静：他正确地游行。 谁已断除信，已到达不死，谁已去除欲望之坑，谁没有任何遗留，没有抛洒：他正确地游行。 谁已超越缠缚，在世间没有执取，谁为神所爱，谁已超越了生死：他正确地游行。 谁在此世没有执着——谁已越过了此与彼，谁已驱除一切忧患与愤怒：他正确地游行。 谁已度过了不满，谁已调御、平静，谁有明智，谁已超越生死，谁之心已整合、稳定：他正确地游行。 谁没有分别心，从一切束缚中解脱，没有执取，坚定调御：他正确地游行。 谁已断除有漏，没有执取的恐惧，在世间的流动中保持平静，没有任何被夺走：他正确地游行。

逐句解释

谁的旗帜已倒下，谁的负担已卸下，谁没有缠缚——无论在哪里他都不执着，没有捆缚：他正确地游行。

「旗帜已倒下」（oropitadhajo，放下了旗帜）是一个生动的战争比喻：旗帜是骄慢（māna）的象征，高举旗帜是对自我与地位的宣扬；放下旗帜意味着彻底放弃自我主张。「负担已卸下」（nikkhittabhāro）与长老偈中的经典表述相呼应——五蕴的负担已放下。「没有缠缚」（visaṃyutto）指已从所有束缚中解脱。「正确地游行」（sammā paribbajeyya）是本经的核心短语，描述僧侣在世间行脚的正确方式——不是外在的仪式，而是内心的解脱状态。

Whose banner is lowered, whose burden is put down, who is unentangled — who does not cling anywhere, has no bonds: he wanders rightly.

The 'lowered banner' (oropitadhaja) is a military image: a banner raised in battle represents pride, the assertion of self and status. To lower it is to abandon all such claims. The 'put-down burden' (nikkhittabhāra) echoes the famous formula in the Canon — the burden of the five aggregates, finally set down. 'Unentangled' (visaṃyutta) describes freedom from all the fetters that bind beings to the cycle of existence. 'Wandering rightly' (sammā paribbajeyya) is the sutta's refrain — not a description of physical movement but of an inner orientation that makes any movement through the world a form of freedom.

谁已断除愤怒，已放弃慢，已超越一切的缚束——没有执取、调御、平静：他正确地游行。

第二颂专注于三种被断除的内心障碍：愤怒（kodha）、慢（māna，骄慢）与缚束（saṃyojana，结，将众生系缚在轮回中的心理锁链）。「没有执取」（anādāna）、「调御」（danta，已被驯服的，如驯良的动物）、「平静」（subbato，品行良好、平静）——这三个词描述解脱者的正面特质，与前面被断除的负面特质形成对照。整首经以这种双重结构展开：断除什么 + 具有什么。

Whose anger is cut off, who has abandoned conceit, who has gone beyond all fetters — without clinging, tamed, at peace: he wanders rightly.

Each verse in the sutta follows a similar double structure: what has been eliminated, and what is positively present. Here: anger (kodha) — the reactive surge of ill-will; conceit (māna) — the persistent sense of 'I am better/equal/worse than'; and fetters (saṃyojana) — the ten bonds that tie beings to rebirth. Against these negatives: without clinging (anādāna), tamed (danta — the image of a well-trained animal, responsive and controlled), and at peace (subbato — well-behaved, serene). The combination describes a character shaped by the removal of internal obstacles rather than by external restraint.

谁依靠自己的力量，不执着于任何事物，全然知道法，不依靠任何东西：他正确地游行。

「依靠自己的力量」（sayaṃ ñatvā，通过自己的知道）强调直接的亲身知见，而非依赖他人的权威或传承。「全然知道法」（sabbadhammaṃ viditvāna）不是百科全书式的知识，而是对一切现象之性质（无常、苦、无我）的直接洞见。「不依靠任何东西」（anissito，不依赖的）呼应整首经的主题：正确游行的僧侣是内在自足的——不依赖外部认可、仪式、或任何有条件的支撑。

Who knows by themselves, who clings to nothing, who knows all the Dhamma thoroughly, who does not depend on anything: he wanders rightly.

Sayaṃ ñatvā — 'knowing for oneself' — is a fundamental principle in the Pali Canon: the liberated person's understanding is direct and personal, not borrowed from authority. This is not rejection of teachers but description of what genuine understanding looks like once it is achieved — it stands on its own. 'Knowing all the Dhamma' is comprehensive insight into the nature of all phenomena — their impermanence, unsatisfactoriness, and selflessness. 'Not depending on anything' (anissita) is perhaps the sutta's central theme: the rightly-wandering monk carries no invisible dependencies — on approval, on ritual efficacy, on any conditioned support.

谁在世间没有执取——谁已越过了此与彼，谁已驱除一切忧患与愤怒：他正确地游行。

「此与彼」（idam amuñ ca，这个与那个）在早期佛教的语境中常指「此岸与彼岸」——世俗存在的两极。「越过了此与彼」描述了超越二元对立的状态：不执着于任何一端，不被生死之间的往返所困。「驱除一切忧患与愤怒」（soka-rosaṃ）——忧愁与愤怒是情感痛苦的两个主要面向，前者朝向过去与失去，后者朝向当下的障碍与威胁；两者皆灭，正确游行于世。

Who does not grasp in the world — who has crossed over this and that, who has removed all grief and anger: he wanders rightly.

'This and that' (idam amuñca) refers to the poles of conditioned existence — the world of here and there, self and other, pleasant and unpleasant. To have 'crossed over' them is to be free of reactive identification with either pole. Soka (grief — the contraction of loss and longing) and rosa (anger — the reactive surge against what obstructs or threatens) are the two primary emotional drivers of suffering: the first oriented toward the past, the second toward the present. Their removal describes a mind that can be present with whatever arises without the distortion of these two forces.

谁已断除有漏，没有执取的恐惧，在世间的流动中保持平静，没有任何被夺走：他正确地游行。

「有漏」（āsava，漏，或译为流出物）是巴利文中最重要的烦恼概念之一，指欲漏、有漏、见漏、无明漏——从内心流出的染污。「没有执取的恐惧」描述了与无执相应的无惧：执取之所以产生恐惧，是因为所执着的东西可能失去；无执者没有什么可失去，因此没有恐惧。「在世间的流动中保持平静」是最后的画像：世间万物流动变化，正确游行的僧侣在这流动中保持稳定，没有任何东西能从他那里被夺走。

Whose taints are destroyed, who has no fear of clinging, who remains equanimous in the world's flux, who has nothing that can be taken away: he wanders rightly.

The āsavā (taints or outflows — sensual desire, existence, views, ignorance) are the deepest-rooted conditions that perpetuate rebirth. Their destruction marks the arahant. 'No fear of clinging' is the consequence of non-attachment: clinging generates fear because what is clung to can be lost; when there is nothing clung to, there is nothing to lose. The final image — remaining equanimous in the world's constant flux, with nothing that can be seized or taken — is the fullest portrait of the rightly-wandering monk: fully present, fully engaged with the world's movement, and utterly ungraspable.

总结 · Summary

正游行经以一个天神的问题开始：什么样的比丘能令诸天也敬礼？佛陀以十余颂回答，每颂均以「他正确地游行」（sammā paribbajeyya）作结，形成贯穿全诗的反复。「正确地游行」不是外在的行脚方式，而是内在的解脱状态：旗帜（骄慢）已倒下，负担（五蕴执取）已卸下，愤怒、慢、缚束已断除，执着、恐惧、忧患已灭，有漏已尽。正确游行的僧侣在世间的流动中保持稳定，无所依赖，无所失去——他的游行本身便是解脱在世间的显现。这首经是经集中对解脱者形象描述最为密集、最为系统的经典之一。

The Sammāparibbājanīya Sutta responds to a deity's question — what kind of monk do even the gods revere? — with a series of verses each closing with the refrain 'he wanders rightly.' Right wandering is not a matter of how one moves through the world physically; it is the inner state of liberation that makes all movement through the world an expression of freedom. Each verse catalogues what has been eliminated (conceit, anger, fetters, taints, fear, clinging) and what is positively present (equanimity, knowledge, non-dependence, peace). The portrait that emerges is of a monk who is fully in the world — wandering, encountering, engaging — while being utterly ungraspable by it: nothing can be taken from one who holds nothing. This is the most systematic characterisation of the liberated person in the Cūḷavagga.
