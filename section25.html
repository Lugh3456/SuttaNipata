第二品·第十三经 · 正经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第二品·第十二经 目录 Contents 第二品·第十四经 →

善加修习正道，而非如此的错误道路——那些沿着错误道路行走的人，在此处与彼处都不能到达涅槃。 正道是单一的，而错误的道路不是涅槃道——那些沿正道而行的比丘，使魔罗失望。 弯曲是木材的缺陷，锈是铁的损害，粪便是木材和灰土的缺陷——懈怠是精进者的损害。 不持戒是说法者的损害，散漫是美的损害，不正念是守护者的损害。 女人对那些追求出离者的损害，是吝啬——对布施者的损害是吝啬。 在此世界与来世，怯懦是缺陷——破戒的行为对于好名声是缺陷。 因此，比丘应该做好准备，在此处正确地生活——精进，快乐，了知，受过训练，有节制，有正念。

逐句解释

善加修习正道，而非如此的错误道路——那些沿着错误道路行走的人，在此处与彼处都不能到达涅槃。

正经（Sammā Sutta，「正」即「正确」）开篇以直接的陈述建立框架：正道（sammā patha，正确的道路）与邪道（邪道，错误的路）之间的区分是决定性的。「在此处与彼处」——此生与来世——邪道都不能通向涅槃。这不是相对主义的「各条路都能抵达」，而是明确地坚持道路的质量决定目的地的可达性。经集在此与其他经典一道，对「所有修行方式都等效」的观念提出质疑。

Cultivate the right path properly, not the wrong path like this — those who walk the wrong path cannot reach Nibbāna, here or beyond.

The Sammā Sutta's opening frames the entire teaching: path quality is not interchangeable. The right path (sammā patha) leads to Nibbāna; the wrong path does not, in this life or beyond. This is an explicit rejection of the idea that all spiritual approaches are equivalent in their destination. The distinction matters enormously — not as exclusivism but as a practical concern: effort expended on a path that does not lead to liberation is not merely ineffective but perpetuates the very condition (suffering) it is meant to address. The sutta invites honest evaluation of one's practice.

弯曲是木材的缺陷，锈是铁的损害，粪便是木材和灰土的缺陷——懈怠是精进者的损害。

经文以一系列「物质缺陷」的比喻描述修行中的损害：木材的弯曲、铁器的锈蚀、木柴与灰土中的污物——每一种都是使材料失去其用途的内在缺陷。「懈怠是精进者的损害」：懈怠（kosajja）对于致力于精进的修行者，就是这样一种内在的腐蚀——它不从外部破坏修行，而是从内部使精进失去效力。这组比喻以日常物质世界的经验说明抽象的道德缺陷，使其具体而可感。

Crookedness is the defect of timber; rust is the blemish of iron; dung is the fault of wood and ash — sloth is the blemish of the striving one.

The series of material defects operates through a clear logic: each named defect is something intrinsic to the material that undermines its function — crookedness makes timber unusable as a straight beam, rust destroys iron's utility, contamination makes fuel unusable. Sloth (kosajja) is presented as this kind of intrinsic undermining for the practitioner: it does not attack from outside but corrodes from within, making effort ineffective even when the external form of practice continues. The concreteness of the physical comparisons makes the spiritual defect viscerally tangible.

不持戒是说法者的损害，散漫是美的损害，不正念是守护者的损害。

第二组「缺陷」比喻针对具体角色：「说法者」（dhammakathika，讲法的人）若不持戒，其说法便失去权威与真实性——言行不符是对教导本身的损害；「美」因散漫（pamāda，放逸）而受损，因为美需要用心维护；「守护者」（ārakkhā，守卫）因不正念而失职——守卫若心不在焉，便无法真正守护。每一个损害都揭示了角色与其相应缺陷之间的内在矛盾：那正是最能破坏该角色的东西。

Non-virtue is the blemish of a Dhamma teacher; heedlessness is the blemish of beauty; inattentiveness is the blemish of a guard.

Each pairing identifies the specific defect that most undermines a particular role. A Dhamma teacher who does not practise what they teach is precisely contradicted by their own role — the teaching becomes hollow. Beauty maintained without care fades and fails. A guard who is not paying attention has lost the defining capacity of their role. The pattern teaches discernment about what the actual function of each position requires — and by extension, what the actual function of a practitioner's life requires. The defect is always the thing that specifically undermines the role, not a generic failing.

因此，比丘应该做好准备，在此处正确地生活——精进、快乐、了知、受过训练、有节制、有正念。

结语以六个品质的正面描述作结：精进（ātāpin，热诚）、快乐（sumanā，心情愉快）、了知（viditā，清楚知道）、受过训练（sikkhitā，有过训练的）、有节制（saṃyatā，自律节制）、有正念（satimā，具足正念）。这六个品质互为补充：精进提供动力，快乐提供内在的轻盈（修行不应是沉重的负担），了知提供清晰，训练提供能力，节制提供边界，正念提供持续的觉知。六者合在一起，描述了「在此处正确地生活」的全面图景。

Therefore a monk should be prepared, living rightly here — ardent, joyful, knowing, trained, restrained, with mindfulness.

The six qualities that define 'living rightly here' are carefully chosen as complements: ardour (ātāpin) provides energy; joy (sumanā) — notably included — prevents the path from becoming grim and self-punishing; knowing (viditā) ensures clarity rather than mechanical habit; training (sikkhitā) provides the cultivated capacity to carry out the practice; restraint (saṃyatā) provides the boundaries that protect practice from being overwhelmed; and mindfulness (satimā) provides the continuous awareness that integrates all the rest. The list is diagnostic: where a practitioner is struggling, one of these six is likely missing.

总结 · Summary

正经是一首关于辨别与品格的简洁经典。它以「正道与邪道」的区分开篇，随后以一系列「物质缺陷」的比喻描述修行中的损害：懈怠腐蚀精进，不持戒损害说法的权威，散漫败坏美，不正念使守护失职。每一组比喻都以内在最具破坏力的缺陷来定义特定角色的失败。经文以六个正面品质的列举作结——精进、快乐、了知、训练、节制、正念——为「在此处正确地生活」提供了一幅完整的图景。「快乐」（sumanā）被明确列入，提醒修行者：道路不仅需要力量，也需要内心的轻盈。

The Sammā Sutta is a compact teaching on discernment and character. Opening with the distinction between right and wrong path — both in destination and in what they require — it proceeds through a series of material-defect similes: sloth undermines the striving practitioner, non-virtue undermines the Dhamma teacher, heedlessness undermines beauty, inattentiveness undermines the guard. Each pairing identifies the defect that most specifically corrupts a given role. The sutta closes with six qualities for 'living rightly here': ardour, joy, knowing, training, restraint, and mindfulness. The explicit inclusion of joy (sumanā) is significant — the path requires not only strength but lightness, not only effort but a quality of gladness in the practice itself.
