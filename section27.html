第三品·第一经 · 出家经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第二品·第十四经 目录 Contents 第三品·第二经 →

我将歌颂出家——世尊是如何出家的，是怎样通过省思而出家的。 在家生活是局促的，是尘埃聚积的处所；出家是开阔的——见到了这一点，他出家了。 出家之后，他远离了恶的身业，他放弃了恶的语业，他净化了他的生活。 佛陀来到了摩揭陀国的王舍城——以胜相庄严的身形，进行托钵游行。 频婆沙罗王站在宫殿上看见了他，见到了相好庄严——他这样说道： 请注意这一位，他多么美丽，多么高大，多么清净——他所经行的路线是美好的，他的双眼低垂在犁沟之前不超过一轭。 这位比丘不是来自卑微的家庭——派遣使者去了解：这位比丘会去哪里？ 被派遣的使者跟随他，了解了「他向耆阇崛山去了」——这位有成就的圣人，向那岩石洞穴去了。 使者回报国王：「那位比主坐在那岩石山上，如一只狮子，如一只老虎，如一头从野地里来的狮子。」 那位战车英雄，频婆沙罗王，乘坐美丽的马车前往——他前往那耆阇崛山，走到马车所能到达的地方。 国王走近，问候了那位牟尼，坐下后，对他友好地交谈： 你还年轻，是青年，处于生命的第一段，具有好的身形——有着贵族的相貌，你看起来像第一排的战象。 我会给你军队的指挥，将财富分配给你——接受它！请告诉我你的出生。 【世尊】在雪山的脚下，有一个繁荣的国家，以太阳的后裔出名，他们是憍萨罗国人。 我属于那个家族，出生于释迦族——不是为了感官欲乐而出家。 见到了感官欲乐中的危险，见到了出离的安全——我将去努力，我的心欢喜于此。

逐句解释

在家生活是局促的，是尘埃聚积的处所；出家是开阔的——见到了这一点，他出家了。

出家经以第一人称叙事开篇，追溯世尊出家的决定与其后在王舍城的化缘行脚。「在家生活是局促的，是尘埃聚积的处所」（sambādho gharāvāso, rajassāyatanaṃ）是整个巴利文学中对在家生活最经典的描述——不是贬低在家生活，而是描述它的结构性局限：在家人必须维系家庭、财产、关系，这一切都产生「尘埃」（贪嗔痴的累积）。「出家是开阔的」（abbhokāso pabbajjā）：出家生活去除了这些结构性的依赖，提供了更大的内在空间。这是出家的动机——不是逃避，而是为了更大的开阔。

The household life is cramped, a path of dust; going forth is open air — seeing this, he went forth.

This phrase — sambādho gharāvāso ('the household is cramped') and abbhokāso pabbajjā ('the going-forth is open air') — is one of the most quoted characterisations of the difference between lay and monastic life in the Pali Canon. It appears in numerous suttas as the standard description of why the Buddha and others chose renunciation. 'Cramped' is not a moral condemnation of household life but a spatial-structural observation: maintaining a household requires constant management of possessions, relationships, and obligations, which inevitably generates friction, attachment, and the 'dust' of defilements. The going-forth removes these structural constraints, creating the internal spaciousness in which deep practice becomes possible.

频婆沙罗王站在宫殿上看见了他，见到了相好庄严——他多么美丽，多么高大，多么清净——他的双眼低垂在犁沟之前不超过一轭。

频婆沙罗王（Bimbisāra）在宫殿上观察托钵的未来佛陀，被他的外在相貌所吸引，但真正令国王印象深刻的是「双眼低垂」——这是正念行走的标志：不四处张望、不被感官刺激分散注意，目光垂下，专注当下。「不超过一轭」（yugamattaṃ）是传统的正念行走标准，描述一个具足戒律与正念的僧侣如何在世间行走——彻底临在于当下，不被外界拉扯。这个细节在出家经中具有重要的意义：真正令王动心的，不是外在的华丽，而是内在的定力。

King Bimbisāra, standing on his palace, saw him, saw his noble marks — how beautiful, how tall, how pure — his eyes cast down before him by no more than a yoke's length.

The description of the pre-enlightenment Bodhisatta's alms round is told through the king's eyes, which gives it an outsider's freshness. What strikes Bimbisāra is not first the physical beauty but the quality of movement: eyes cast down the distance of a yoke (yugamatta — the standard distance for mindful walking in Pali texts). This is the mark of a mind collected and present — not scanning the environment for stimulation or distraction but absorbed in the act of walking. The king's appreciation of this detail before he appreciates the teaching shows that practice is visible in comportment; the external form of mindful walking communicates the internal reality of a trained mind.

国王问：你还年轻，是青年——我会给你军队的指挥，将财富分配给你——接受它！请告诉我你的出生。

频婆沙罗王的提议是一种典型的世俗诱惑：权力、财富、地位——尤其是向一位明显来自贵族背景的年轻人提出这样的诱惑。国王的逻辑是合理的：这样出色的年轻人，应当在世间有所作为。这一提议给了佛陀一个机会来陈述他的出家理由——不是因为贫困，不是因为没有选择，而是因为他清晰地看见了感官欲乐的危险与出离的安全。出家是自由的选择，不是逃避。

The king: You are young, in the prime of life — I will give you command of armies, distribute wealth to you — accept it! Tell me of your birth.

Bimbisāra's offer is the world's best offer: wealth, power, armies — and it is made to someone who clearly comes from a noble background and could plausibly accept. The king is not wrong about the Bodhisatta's capacity for worldly leadership; the Bodhisatta's rejection is therefore not the rejection of someone who has no other option. It is a free choice made on the basis of clear seeing — seeing both the dangers of the sensual life and the security of the going-forth. This exchange establishes the going-forth as a fully voluntary renunciation of genuine alternatives, not a flight from deprivation.

【世尊】在雪山的脚下，有一个繁荣的国家，以太阳的后裔出名，他们是憍萨罗国人——我属于那个家族，出生于释迦族——不是为了感官欲乐而出家。见到了感官欲乐中的危险，见到了出离的安全——我将去努力，我的心欢喜于此。

佛陀的回答以家族自我介绍开始（释迦族，Sakya，出自憍萨罗国，Kosala，位于雪山——喜马拉雅山——的脚下），随后以两句话陈述出家的理由：「见到了感官欲乐中的危险」（kāmesu ādīnavaṃ disvā，看见了感官欲乐的过患）、「见到了出离的安全」（nekkhamme ca khemadassī，看见了出离的安全）。这两个「见到了」（disvā）是关键：出家是基于清晰的洞见，不是情绪的冲动，不是对世俗的失望，而是真实的认识——认识到感官欲乐的局限与出离道路的可靠。

The Blessed One: At the foot of the Himalayas, there is a prosperous country of the Kosalans, called the offspring of the Sun — I belong to that clan, born of the Sakyan family. Not for sensual pleasures did I go forth. Seeing the danger in sensual pleasures, seeing security in renunciation — I go to strive; my mind delights in this.

The self-identification — Sakyan, Kosalan, of the solar lineage — establishes unambiguously that the going-forth was not poverty or social failure. The two 'seeings' (disvā) are the pivot of the entire sutta: seeing the danger (ādīnava) in sense pleasures — the way they generate craving, which generates suffering — and seeing the security (khema) in renunciation — the peace that comes from not being subject to the constant need to have and hold. These are not philosophical positions adopted theoretically but direct perceptions. The Pabbajjā Sutta presents the Buddha's going-forth as the most important sight-seeing in human history: seeing these two things changed everything.

总结 · Summary

出家经以第一人称叙事的方式追溯世尊的出家经历：从出家的决定（「在家生活是局促的，出家是开阔的」）到在王舍城的化缘行脚，到频婆沙罗王在宫殿上的观察，到国王亲自前往耆阇崛山、以财富与权力诱惑的场景，再到佛陀以释迦族贵族身份的自我介绍与出家理由的陈述。经文的核心是两个「见到了」：见到感官欲乐的危险，见到出离的安全。这是佛教出家传统的起点——不是对世俗的绝望，不是贫困的逃避，而是清醒地看见了两条路的不同结局，选择了那条通向真正安全的路。

The Pabbajjā Sutta narrates the going-forth of the future Buddha in first-person verse — from the recognition that launched his renunciation ('the household is cramped; the going-forth is open air') through his alms-round in Rājagaha, Bimbisāra's observation from his palace, the king's personal visit to the mountain cave with offers of wealth and armies, to the Bodhisatta's identification of his Sakyan lineage and his clear statement of purpose. The pivot of the sutta is two seeings: the danger in sensual pleasure, and the security of the going-forth. These direct perceptions — not philosophical reasoning, not social failure — are the foundation of the Buddha's renunciation, and by extension, the foundation of the entire Buddhist tradition of the going-forth.
