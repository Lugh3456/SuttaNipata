第二品·第十经 · 起立经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第二品·第九经 目录 Contents 第二品·第十一经 →

起来！坐起来！沉睡对你有什么好处？对于那些被箭所击中、痛苦煎熬的人，还有什么睡眠可言？ 起来！坐起来！努力学习以得安宁——不要让死王知道你懈怠、被欲所缚，而欺骗了你。 越过这条天神与人类视之为可欲而停留其中的河流——不要错失良机，不要后悔地守候恶的时日。 此机会不会再来第二次。这个生命的消逝，就是苦的增长。此岸与彼岸，知道此时机，精进于法，不再令自己重生。

逐句解释

起来！坐起来！沉睡对你有什么好处？对于那些被箭所击中、痛苦煎熬的人，还有什么睡眠可言？

起立经以急迫的命令式开篇，语气在经集中是独一无二的——直接、甚至带有危机感的紧迫感。「被箭所击中」（sallena viddho，被箭刺穿）是佛教中表示苦的核心比喻：苦如箭，刺入心中；不拔箭而只是躺下睡觉，是荒诞的。「对于那些被箭击中的人，还有什么睡眠可言」——这是一个修辞性的反问，它说的是：只要苦还在，安逸的睡眠就是一种自我欺骗。精进修行是唯一合理的回应。

Rise up! Sit up! What is sleep to you? What sleep can there be for the one struck by an arrow, afflicted and suffering?

The Uṭṭhāna Sutta opens with an imperative — Rise! — that gives it an urgency unusual in the Sutta Nipata. The 'arrow' (salla) is one of the Canon's central images for suffering: the arrow of craving or grief, already lodged in the body. A person struck by a real arrow does not lie down and sleep; they seek extraction. Continuing to pursue comfort while the arrow of suffering is lodged is presented as exactly this kind of absurdity. The question — 'what sleep can there be?' — is rhetorical: there is none, if one is genuinely attending to one's situation.

起来！坐起来！努力学习以得安宁——不要让死王知道你懈怠、被欲所缚，而欺骗了你。

「死王」（Māra，魔罗）在此不仅是个人死亡，而是轮回与死亡的统治力量——他的领域是一切执取中存在的众生。「不要让死王欺骗了你」：放逸（pamāda）是死王的机会，懈怠开了一扇门让死亡不知不觉降临。「努力学习以得安宁」（sikkhama，学习；santi-pada，安宁之道）：修行的路径是主动的学习过程，安宁不是不劳而获的，而是通过持续的努力与学习而来。

Rise up! Sit up! Strive earnestly to learn for peace's sake — do not let the King of Death find you negligent, bound by desires, and deceive you.

Māra — the King of Death — represents the principle of mortality and the power of the cycle of existence over unawakened beings. His weapon is not force but negligence: he 'deceives' those who are inattentive, bound by desires, drifting in the assumption that there will always be more time. The sutta's urgency is built on the recognition that this assumption is exactly wrong. 'Striving to learn for peace's sake' frames the path as an active, ongoing education — not a once-for-all conversion but a sustained application of attention to the project of liberation.

越过这条天神与人类视之为可欲而停留其中的河流——不要错失良机，不要后悔地守候恶的时日。

「这条河流」是欲流（kāma-ogha）与有流（bhava-ogha）——渴望的洪流，将天神与人类统统淹没在「可欲而停留其中」的幻象里。天神（devā）同样被列为被欲望所困者——这强调了一点：即使是天界的存在，也仍然处于轮回之中，仍然需要渡越。「不要错失良机」：人身难得，遇法难遇——错失这个机会，将在「恶的时日」中悔恨，而那时再无法修行。

Cross over the flood that gods and humans find desirable and in which they linger — do not waste the opportunity; do not wait, regretting it, in evil times.

The flood that both gods and humans linger in — finding it desirable — is the stream of craving and existence. Even divine rebirth does not constitute crossing over; it merely represents a more comfortable form of being caught in the current. 'Evil times' (kāpurisa-kāla) refers to rebirths in conditions unfavourable for practice — states of deprivation, pain, or ignorance where the Dhamma cannot be heard or practised. The present moment, with access to the teaching and a human life in which practice is possible, is the opportunity that must not be squandered.

此机会不会再来第二次。这个生命的消逝，就是苦的增长。精进于法，不再令自己重生。

结语以两个不可逆的事实强调紧迫性：第一，这个机会（此生遇法的机会）不会再来；第二，生命的消逝——不是死亡的悲哀，而是时机的流失——就是苦的增长。「精进于法，不再令自己重生」（nappamajjeyya paṇḍito，智者不应放逸）是全诗的结论：了解无常与机会的短暂，智者便不会放逸，以修法结束再生的轮回。这短短四颂，是整个经集中最急迫的催促之一。

This opportunity will not come a second time. The passing of this life is the growth of suffering. Strive in the Dhamma; do not cause yourself to be born again.

The sutta closes with two irreversible facts that give force to its urgency: this opportunity — the convergence of human birth, access to the teaching, and sufficient conditions for practice — does not recur reliably; and the passage of time without practice is not neutral but represents an accumulation of causes for future suffering. 'Do not cause yourself to be born again' is the practical conclusion: understanding this situation, the wise person applies themselves to the practice that ends the cycle of rebirth. The Uṭṭhāna Sutta is one of the most compressed expressions of urgency in the entire Pali Canon.

总结 · Summary

起立经是经集中最短小、也最急迫的经典之一，仅四颂，却以罕见的紧迫感反复催促：起来、坐起来、精进修行。核心论证是对现实情境的清醒评估：苦如箭，已刺入心中，继续沉睡是荒诞的；死王在等待懈怠者；天神与人类都沉溺于欲望的洪流而不自知；此生遇法的机会不会再来第二次；时间的流逝本身就是苦的增长。面对这样的情境，只有一个合理的回应：精进于法，以此结束再生的轮回。这首经不是说教，而是急救——对一个被箭击中却还在考虑是否起身的人的紧急呼唤。

The Uṭṭhāna Sutta is among the most compressed and urgent texts in the entire Pali Canon — four verses, each building the case for immediate, earnest practice. The argument runs: suffering is already present (the arrow is already lodged); the King of Death exploits negligence; both gods and humans are caught in the desirable current of craving, not knowing they are drowning; this opportunity will not recur; and time passing without practice is not neutral but is the accumulation of further suffering. Against this diagnosis, only one response is rational: rise, attend to the teaching, practise with urgency. The sutta does not scold — it calls. It is an emergency summons to someone already wounded who is still weighing whether to get up.
