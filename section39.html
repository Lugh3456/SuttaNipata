第四品·第一经 · 欲经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

以下经文据汉译《义足经》（T0198，支谦译，三国·吴）

若人所欲，得其欲已，心实悦者—— 欲意得随，是人所喜，意得而乐。 若人所欲，念欲所爱—— 彼欲增长，若不如欲，箭所中伤，如毒矢射。 慎莫触欲，如蛇头触—— 踏蛇中毒，触欲亦然，二俱有苦。 田园屋宅，马牛鸡豚—— 妻妾婢仆，财物众多—— 欲意所爱，贪染不舍，势力弱劣，苦患侵之。 若不从心，如脚踏荆—— 小剌痛脚，欲亦如是，微触成大患。 所爱之欲，必当有忧—— 无欲则无忧，无忧则无惧，欲尽则安稳。 若人务欲，所愿得遂—— 彼得欲已，悦意而乐—— 若彼欲衰，如箭中身，苦痛难忍。 避欲犹如避蛇，远欲犹如渡河—— 彼岸无欲，无忧无惧，意专清净，此为牟尼道。

逐句解释

若人所欲，得其欲已，心实悦者——欲意得随，是人所喜，意得而乐。若人所欲，念欲所爱——彼欲增长，若不如欲，箭所中伤，如毒矢射。

欲经（Kāma Sutta）以一个心理分析开场：欲（kāma）在得到满足时给人快乐，这是真实的——经文不否认感官之乐的存在。但接下来立即揭示其结构：欲得到满足，欲望就增长（「彼欲增长」）；当欲望不被满足时，「如箭所中伤」——这是贯穿全经的核心意象：未满足的欲望是一支射入心中的毒箭。欲经的智识诚实在于：它先承认欲乐的真实性，然后分析其内在的脆弱机制。

If a person gets what they desire, they are truly pleased — following desire, it delights them, and having attained it, they are happy. But if desire grows and the wished-for thing does not come, one is wounded as if struck by an arrow, as if shot by a poisoned dart.

The Kāma Sutta opens with intellectual honesty: it acknowledges the genuine pleasure of satisfied desire before turning to the structure of suffering that desire entails. The arrow metaphor — desire as a poisoned dart lodged in the heart — recurs throughout the Aṭṭhakavagga. The teaching does not deny that sense-pleasures give pleasure; it tracks what that pleasure depends on (continued satisfaction) and what happens when the condition fails. The Aṭṭhakavagga's characteristic move is this: not moral condemnation of pleasure but precise analysis of its conditional nature.

慎莫触欲，如蛇头触——踏蛇中毒，触欲亦然，二俱有苦。田园屋宅，马牛鸡豚——妻妾婢仆，财物众多——欲意所爱，贪染不舍，势力弱劣，苦患侵之。

第二组意象是「蛇头」：触摸欲望如同触碰蛇头，无论踩着还是触碰，中毒是必然的结果。这个比喻的要点不是「欲望是邪恶的」，而是「欲望有其内在的毒性机制」——接触即中毒，不分意图。随后的列举（田园、屋宅、牲畜、妻妾、财物）是义足经特有的汉化细节，呈现的是古代中国家产制的图像，但内容与巴利文原偈吻合：这些都是人们执取、无法放手的对象，一旦失去就带来苦患。「势力弱劣，苦患侵之」——执取使人易受伤害。

Be careful not to touch desires as you would a snake's head — stepping on a snake one is poisoned; touching desire is the same, both bring suffering. Fields, houses, horses, cattle, chickens, pigs — wives, servants, and many possessions — these are loved by desire, soiled by clinging; those who cannot release them are weakened, assailed by affliction.

The snake-head simile conveys the inevitability of harm: touching the head of a snake brings poison regardless of intention. Desire operates the same way — not as a moral judgment but as a description of mechanism. The inventory of property (fields, livestock, household members) is characteristic of Zhi Qian's 3rd-century Chinese translation, domesticating the Indic context into the Chinese landed-household framework. The underlying Pali teaching remains intact: attachment to any of these is what creates vulnerability — 'weakness' here is the structural fragility of a life whose wellbeing depends on maintaining conditions it cannot control.

所爱之欲，必当有忧——无欲则无忧，无忧则无惧，欲尽则安稳。

这一颂是欲经的核心命题，呈现为一个逻辑链条：有欲→有忧；无欲→无忧；无忧→无惧；欲尽→安稳。「欲尽」（kāmānaṃ khaya，欲的消尽）不是强行压抑感官，而是欲望的自然消散——当对感官对象的渴望不再被执取维持，它自然止息。「安稳」（khema，安全、无危险）是佛教术语中对涅槃的描述之一，字面意义是「无危险之处」——一个内心不再受欲望驱使而处于危险之中的状态。

Whatever one loves through desire will bring worry — where there is no desire, there is no worry; without worry there is no fear; when desire is exhausted, one is secure.

The causal chain — desire → worry → fear → insecurity — is the sutta's central logical argument. 'Exhaustion of desire' (kāmānaṃ khaya) in Pali is not suppression but the natural dissolution of craving when clinging is no longer maintained. Khema — security, safety, the state of no danger — is one of the Pali Canon's standard epithets for Nibbāna: the place that is safe because nothing in it can be taken away. The chain runs in both directions: following desire leads to worry and fear; releasing desire leads to security and peace. The Kāma Sutta's argument is structural, not moralistic — it does not say desires are wicked but that they create a specific, describable form of vulnerability.

避欲犹如避蛇，远欲犹如渡河——彼岸无欲，无忧无惧，意专清净，此为牟尼道。

经文以渡河比喻收尾：远离欲望犹如渡过一条河流，到达彼岸——「彼岸无欲，无忧无惧」。这个意象与经集全书的核心意象呼应：彼岸（pāra）是对涅槃的常用比喻，此岸是充满苦难的世间，渡河是修行的过程。「意专清净」（citta-ekaggatā，心的一境性）表明欲尽之后的心不是空洞的，而是专注、清净的——这是禅定的基础状态。「此为牟尼道」：牟尼（muni，圣者）的道路，不是苦行，而是超越欲望之后的清净心境。

Avoiding desires as one avoids a snake, leaving desires behind as one crosses a river — on the far shore there is no desire, no worry, no fear; the mind is unified and pure. This is the path of the sage.

The closing image brings together the sutta's two central similes — the snake (danger of contact) and the river crossing (liberation as reaching the far shore). The 'far shore' (pāra) is the Pali Canon's most common spatial metaphor for Nibbāna: the place beyond the currents of craving, aversion, and delusion. 'Mind unified and pure' describes the condition of concentration (samādhi) that follows the dissolution of sense-craving — not emptiness but gathered, stable clarity. 'The path of the sage' (muni-magga) frames the whole teaching: this is not advice for householders managing their desires but a description of the complete liberation of one who has crossed over.

总结 · Summary

欲经是八偈品（Aṭṭhakavagga）的开篇，也是经集第四品的基调设定：以「欲」（kāma，感官欲望）为主题，以精准的心理分析代替道德谴责。经文承认欲望得到满足时的真实快乐，随即揭示其内在机制：欲得则增长，欲不得则如毒箭射入心中；执取田园财物使人弱劣易受苦患；欲越多，忧越多，惧越多。反向的逻辑链条是：无欲→无忧→无惧→安稳。经文以两个意象作结：避欲如避蛇（危险在于接触本身），渡欲如渡河（彼岸是无欲无惧的清净心境）。「此为牟尼道」——牟尼的路不是压抑欲望，而是欲尽之后的自然安稳。

The Kāma Sutta opens the Aṭṭhakavagga with the section's characteristic intellectual honesty: it does not moralize about desires but analyses their mechanism. Satisfied desire gives genuine pleasure — the sutta grants this. But desire, once satisfied, grows; when unsatisfied, it wounds like a poisoned arrow. Clinging to possessions creates vulnerability. The causal chain runs precisely: desire → worry → fear → insecurity; and in reverse: no desire → no worry → no fear → security (khema). The closing images — avoid desires as you avoid a snake, leave them behind as you cross a river — establish the Aṭṭhakavagga's spatial metaphor: the far shore is the destination, sense-craving is the current, and the sage who crosses arrives at a mind that is unified, pure, and no longer at risk.
