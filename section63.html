第五品·第八经 · 醯摩迦经

经集 · Sutta Nipata · 彼岸道品

← 第五品·第七经 目录 Contents 第五品·第九经 →

【醯摩迦问】 在我来找你之前，瞿昙—— 那些以前回答我的人—— 他们都说「它曾是，它将是」—— 这一切对我来说只是传统， 这一切只是增加了我对话的喜悦，没有令我满足。 现在，告诉我，灭除渴爱的法—— 了知它，以正念而行， 渡越世间的执取。 【世尊答】 醯摩迦，在这世间—— 所见、所闻、所感、所识的事物中—— 对这些的欲望与贪著—— 就是苦的来源。 不执取它们，正念的人， 在此已得灭苦—— 平静，在当下， 不再出生，不再老。

逐句解释

那些以前回答我的人——他们都说「它曾是，它将是」——这一切对我来说只是传统，只是增加了我对话的喜悦，没有令我满足。

醯摩迦经（Hemaka Sutta）的开场是整个彼岸道品中最直接的知识批评：之前的老师们给醯摩迦的答案都是「它曾是，它将是」——形而上学的断言，关于过去与未来的存在论命题。这些答案「只是传统，只是增加了我对话的喜悦」——没有解脱性的作用，只是扩大了谈论的内容，没有消除苦。醯摩迦的批评非常精准：他不是在指责这些老师无知，而是指出那些答案的功能性缺陷——它们只是增加了戏论（papañca），没有带来解脱。

Those who answered me before — they all said 'it was, it will be' — all of that for me was only tradition, all of it only increased my delight in discussion, it did not give me satisfaction.

Hemaka's opening critique is one of the most philosophically precise in the Pārāyanavagga: previous teachers' answers were metaphysical assertions — 'it was, it will be' — claims about past and future existence. These 'only increased delight in discussion' — the very proliferation of views (papañca) that the Aṭṭhakavagga diagnosed as the problem. Hemaka recognises that metaphysical knowledge does not deliver the liberation he is seeking; he turns to the Buddha for something different — not more doctrinal content but the practice that actually ends suffering.

在这世间——所见、所闻、所感、所识的事物中——对这些的欲望与贪著——就是苦的来源。不执取它们，正念的人，在此已得灭苦——平静，在当下，不再出生，不再老。

世尊的回答以「所见、所闻、所感、所识」（diṭṭha、suta、muta、viññāta）覆盖感知经验的四个层面——这是巴利经典中标准的感知枚举。「对这些的欲望与贪著」就是苦的来源——不是这些感知本身，而是对它们的欲望（chanda）与贪著（rāga）。「不执取它们，正念的人，在此已得灭苦」——「在此」（idh'eva，就在这里、就在此生）：解脱不是遥远的将来的事，而是当下可以实现的：「平静，在当下，不再出生，不再老」。这是整个彼岸道品中最简洁完整的解脱描述之一。

In this world — in what is seen, heard, sensed, and cognised — the desire and passion for these things is the origination of suffering. Not grasping at them, the mindful one — here has found the end of suffering — peaceful, in the present, not born again, not aged.

The four-fold enumeration of experience (seen, heard, sensed, cognised — the standard Pali canonical list) followed by 'desire and passion for these' as the source of suffering is the liberating diagnosis in its most direct form. 'Here' (idh'eva — right here, in this very life) is the crucial temporal marker: the end of suffering is not a post-mortem attainment but a present-tense possibility. 'Peaceful, in the present, not born again, not aged' — the sage who releases the desire-and-passion for perceptual experience achieves in the present life the quality that makes the possibility of renewed existence simply not arise. This is the Pārāyanavagga at its most concentrated: four lines of diagnosis, four lines of liberation.

总结 · Summary

醯摩迦经是彼岸道品中最简洁的经典之一，其力量来自其简洁。醯摩迦先批评之前的老师们给的形而上学答案（「它曾是，它将是」）——这些只是增加戏论，没有带来解脱。世尊的回答拒绝了形而上学框架，直接指向：对所见、所闻、所感、所识的欲望与贪著，就是苦的来源；不执取它们，正念的人「在此」（就在当下、就在此生）得灭苦——「平静，在当下，不再出生，不再老」。醯摩迦经用最少的文字完成了整部经集的核心教导：感知→欲望贪著→苦；不执取→灭苦→解脱。

The Hemaka Sutta achieves its power through extreme compression. Hemaka's rejection of metaphysical answers ('it was, it will be') as mere tradition that proliferates discussion without delivering liberation is one of the Pārāyanavagga's sharpest epistemic observations. The Buddha's response refuses the metaphysical framework entirely and goes directly to the functional mechanism: desire and passion for what is seen, heard, sensed, and cognised is the source of suffering; releasing that desire-and-passion right here, in this life, is the end of suffering. The 'here' and 'peaceful, in the present' are the Pārāyanavagga's most direct assertion that liberation is a present-tense possibility rather than a future-state promise.
