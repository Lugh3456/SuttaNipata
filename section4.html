第一品·第四经 · 耕田婆罗堕阇经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第一品·第三经 目录 Contents 第一品·第五经 →

如是我闻。一时，世尊住摩揭陀国南山，在一村落中。时，耕田婆罗堕阇婆罗门，正值播种之时，已备五百具犁。 清早，世尊整衣持钵，前往耕田婆罗堕阇婆罗门处。时，婆罗门正在分配食物。世尊走到食物分配处，站立一旁。 耕田婆罗堕阇婆罗门见世尊站立乞食，乃言：「沙门，我是耕田者，耕种之后方才饮食。你也应当耕种，耕种之后方才饮食。」 世尊言：「婆罗门，我也是耕田者，耕种之后方才饮食。」 婆罗门言：「然而我们看不见世尊的犁、轭、犁铧、刺棒，也看不见世尊的牛。世尊却说自己是耕田者？」 世尊以偈回答： 信心是种子，苦行是甘霖，智慧是我的犁与轭，惭愧是辕木，心是系带，正念是我的犁铧与刺棒。 善护身与语，饮食知节量，以真实为除草，柔顺是我的解缚。 精进是我的役牛，引我走向安稳；不退而直往，抵达无忧之处。 如此耕作，结出不死之果。一旦完成此耕作，便从一切苦中解脱。 于是耕田婆罗堕阇婆罗门以大铜碗盛满乳粥，奉献给世尊，说：「请世尊受此乳粥！世尊是耕田者，世尊所耕，结出不死之果。」 世尊言：以偈所得之物，不为我所应受；婆罗门，此非正见者之法。诸佛不以偈换取所得；此乃佛法，此是佛之行仪。 以完整之德、以断灭烦恼之法而活命的伟大圣者——另行供养饮食与饮料，因为此乃为那些寻求功德者的福田。 婆罗门问：「那么，世尊，我应将此乳粥给谁？」 世尊言：「婆罗门，在这有人类居住的世界中，我看不到任何人，无论天神、魔王、梵天、沙门婆罗门，能消受此乳粥而不受损者，除了如来或如来弟子。婆罗门，因此，你将此乳粥倒入无虫之水或无草之地中吧。」 婆罗门遂将乳粥倒入无虫之水中。乳粥入水，嘶嘶作响，冒出蒸气，如炽热犁铧投入水中。耕田婆罗堕阇婆罗门心生震惊、汗毛竖立，遂礼世尊双足，说：「奇哉！世尊，奇哉！善逝！如人将倒者扶起，将覆者揭开，为迷路者指路，在黑暗中举起灯火以令有眼者能见——世尊亦以种种方式阐明正法。我归依世尊、归依法、归依比丘僧。愿世尊受我为优婆塞，从今日起终身归依。」

逐句解释

【婆罗门】我是耕田者，耕种之后方才饮食；你也应当耕种，耕种之后方才饮食。

耕田婆罗堕阇是一位务实的婆罗门：他相信劳动应得报酬，不劳者不应得食。他以这样的逻辑质问持钵乞食的世尊，要求世尊自食其力。

Kasibhāradvāja: "I plow and I sow, and having plowed and sown I eat. You too, recluse, should plow and sow; having plowed and sown, you should eat."

The challenge is a good-faith one, not merely hostile. The brahmin operates within an economy where value is produced by physical labour. A wandering monk who accepts food without working for it appears, from this perspective, to be a parasite. The Buddha does not dismiss the challenge — he meets it directly and on its own terms, claiming to be a farmer too.

【世尊】我也是耕田者，耕种之后方才饮食。

世尊接受了婆罗门的框架，平静地声称自己也是耕田者。这句话令婆罗门困惑，因为他看不见任何耕具——于是他追问，世尊便以偈颂揭示所谓「耕田」的真正含义。

The Blessed One: "I too plow and sow, brahmin, and having plowed and sown I eat."

The response is deliberate provocation — not to mock but to invite. The Buddha accepts the same terms and claims the same identity: farmer. The brahmin is now forced to ask how, since no plow or oxen are visible. This is the pedagogical structure: create the question before giving the answer. The teaching that follows will redefine what farming means at the deepest level.

信心是种子，苦行是甘霖，智慧是我的犁与轭，惭愧是辕木，心是系带，正念是我的犁铧与刺棒。

世尊逐一揭示精神耕作的工具：信心是播下的种子，苦行（精进修行）是滋润种子的雨水，智慧是翻土的犁和引导的轭，惭愧（对不善的羞耻心）是支撑犁的辕木，心是连接一切的系带，正念是深入土地的犁铧和策励前进的刺棒。

Faith is the seed, austerity is the rain, wisdom is my yoke and plow, moral shame is the pole, mind is the strap, mindfulness is my plowshare and goad.

The extended metaphor is carefully constructed. Faith (saddhā) is the seed — without it nothing grows; it is the starting point of the entire enterprise. Austerity or restraint (tapa) waters the seed — practice must be sustained. Wisdom (paññā) does the actual work of breaking ground, just as the plow and yoke do in physical farming. Moral shame (hirī) — the internal sense that certain actions are beneath one's dignity — keeps the structure upright. Mindfulness is the sharpest tool: it penetrates (plowshare) and directs (goad) simultaneously.

善护身与语，饮食知节量，以真实为除草，柔顺是我的解缚。

守护身与语的行为，饮食有所节制，以真实（不妄语）为除去杂草的工具，以柔顺（不固执）为解开束缚的方式。

Guarded in body and speech, restrained in eating, using truthfulness as my weeding, and gentleness as my unbinding.

Weeds — defilements and unwholesome states — must be removed for the crop to flourish. Truthfulness is the tool that clears them: the truthful mind does not fabricate the self-serving narratives that sustain delusion. Restraint in eating is practical: the overfed mind is sluggish, the underfed is agitated; the middle way extends to what we put in the body as much as to what we cultivate in the mind.

精进是我的役牛，引我走向安稳；不退而直往，抵达无忧之处。

精进是拉动一切的牛，引领我走向安稳之地；不转身、不退却，笔直前行，抵达无忧无苦之所。

Energy is my draft ox that carries me to security; it goes without turning back, to the place where there is no grief.

The ox is energy (viriya) — the sustained effort that actually powers all the other tools. Without the ox, the finest plow sits idle. Energy 'goes without turning back': unlike a person who starts with enthusiasm and loses momentum, genuine spiritual energy is directional and persistent. Its destination — the place without grief — is Nibbāna.

如此耕作，结出不死之果；一旦完成此耕作，便从一切苦中解脱。

以上所述的精神耕作，结出的是不死（涅槃）之果；这样的耕作一旦圆满完成，便从一切苦痛中彻底解脱。

This is how the plowing is plowed; it bears the fruit of the deathless. Having completed this plowing, one is released from all suffering.

The metaphor reaches its conclusion: physical farming produces grain; spiritual farming produces the deathless (amata) — Nibbāna. The Buddha has answered the brahmin's challenge completely and has simultaneously given a compressed account of the entire path. Every element of practice has been assigned its place in the agricultural scheme, and the harvest has been named.

【婆罗门】以偈所得之物，不为我所应受；此非正见者之法。诸佛不以偈换取所得。

婆罗门以大碗乳粥相赠，世尊婉拒：用偈颂换取物质供养，这不是正见者的做法；诸佛不以法语换取报酬。

The Blessed One: "What is obtained by reciting verses is not fitting for me to accept. This, brahmin, is not the way of those who see rightly. The Buddhas do not accept payment in exchange for verses."

The refusal is principled and important. The Dhamma is not a commodity — it cannot be exchanged for food, money, or gratitude. To accept payment for teaching would corrupt the teaching itself, turning it into a transaction and introducing the teacher's material interest into the relationship. The Buddha's teaching is given freely; its fruits belong to the practitioner, not to the teacher.

乳粥入水，嘶嘶作响，冒出蒸气——婆罗堕阇汗毛竖立，礼世尊双足，归依三宝。

乳粥倒入水中，如烧红的犁铧入水般嘶嘶作响、蒸气升腾——这神奇的现象令婆罗堕阇震惊敬畏，当下礼拜世尊，皈依佛、法、僧三宝，成为终身优婆塞。

The milk-rice was dropped into the water and steamed and hissed, as a hot plowshare dropped into water steams and hisses. Kasibhāradvāja, awestruck, took refuge in the Buddha, the Dhamma, and the Sangha.

The marvel of the milk-rice — suffused with the power of the encounter — seals the brahmin's conversion. The sutta does not explain the phenomenon; it simply records it. What matters is its effect: wonder breaking through the rationalist's armour, opening space for a different kind of knowing. The brahmin's final words — 'as one who lifts the fallen, uncovers the hidden, shows the lost their way, lights a lamp in the dark' — became a standard formula for taking refuge throughout the tradition.

总结 · Summary

耕田婆罗堕阇经以一场实际的田间相遇为背景，展开了一场精彩的智识对话。婆罗门的质疑并非出于恶意，而是出于一种朴素的劳动伦理；世尊没有否定这种伦理，而是将其升华：以信心为种子，以智慧为犁，以精进为牛，以解脱为收成，描绘了一幅完整的精神耕作图景。经文的核心教导在于：不以法换利，不将修行商品化——法的给予是无条件的，其果实不归属于给予者。婆罗堕阇最终皈依三宝，不是因为被说服，而是因为被感动——乳粥入水的神奇，让他的理智防线瞬间瓦解，真正的归依由此而生。

The Kasibhāradvāja Sutta is one of the most structurally elegant suttas in the collection: a real-world challenge met with a complete, internally consistent metaphor. The brahmin's question is honest — why should a non-worker eat? — and the Buddha's answer honours the question by accepting its terms entirely. His farming is real farming; it produces a real crop. The extended agricultural metaphor maps every element of the path onto a corresponding element of the field: seed, rain, plow, yoke, ox, harvest. The result is a complete account of practice in a single verse passage. The sutta's second teaching — the refusal of payment for the verses — is equally important: the Dhamma cannot be commodified. It is given because giving is the nature of compassion, not as an exchange. The brahmin's awestruck conversion at the end completes the picture: sometimes it is not argument but encounter that opens the heart.
