第一品·第九经 · 雪山夜叉经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第一品·第八经 目录 Contents 第一品·第十经 →

萨多伽拉夜叉问雪山夜叉：今日是十五布萨日，这夜乃是神圣之夜，我们去见那位著名的乔达摩吧——他是完全觉悟的佛陀吗？ 雪山夜叉答：他的心是否平等对待一切众生？他是否能节制欲望？他是否已渡过深渊？如同商主之良驹，他不再落后了吗？ 萨多伽拉答：他的心平等对待一切众生，他能节制欲望，他已渡过深渊；如同商主之良驹，他不再落后。 雪山夜叉：他是否不偷取未予之物？他是否离欲远行？他是否安住于禅定之中？他的心是否不执着于欲乐？ 萨多伽拉：他不偷取未予之物，他离欲远行，他安住于禅定之中，他的心不执着于欲乐。 雪山夜叉：他是否不说虚假之语？他是否粗语？他是否远离两舌之语？他是否守护自己的言语？ 萨多伽拉：他不说虚假之语，他不粗语，他远离两舌之语，他守护自己的言语，说的都是真实、有益的话语。 雪山夜叉：他是否无有贪欲？他是否不生嗔恨？他是否已超越愚痴？他的心是否已清净？ 萨多伽拉：他无有贪欲，他不生嗔恨，他已超越愚痴，他的心完全清净——佛陀的心无有任何污染。 雪山夜叉：他的心是否具足知见？他是否具备牟尼之智？他是否从一切漏中解脱？他是否不再有任何存有的余留？ 萨多伽拉：他的心具足知见，他具备牟尼之智，他从一切漏中解脱，他不再有任何存有的余留——他就是佛陀，他的成就无可置疑。 雪山夜叉：那么，我们去拜见牟尼吧！他具足美好之行，从一切欲中解脱，如良马渡过洪流，具慧、具智，令人欢喜——愿他接受我们的拜访。 两位夜叉来到世尊面前，礼拜，雪山夜叉问： 何为「世间」？何为「系缚世间」之物？世间被何所舍弃？何为世间的「涅槃」？ 世尊答：六处为世间，六处系缚世间，六处被舍弃，六处的息灭即为涅槃。 雪山夜叉问：以何渡过洪流？以何渡过大海？以何超越苦痛？以何得到清净？ 世尊答：以信渡过洪流，以不放逸渡过大海，以精进超越苦痛，以智慧得到清净。 雪山夜叉问：云何获得智慧？云何获得财富？云何获得名声？云何系缚朋友？在离开此世之后，云何不生忧愁？ 世尊答：信仰善法、不放逸、精进——以此获得智慧；以此获得财富；以此获得名声；以此系缚朋友。在家弟子若如此行持，便不会在离开此世之后生起忧愁。 其他人若来问你：还有什么其他值得询问的吗？——在家弟子应当如此回答：如何避免忧愁与苦痛？如何在此世中解脱？ 世尊为雪山夜叉说法：诸漏已尽、善修正道、在此最后身中行持的如来——他是一位了知世间的智者，对于存有的问题，他不会产生任何犹豫。 雪山夜叉赞叹：我见到了完全觉悟的佛陀！他已越过一切恐怖，他住于正念之中；他超越了一切苦痛，他断除了一切执取——我皈依世尊！

逐句解释

【萨多伽拉与雪山夜叉对话】他是完全觉悟的佛陀吗？他的心是否平等对待一切众生？

两位夜叉（yakkhā，自然精灵）在布萨日的神圣夜晚相遇，讨论是否该去拜见乔达摩。他们相互验证：世尊是否真的具备觉者的品质？这段验证本身就是一种教导——真正的觉悟者，应当经得起问询与检验。

Sātāgira and Hemavata, two yakkhas, meet on the sacred night of the full-moon observance. Is Gotama fully enlightened? Is his mind evenly disposed toward all beings?

The sutta's opening is unusual: instead of a human questioning the Buddha, two non-human beings verify his qualities before approaching. The checklist they use — equanimity toward all beings, sense restraint, having crossed the flood, restraint in speech, absence of the three roots of unwholesomeness — is in effect a profile of arahantship. Their exchange functions as an external character witness before the meeting that follows.

【六问六答】心平等、节制欲望、渡过深渊、不偷取、离欲禅定、言语清净。

两位夜叉以六组问答，逐项核查世尊是否具备觉者的品质：心的平等性、欲望的节制、渡过轮回之渊、不盗、安住于禅定、四种语业的清净（不妄语、不粗语、不两舌、守护言语）。

Six rounds of questioning: equanimity, restraint, crossing the abyss, non-theft, meditative dwelling, and purity in all four aspects of speech — false, harsh, divisive, and unguarded.

The six exchanges constitute a thorough vetting. What is striking is how practical the criteria are — not metaphysical claims about cosmic status but observable qualities of conduct and character. The four aspects of speech purity (no false speech, no harsh speech, no divisive speech, guarded speech) are particularly detailed, reflecting the Canon's consistent emphasis on speech as the most immediate and consequential form of action in daily life.

何为「世间」？何为「系缚世间」之物？世间被何所舍弃？何为世间的「涅槃」？

雪山夜叉见到世尊后，直接提出四个根本性的哲学问题：世间是什么？是什么系缚了它？如何舍弃？什么是世间意义上的涅槃？

What is the world? What binds the world? What is the world abandoned by? What is called the world's Nibbāna?

Four questions about the nature of experience, its bondage, its relinquishment, and its cessation. The Buddha's answer — that all four questions are answered by 'the six sense bases' — is compact and radical. The world is not an external reality 'out there' but the totality of experience arising at the six sense doors: eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. This is not metaphysical idealism but a phenomenological claim: the 'world' we inhabit is the world of experience, constituted by the meeting of sense faculty and sense object.

【世尊】六处为世间，六处系缚世间，六处被舍弃，六处的息灭即为涅槃。

世尊以「六处」（六根：眼、耳、鼻、舌、身、意）回答了四个问题：六处是世间本身，六处是世间的束缚，舍弃对六处的执取即是超越世间，六处活动的息灭（涅槃）即是彻底的平息。

The six bases are the world; the six bases bind the world; the six bases are what the world is abandoned by; the cessation of the six bases is called Nibbāna.

One answer for all four questions: the six sense bases (āyatana). This is a characteristic Buddha move — the apparent multiplicity of questions resolves to a single framework. The 'world' in Buddhist phenomenology is the world of sensory and mental experience, not a metaphysical realm. It binds through craving and clinging at the sense bases. It is abandoned by releasing that clinging. Nibbāna is the cessation of the agitated, craving-driven activity at the sense bases — not the destruction of experience, but its radical pacification.

以信渡过洪流，以不放逸渡过大海，以精进超越苦痛，以智慧得到清净。

世尊以四种「渡越」来回答四个问题：信（saddhā）渡过洪流，不放逸（appamāda）渡过大海，精进（viriya）超越苦痛，智慧（paññā）得到清净。每一个「渡越」都对应一种修行品质，也对应一种境界的跨越。

By faith one crosses the flood; by heedfulness, the sea; by energy, one surmounts suffering; by wisdom, one is purified.

Four means for four crossings. Faith (saddhā) is not blind belief but the confidence that comes from having seen enough truth to trust the path — it provides the motivation to enter the flood at all. Heedfulness (appamāda) — often translated as non-negligence or diligence — is the quality that keeps one present and attentive through the long crossing of the sea. Energy (viriya) is the sustained exertion that carries one through suffering. Wisdom (paññā) is the final purification — the direct seeing that completes the crossing.

信仰善法、不放逸、精进——以此获得智慧、财富、名声；以此系缚朋友；以此离世不生忧愁。

世尊在此将灵性修行的品质与世俗生活的利益连接起来：同样的信、不放逸、精进，不仅带来智慧（灵性利益），也带来财富、名声和朋友（世俗利益），并使人在死后无有忧愁。修行与世俗生活并不对立。

Having faith in the good Dhamma, diligent and energetic — one acquires wisdom, wealth, fame, and friends; departing this world, one does not grieve.

The sutta here addresses the lay Buddhist's life directly: the same qualities that lead to liberation also produce worldly flourishing. This is not a contradiction but a coherent view of human action. Faith in the good Dhamma, diligence, and energy are not just spiritual virtues — they are the qualities that build a trustworthy, productive, and well-respected human life. The practitioner who cultivates them gains both: a life well-lived in the world and a death without regret.

【雪山夜叉】我见到了完全觉悟的佛陀！他越过了一切恐怖，住于正念，断除了一切执取——我皈依世尊！

雪山夜叉在聆听完世尊的教导后，以发自内心的赞叹和皈依作结：见到了真正的觉者，一切疑惑随之消散，皈依三宝由此生起。这种转化是自然的——不是被说服，而是被所见所感动。

Hemavata: "I have seen the fully awakened Buddha! He has crossed beyond all terror, dwells in mindfulness, has cut off all clinging — I take refuge in the Blessed One!"

Hemavata's conversion follows the same pattern as Kasibhāradvāja's and others in the collection: the encounter with genuine awakening produces awe and refuge-taking, not merely intellectual agreement. The three qualities Hemavata names — beyond terror, dwelling in mindfulness, all clinging cut — echo the qualities he verified at the start of the sutta. The circle closes: what was initially a checklist has become a lived encounter. He takes refuge not because he was argued into it but because he saw it.

总结 · Summary

雪山夜叉经是《经集》第一品中篇幅最长的经典，以两位夜叉的夜间对话为开端，展开了一场从验证、问道到皈依的完整叙事。萨多伽拉与雪山夜叉在布萨日的神圣夜晚，以一系列品质核查来验证世尊是否真的是觉者——这种「先验证、再拜访」的结构，本身就是一种智慧的态度。经文的核心教导在于两组问答：六处是世间的本质与系缚，信、不放逸、精进、智慧是渡越的方式。最后，雪山夜叉皈依世尊，以亲身所见印证了他最初的检验——世尊确实是他们所寻找的那位觉者。

The Hemavata Sutta is the longest text in Vagga 1 and structurally the most elaborate. Two yakkhas — non-human beings of the wild — conduct a careful vetting of the Buddha's qualities before approaching him, which is itself a teaching: spiritual authority should be verifiable, not merely claimed. The core exchanges address two fundamental questions: what is the world (answered: the six sense bases) and how is it crossed (answered: faith, heedfulness, energy, wisdom). The connection between spiritual and worldly benefit in the final exchange is practically important: the Buddha does not present practice as opposed to flourishing in daily life — faith, diligence, and energy produce both. Hemavata's closing refuge-taking completes the arc from intellectual verification to direct encounter to committed refuge.
