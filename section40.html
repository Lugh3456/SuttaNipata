第四品·第二经 · 洞穴八偈

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第四品·第一经 目录 Contents 第四品·第三经 →

以下经文据汉译《义足经》（T0198，支谦译，三国·吴）

人身如空窟，爱著在其中—— 愚者不能舍，转转生苦恼。 所嗜在爱欲，恒随世间人—— 取著世间苦，难得出离心。 一切欲所覆，不见己过失—— 贪著诸有为，悉在欲网中。 若人慕世乐，欲得而复失—— 如鱼吞钩饵，沉没难自拔。 贪著世间者，忧苦随逐之—— 若能舍爱著，忧苦不相随。 众生处世间，如蛇在洞穴—— 执著于自见，难离自见网。 贤圣无取著，于法不争论—— 彼岸已超越，此岸更无忧。 知苦知苦灭，超越此彼岸—— 牟尼所行道，无取无所著。

逐句解释

人身如空窟，爱著在其中——愚者不能舍，转转生苦恼。

洞穴八偈（Guhaṭṭhaka，「洞穴·八偈」）的核心意象：人的身体（或心理的内在世界）被比作一个洞穴（guhā），执著（upādāna）藏在其中。这是八偈品中最深邃的意象之一——洞穴不是身体本身，而是内心对身体、对自我、对存在的执著所形成的「窟居」。愚者住在这个洞穴中，不肯出来，「转转生苦恼」：每一次执著都深化了苦，每一次轮回都是对洞穴的进一步深陷。

The body is like an empty cave, with attachment dwelling within — the fool cannot release it, and suffering multiplies turn by turn.

The cave (guhā) is the Guhaṭṭhaka's central and most distinctive image. In Pali, guhā refers both to a physical cave and to the body as the 'cave' in which the mind-consciousness dwells — a traditional Indic metaphor for the enclosed, defended space of individual selfhood. What makes the cave a trap is not the body itself but the attachment that takes up residence within it: the clinging to bodily experience, to a defended sense of self, to the comfort of interior walls. The fool who cannot leave the cave finds suffering compounding with each turn — each act of clinging reinforces the structure that generates it.

一切欲所覆，不见己过失——贪著诸有为，悉在欲网中。众生处世间，如蛇在洞穴——执著于自见，难离自见网。

洞穴八偈的独特深度在于它将「欲」的分析扩展到「见」（diṭṭhi，见解、观点）的执著：「执著于自见，难离自见网」——不仅是对感官享乐的执著，连对自己观点、见解的执著，也是一种将人困在洞穴中的绳索。「自见」（attā-diṭṭhi，我见，或更广泛的「自己的见解」）的执著是八偈品整体最关注的主题：对任何见解的「我这样认为，这是真理」的执著，都是一种系缚。

Covered by all desires, one does not see one's own faults — clinging to all conditioned things, entirely caught in the net of desire. Living beings in the world are like snakes in a cave — clinging to their own views, difficult to leave the net of self-views.

The extension from sense-desire to view-clinging is the Guhaṭṭhaka's distinctive move, and it is central to the entire Aṭṭhakavagga's agenda. Not only craving for sensory pleasure but clinging to one's own views — 'this is how things are, this is true, this is what I think' — constitutes a cave that traps the mind. Diṭṭhi-upādāna (clinging to views) is one of the four forms of clinging in Pali analysis, and the Aṭṭhakavagga is particularly focused on its subtlety: one can be entirely free of sensual craving and still be trapped in the cave of view-identity.

贤圣无取著，于法不争论——彼岸已超越，此岸更无忧。

八偈品的贤圣（ariya，圣者）形象在这一颂中清晰呈现：「于法不争论」——圣者不执取任何见解到要与人争论的程度。这是八偈品最具挑战性的教导之一：连「对法的正确理解」也不应被执取到形成争论的程度。「彼岸已超越」：证得解脱的圣者已到达彼岸；「此岸更无忧」：即使还在此岸，也没有忧虑——因为执著的消失，忧虑的根源也随之消失。

The noble one has no clinging, does not dispute about the Dhamma — having crossed to the far shore, there is no more worry even on this shore.

Non-contention (arana, 'non-strife') is one of the Aṭṭhakavagga's most characteristic teachings. The noble one does not cling to the Dhamma itself as a position to be defended in argument — even correct understanding, when grasped as 'my view,' becomes a form of attachment. This is a demanding standard: it means that doctrinal confidence, when it generates the urge to win arguments or defeat opponents, has crossed into clinging. The teaching is not skepticism or relativism — the noble one has crossed to the far shore — but a warning that the final step requires releasing even the raft.

知苦知苦灭，超越此彼岸——牟尼所行道，无取无所著。

结颂将洞穴八偈的教导收束为牟尼（圣者、沉默者）的道路：「知苦知苦灭」——直接了知苦的生起与消灭（与二随观经的结构呼应）；「超越此彼岸」——超越了此岸（samsāra，轮回的世界）与彼岸（nibbāna，涅槃）的分别——「无取无所著」才是最终的状态：不执取任何一边。这种「不执取涅槃」的教导，是八偈品中最微妙的教导：连解脱本身也不应被执取为一个「我已得到的东西」。

Knowing suffering, knowing the cessation of suffering, having crossed beyond this shore and the far shore — the path walked by the sage: no grasping, nothing clung to.

The final verse introduces the Aṭṭhakavagga's most subtle teaching: the sage who has truly arrived does not cling even to Nibbāna. 'Crossing beyond this shore and the far shore' — beyond saṃsāra and beyond a reified Nibbāna as a destination to possess — is the complete non-grasping that ends the Guhaṭṭhaka. This is not nihilism: suffering and its cessation are known directly. But the knowing does not become a new form of identity or possession. The path of the sage (muni-paṭipadā) is therefore characterised entirely negatively: no grasping, nothing clung to — the cave is empty because no one moved in.

总结 · Summary

洞穴八偈是八偈品中哲学深度最高的经典之一。其核心意象——人身如洞穴，执著藏其中——将苦的根源定位于内心的「占据」（upādāna，执取）：不仅是对感官享乐的执著，也包括对自己见解（自见）的执著。这是八偈品的标志性主题：「于法不争论」的圣者，是一个连对正确教法的执著也已放下的人。经文的结颂将这个教导推向极致：牟尼的道路是「超越此彼岸」——不执取轮回，也不执取涅槃，连解脱本身也不被执取为「我所拥有的成就」。这是整个八偈品的哲学核心：彻底的非执取，包括对非执取本身的非执取。

The Guhaṭṭhaka is philosophically the richest of the Aṭṭhakavagga's opening suttas. Its central metaphor — the body as cave, attachment as its occupant — locates suffering in the internal act of taking up residence: not just in sensual craving but in the clinging to one's own views that makes the cave feel like home. The noble one who 'does not dispute about the Dhamma' has released even correct understanding from the grip of identity. The closing verse pushes this to its limit: the sage crosses beyond both this shore and the far shore — beyond a reified saṃsāra and a reified Nibbāna, beyond the dualism that makes liberation into a possession. The cave is empty not because someone left but because no one was ever truly there to occupy it.
