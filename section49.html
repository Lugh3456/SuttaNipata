第四品·第十一经 · 诤论经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第四品·第十经 目录 Contents 第四品·第十二经 →

以下经文据汉译《义足经》（T0198，支谦译，三国·吴）

诤论喜乐哀——从何处而生？ 离别忧愁悲——从何处而来？ 诸乐从何生？世间诸苦恼——何者为其因？ 乐从欲而生，诤论从乐生—— 离别忧愁悲，从诤论而来—— 诤论生世间，从乐欲而起。 世间诸乐欲，从何处而生？ 贪欲从何起？来世苦从何？ 世间欲乐者，其欲从想生—— 贪欲从想生，想从何处来？ 想从触而生，若无有触者—— 想不得生起，乐苦皆寂灭。 触从何处生？若人如是问—— 触从名色生，此为触之因。 名色从何生？欲从何处起？ 名色由识生，欲由受而起。 识由行而生，行由痴而起—— 痴是诸行因，此是轮回根。 知此缘起故，善观诸缘起—— 圣者断此缘，苦恼不相随—— 此名为解脱，此名为牟尼。

逐句解释

诤论喜乐哀——从何处而生？离别忧愁悲——从何处而来？

诤论经（Kalahavivāda Sutta，kalaha = 诤论/争吵，vivāda = 论辩）以一系列「从何处来？」的追问开场，引发一段对苦的根源的系统性溯源——这是整个八偈品中结构最接近缘起论（paṭicca-samuppāda）分析的经典。问题层层深入：诤论、喜乐哀、离别忧悲——这些苦从哪里来？答案的推演将读者带入一个完整的缘起链条，从现象表面（争论）追溯到最深处（痴，无明）。这种溯源式结构在经集中是独特的。

From where do contentions, joys, sorrows arise? From where do separation, grief, and lamentation come?

The Kalahavivāda Sutta is the Aṭṭhakavagga's most analytically structured text, and one of the Sutta Nipata's closest approaches to the formal dependent origination analysis (paṭicca-samuppāda). The series of 'from where?' questions initiates a regressive inquiry that will trace contention through desire, perception, contact, name-and-form, consciousness, formations, and finally to ignorance. This reverse-engineering of suffering from surface phenomena to root cause mirrors the analysis in the Mahānidāna Sutta and other discourses, but here it is pursued in verse with the Aṭṭhakavagga's characteristic concision.

世间欲乐者，其欲从想生——贪欲从想生，想从触而生——若无有触者，想不得生起，乐苦皆寂灭。

缘起链的中段：欲望（kāma）从想（saññā，感知/概念）而生；想从触（phassa，感官接触）而生——「若无有触者，想不得生起，乐苦皆寂灭」：如果没有感官接触，感知就无法生起，快乐与苦也随之寂灭。这个洞见将痛苦的根源定位于感官接触的层面，但并不是说「应当避免所有接触」——而是说：接触本身是中性的，是接触所产生的想（带着喜好与厌恶的感知）才是欲望的直接来源。

The desires of worldly beings arise from perception — craving arises from perception; perception arises from contact. If there were no contact, perception would not arise — pleasure and pain would both be stilled.

The middle links of the chain — desire from perception, perception from contact — locate the origin of suffering in the cognitive processing of sense experience rather than in the sense experience itself. Contact is not the problem; it is the neutral event from which everything subsequent can arise. Perception (saññā), however, is not neutral: it comes pre-loaded with value-attributions, aesthetic judgments, and habitual responses that feed craving. 'If there were no contact' is not an instruction to seek sensory deprivation but an analytical demonstration: trace the chain to its origin and the root becomes clear.

识由行而生，行由痴而起——痴是诸行因，此是轮回根。知此缘起故，善观诸缘起——圣者断此缘，苦恼不相随——此名为解脱，此名为牟尼。

缘起链的最深处：识由行（saṅkhāra，意志构成/业力）而生，行由痴（avijjā，无明）而起——「痴是诸行因，此是轮回根」：无明是整个缘起链的起点，也是轮回的根源。「知此缘起故，善观诸缘起——圣者断此缘，苦恼不相随」：知道这个链条是怎么运作的，并且「善观」（anupassati，随观）——这个知道本身就是断链的力量：当无明被看见，链条就开始松动。「此名为解脱，此名为牟尼」：看见缘起并从中解脱的人，就是牟尼，就是解脱。

Consciousness arises from formations; formations arise from delusion — delusion is the cause of formations, this is the root of the cycle. Knowing this dependent arising, well-observing all dependent arisings — the noble one cuts this chain; suffering does not follow — this is called liberation, this is called sage.

The chain bottoms out at ignorance (avijjā) — the root of formations, and through them of the entire conditioned process. The term 'well-observing' (anupassati) is significant: it is not intellectual understanding alone but the sustained, direct observation of the chain's arising and passing that constitutes the liberating practice. The noble one who 'cuts the chain' does so through this observing — not through an act of will but through the quality of seeing that ignorance, once seen through, cannot maintain. Liberation is the natural result of genuinely seeing what was previously unseen.

总结 · Summary

诤论经是八偈品中结构最接近缘起论分析的经典。从表面现象（诤论、喜乐哀、离别悲愁）层层追溯，经文展开一条完整的缘起链：诤论←乐←欲←想←触←名色←识←行←痴（无明）。每一个问题都引向下一个更深的问题，直到抵达「痴是诸行因，此是轮回根」。解脱的路径与缘起链的方向相反：「知此缘起，善观诸缘起——圣者断此缘，苦恼不相随」。诤论经将八偈品的「非争论」主题与缘起分析结合：争论是表象，其根源是欲望、感知、接触，其最深的根是无明——看见这个链条，就是打开解脱之门。

The Kalahavivāda Sutta is the Aṭṭhakavagga's most analytically structured text, tracing the origin of contention through a full dependent origination chain: contention ← pleasure ← desire ← perception ← contact ← name-and-form ← consciousness ← formations ← ignorance. The regressive inquiry form — 'from where does X arise?' — mirrors the paṭicca-samuppāda analysis of the Mahānidāna and related discourses. The liberating move is not escaping from contact but seeing the chain clearly: when ignorance is observed rather than acted from, the chain loses its grip. The Aṭṭhakavagga's theme of non-contention is here given its deepest root: contention is not a social failing but a downstream consequence of a chain that begins in unknowing.
