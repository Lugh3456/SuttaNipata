第一品·第七经 · 贱民经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第一品·第六经 目录 Contents 第一品·第八经 →

如是我闻。一时，世尊清晨整衣持钵，入舍卫城乞食。时，火神婆罗堕阇婆罗门正在生火供祭。世尊见之，前往其处。婆罗堕阇见世尊到来，言道：「停下，秃头！停下，贱民！停下，旃陀罗！」 世尊言：「婆罗门，你知道何为贱民，何为造成贱民之法吗？」婆罗门言：「不知。请乔达摩为我说明，使我了解何为贱民与造成贱民之法。」 世尊以偈说道： 易怒、怀恨、恶毒、嫉妒、虚伪——此人为贱民。 伤害生命、无有悲悯心——此人为贱民。 毁坏村庄与城市、压迫众人——此人为贱民。 在村落或荒野中，偷取他人未予之物——此人为贱民。 借了钱财，被催讨时说「我没有欠你的」——此人为贱民。 为了一点点财物，在路上杀害路人并夺取其物——此人为贱民。 被他人请求出庭作证，为自己或他人的利益而作伪证——此人为贱民。 用强迫或同意的方式，与亲族的妻子或朋友的妻子发生关系——此人为贱民。 虽有能力赡养，却不养活年迈的父亲与母亲——此人为贱民。 以虚伪的言语打击婆罗门、沙门或其他出家者——此人为贱民。 当食物已上桌，却不给婆罗门或沙门，以谎言搪塞——此人为贱民。 对婆罗门或沙门或其他乞者，出口辱骂——此人为贱民。 在此世愤怒时说谎话，在彼世也不后悔——此人为贱民。 侵吞三宝的供养品，或夺取他人施与僧团之物——此人为贱民。 不敬拜值得敬拜者、不尊重值得尊重者——此人为贱民。 对婆罗门或任何出家者怀有错误期待，做出错误的事——此人为贱民。 在世间以愤怒激动，以吝啬束缚自己，没有布施，只会说「给我！给我！」——此人为贱民。 自我炫耀，贬低他人，以傲慢而骄——此人为贱民。 以愤怒、吝啬、邪见折磨他人——此人为贱民。 不是因为出生而成为贱民，不是因为出生而成为婆罗门；是因为行为而成为贱民，是因为行为而成为婆罗门。 请了知我举出的这个例子——旃陀罗之子摩登伽，以其行为之高洁而著称。 他放弃了骄傲、谦逊，勇于精进，达到了完全解脱的目标——在如此修行之后，在水与火面前，他被称为婆罗门。 出生于婆罗门家的人当中，有许多因行为之恶而堕入恶道——在今生便受到谴责，来世则生于苦处。 出身并不能拯救人，也不能使人堕落；是行为的善与恶，决定了一切。

逐句解释

【婆罗门】停下，秃头！停下，贱民！停下，旃陀罗！

火神婆罗堕阇婆罗门看见世尊前来，以种姓歧视的语言辱骂他——称他为秃头、贱民（vasala）和旃陀罗（最低种姓）。这正是佛陀要挑战和颠覆的偏见的核心：以出身而非行为来定义一个人的价值。

Aggika-Bhāradvāja: "Stop, shaveling! Stop, outcast! Stop, caṇḍāla!"

The insult is triple: shaveling (baldhead — mocking the monk's shaved head), outcast (vasala — the word the sutta will redefine), and caṇḍāla (the lowest of the low in the varna system, considered ritually impure). The brahmin uses ritual purity and birth as weapons. The Buddha's response will systematically dismantle both as criteria for human worth.

易怒、怀恨、恶毒、嫉妒、虚伪——此人为贱民。

世尊列举的第一组「贱民」特征，全是内心的品质：易怒、持续怀恨、心存恶毒、嫉妒他人、言行虚伪。这些内心状态——而非出身——才是真正令人低贱的东西。

One who is irritable, hostile, malicious, envious, hypocritical — know that one as an outcast.

The Buddha begins his redefinition of 'outcast' with inner qualities rather than outer actions. Irritability, persistent hostility, malice, envy, and hypocrisy are all states of mind that generate harm from the inside out. They degrade the person who holds them before they harm anyone else. This is the first inversion: the brahmin calls the Buddha an outcast based on his shaved head; the Buddha shows that outcast status is determined by what lives in the heart.

伤害生命、无有悲悯心——此人为贱民。

主动伤害有情生命，且对它们的痛苦毫无悲悯——这种行为与心态的结合，是真正低贱的标志。悲悯心的缺失，是人性堕落的根本。

One who harms living beings and has no compassion for them — know that one as an outcast.

Violence paired with the absence of compassion is the core of moral degradation in the sutta's framework. The Buddha does not say 'one who accidentally harms' or 'one who harms in self-defence' — but one who harms without any trace of compassion. Compassion (karuṇā) is the recognition that other beings suffer as we do; its absence means treating others as objects rather than subjects.

在村落或荒野中，偷取他人未予之物——此人为贱民。

无论是在人多的村落还是无人的荒野，只要拿取了他人未给予的东西——这就是偷盗，也是令人低贱的行为。「未予之物」这个表达，精确地定义了盗窃：不在于价值大小，而在于是否得到允许。

One who takes in village or wilderness what has not been given — know that one as an outcast.

Theft is defined with canonical precision: 'what has not been given.' The location (village or wilderness) covers both social and private contexts — there is no safe space for theft. The phrasing avoids defining theft by value or by method; the criterion is simply the absence of the owner's consent. This clarity cuts through rationalisation.

借了钱财，被催讨时说「我没有欠你的」——此人为贱民。

借债不还，还否认欠债——这是欺骗与不义的双重罪行。它破坏了人际信任的基础，也是一种间接的偷盗。

One who borrows and when pressed says "I owe you nothing" — know that one as an outcast.

Debt denial is a specific form of both dishonesty and theft. It involves two violations: the original deception of borrowing without intent to repay, and the active lie of denial when pressed. The sutta singles it out because it is a form of harm that wears the face of a normal transaction — it exploits the trust that makes economic life possible.

以强迫或同意的方式，与亲族的妻子或朋友的妻子发生关系——此人为贱民。

与亲属或朋友的妻子私通——无论是强迫还是双方同意——都是对信任关系的根本背叛，是令人低贱的行为。性的不忠不仅是个人道德问题，更是对家庭与友谊纽带的破坏。

One who has intercourse with the wife of a relative or friend, whether by force or by consent — know that one as an outcast.

The inclusion of 'by consent' is notable: the moral failure here is not only about coercion but about the betrayal of trust. A friend's or relative's spouse is in a protected relationship; that protection is violated regardless of whether both parties willingly participate. The harm is to the fabric of trust that holds families and friendships together.

虽有能力赡养，却不养活年迈的父亲与母亲——此人为贱民。

同样的原则在《败亡经》中也出现过：有能力而不尽孝，是真正的低贱。这在两部经典中同时被列为严重的道德过失，足见孝道在佛陀伦理观中的核心地位。

One who though able does not support aging father and mother — know that one as an outcast.

The repetition of this teaching from the Parābhava Sutta underscores its importance. In both the 'causes of downfall' and 'marks of an outcast' lists, neglect of aging parents occupies the same position. The qualification 'though able' remains crucial: this is about chosen neglect, not inability. The parent-child relationship is treated as one of the most fundamental moral obligations in the Canon.

自我炫耀，贬低他人，以傲慢而骄——此人为贱民。

自我吹嘘、贬低他人、以傲慢为荣——这种姿态不仅伤害他人，也是内心贫乏的表现。真正有德行的人无需炫耀；炫耀本身就暴露了内在的空虚。

One who praises oneself and disparages others, puffed up with pride — know that one as an outcast.

Self-promotion and disparagement of others are two sides of the same movement: building oneself up by pushing others down. The 'puffed up' image is telling — the swollen sense of self is all air, no substance. Pride of this kind is particularly corrosive because it masquerades as confidence and self-respect while actually expressing insecurity and contempt.

不是因为出生而成为贱民；不是因为出生而成为婆罗门——是因为行为而成为贱民，是因为行为而成为婆罗门。

这是整部经文的核心宣言：出生不决定人的价值，行为才是。无论出生于何种家庭、何种种姓，都不自动使人高贵或低贱——只有实际的行为与品德才能做到这一点。这是对婆罗门种姓制度的根本性挑战。

Not by birth is one an outcast; not by birth is one a brahmin. By deeds one becomes an outcast; by deeds one becomes a brahmin.

This is the sutta's thesis, stated with maximum clarity. The brahmin system defined status by birth — one was born brahmin or born caṇḍāla, and nothing could change that. The Buddha reverses this entirely: birth is irrelevant; conduct is everything. This was a radical social claim in a rigidly hierarchical society, and the sutta supports it with both the negative list (deeds that make one an outcast) and the positive example that follows.

旃陀罗之子摩登伽，以其行为之高洁而著称，达到了完全解脱。

摩登伽（Mātaṅga）出身旃陀罗，是种姓制度中最低的阶层。然而他通过修行，放弃骄傲、勇于精进，最终达到解脱——成为真正意义上的婆罗门。这个例子证明：出身不是命运，行为才是。

The caṇḍāla's son Mātaṅga became famous for his noble conduct; he reached complete liberation. Many born of brahmin families went to ruin through their deeds.

The counter-example is carefully chosen: not a middle-caste person but the lowest possible birth — caṇḍāla. Mātaṅga attained what brahmin-born practitioners failed to achieve through his conduct, not his birth. The parallel failure of brahmin-born practitioners driven by bad conduct completes the symmetry: birth guarantees nothing in either direction. Conduct alone is the measure.

总结 · Summary

贱民经是《经集》中社会批判色彩最为鲜明的经典。一位婆罗门以种姓之词辱骂世尊，世尊以一系列行为标准逐一定义何为真正的「贱民」——结论是：令人低贱的，不是出身，而是行为。易怒、欺骗、偷盗、不孝、以强力伤害他人、自我炫耀——这些才是真正令人低贱之处；而出身最低的旃陀罗，若行为高洁，也能成为真正的婆罗门。这部经文以彻底的伦理平等主义，挑战了当时印度社会赖以为基础的种姓秩序，其核心宣言至今仍振聋发聩：「不是因为出生，而是因为行为。」

The Vasala Sutta is one of the most socially radical texts in the Pali Canon. Its occasion — a brahmin hurling caste insults at the Buddha — becomes the opportunity for a systematic dismantling of birth-based hierarchy. The Buddha's response is not to claim brahmin status for himself but to redefine the entire framework: the qualities that make one an outcast are behavioural, not genealogical. The list that follows covers harm to beings, dishonesty, theft, sexual betrayal, neglect of parents, pride, and violence — all entirely independent of birth. The counter-example of Mātaṅga, who achieves liberation from the lowest birth, while many brahmin-born practitioners go to ruin, makes the argument irrefutable on its own terms. By deed one becomes what one is — not by the accident of one's birth.
