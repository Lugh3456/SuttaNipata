第四品·第四经 · 清净八偈

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第四品·第三经 目录 Contents 第四品·第五经 →

以下经文据汉译《义足经》（T0198，支谦译，三国·吴）

我见清净，最上最妙—— 如是见者，知彼清净。 若以见清净，若以闻清净—— 若以戒禁清净，若以行法清净—— 执此为清净，彼见未脱离。 婆罗门说，以见得清净—— 沙门说，以戒得清净—— 各执己见，以彼为清净—— 争论不休，未见真清净。 若以洗浴清净，世间多清净—— 若以苦行清净，如何说清净？ 若以此清净，若以彼清净—— 清净非在外，清净在内心。 不以见清净，不以闻清净—— 不以戒禁清净，亦非苦行清净—— 无取无所著，安稳不动摇—— 此名为清净，此名为解脱。

逐句解释

我见清净，最上最妙——如是见者，知彼清净。若以见清净，若以闻清净——若以戒禁清净，若以行法清净——执此为清净，彼见未脱离。

清净八偈（Suddhaṭṭhaka，「清净·八偈」）的主题是：什么是真正的清净（suddhi）？经文先呈现各种被宣称为清净之路的途径：「我见清净」（以自己的见解达到清净）、「以见清净」、「以闻清净」、「以戒禁清净」、「以行法清净」——这覆盖了印度宗教传统中几乎所有的清净路径。对每一条路径，经文都指出同样的问题：「执此为清净，彼见未脱离」——只要还在执取一个外在方法作为达到清净的手段，这个执取本身就是「见」的系缚，尚未脱离。

I see purity as the highest, the most excellent — one who sees thus knows that purity. If purity is through views, through hearing, through precepts and vows, through ascetic practices — clinging to this as purity, that view is not yet abandoned.

The Suddhaṭṭhaka systematically interrogates every external path to purity claimed in the Indian religious landscape: purity through correct views (the philosophical schools), through hearing authoritative teaching, through moral precepts and observances, through ascetic practices. The critique is structural: any method that one holds as 'this is the way to purity' is itself a view to which one clings. The clinging is the problem, not the particular method — but a method that is clung to cannot deliver genuine purity because it perpetuates the grasping mind.

婆罗门说，以见得清净——沙门说，以戒得清净——各执己见，以彼为清净——争论不休，未见真清净。

经文以婆罗门（以见得清净）与沙门（以戒得清净）的争论为例，指出：各执己见作为「清净」的定义，导致的是无休止的争论（vivāda），而不是清净。这里有一个微妙的批评：连「以戒得清净」（这听起来接近佛教的观点）也受到同样的批评——只要「戒」被执取为「通过这个我变得清净」的工具，戒本身就成了执著的对象。清净八偈的批评是全面的：没有任何外在手段能够通过被执取而带来清净。

The brahmin says: purity is through views. The ascetic says: purity is through precepts. Each holding to their own view as the path to purity — disputes do not cease, and true purity is not seen.

The brahmin/ascetic contrast names the two great competing streams of Indian religious practice: the Vedic-philosophical tradition emphasising correct knowledge and the śramaṇa tradition emphasising correct conduct and discipline. By placing both under the same critique, the sutta demonstrates that the problem is not the content of the method but the structure of the claim — 'through this I become pure.' Even ethical discipline, held as an identity-project ('I am the one who purifies through precepts'), perpetuates the grasping that blocks genuine purification. The result is religious controversy without resolution — both sides genuinely believe their method works, neither notices that the believing-structure is the obstacle.

若以洗浴清净，世间多清净——若以苦行清净，如何说清净？清净非在外，清净在内心。

经文以两个反问揭示外在清净方法的荒谬：「若以洗浴清净，世间多清净」——如果沐浴可以带来清净，那么天天洗澡的人岂不是最清净的？「若以苦行清净，如何说清净？」——如果痛苦本身能带来清净，那苦行者受的苦越多越好？这两个反问指向同一个结论：「清净非在外，清净在内心」——真正的清净是内在的，不依赖于任何外在的仪式、行为或苦行。这是古印度宗教批判中一个深刻的转向：清净的场所从外部世界转移到内心。

If bathing brings purity, then there is much purity in the world — if ascetic practice brings purity, what can be called purity? Purity is not outside; purity is within the mind.

The two rhetorical questions demolish the logic of ritual and ascetic purification: if bathing worked, the frequently-bathing masses would be purified; if self-mortification worked, the logic demands ever-increasing pain. Both arguments show the absurdity of locating purity in external acts. The turn inward — 'purity is within the mind' — is the sutta's pivot, but it is not a simple claim that 'just feel good about yourself.' In the Aṭṭhakavagga's frame, inner purity means specifically the absence of grasping: a mind that does not clutch at views, practices, or experiences as identity-projects.

不以见清净，不以闻清净——不以戒禁清净，亦非苦行清净——无取无所著，安稳不动摇——此名为清净，此名为解脱。

结颂以否定式列举所有方法之后，给出真正清净的正面定义：「无取无所著，安稳不动摇」——不执取任何事物（无取），没有任何依附（无所著），因此安稳而不动摇（upekhā，平等心）。「此名为清净，此名为解脱」——清净与解脱是同一件事，不是通过任何方法「获得」的，而是执取消失之后自然显现的状态。这是义足经风格中最接近于将suddhi（清净）与vimutti（解脱）等同的表达。

Not through views is there purity, not through hearing, not through precepts and vows, not through ascetic practice — with no grasping, nothing clung to, stable and unshaken: this is called purity, this is called liberation.

The final verse completes the sutta's argumentative structure: after systematically negating every external path, it offers a positive definition in purely negative terms — no grasping (anupādāya), nothing clung to, stability. This is not a description of a method but of a quality: the mind that has released all methods as identity-projects arrives at genuine purity not by performing another action but by the absence of the grasping that made all previous actions impure. 'This is called purity, this is called liberation' — the two terms coincide: purity is not a precondition for liberation; it is liberation's description.

总结 · Summary

清净八偈以系统性的批判贯穿全经：凡是被执取为「通过这个我变得清净」的方法——见、闻、戒禁、苦行、洗浴——都因其被执取的方式而失效。婆罗门以见得清净，沙门以戒得清净，两者都在执取自己的方法，因此争论不休而未见真清净。清净不在外，清净在内心——但这个「内心的清净」不是又一个可以被执取的方法，而是「无取无所著」之后自然显现的状态：安稳不动摇。这与解脱是同一件事。清净八偈是八偈品对宗教仪式主义与苦行主义最直接的批判，也是对「清净必须通过某种手段获得」这一预设本身的根本质疑。

The Suddhaṭṭhaka runs a systematic critique of every religious claim to purity: views, hearing, precepts, asceticism, ritual bathing — all fail not because the activities are wrong in themselves but because they are grasped as identity-projects ('through this I become pure'). The brahmin and ascetic traditions are placed under equal scrutiny; the problem is structural, not sectarian. 'Purity is within the mind' is the pivot, but it too resists being converted into a new method: the mind that is genuinely pure is characterised by the absence of grasping — no grasping of views, methods, or the identity of being the one who is pure. Liberation and purity coincide in this absence: not an achievement but an unconcealing.
