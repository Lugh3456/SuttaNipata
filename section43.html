第四品·第五经 · 最上八偈

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第四品·第四经 目录 Contents 第四品·第六经 →

以下经文据汉译《义足经》（T0198，支谦译，三国·吴）

执此见为最上，谓余见皆劣—— 此名为著见，智者不如是。 若以自见为胜，论议世间中—— 因此生诤论，以见相高下。 诸见各异说，各谓己为真—— 若人执一见，与余人争论—— 是人不清净，以见故争论。 自见所执持，谓此为清净—— 因此生我慢，以见故轻他。 所见各不同，真伪难分别—— 以见断定他，以见轻视他—— 此人以见故，终不得寂静。 若见有对待，则非最上见—— 若见无对待，则无可执取。 贤圣于世间，不立最上见—— 不以见争论，不以见高下。 无见则无著，无著则寂静—— 此贤圣之道，不执不争论。

逐句解释

执此见为最上，谓余见皆劣——此名为著见，智者不如是。若以自见为胜，论议世间中——因此生诤论，以见相高下。

最上八偈（Paramaṭṭhaka，「最上·八偈」，parama = 最上、最高）直接针对「我的见解是最高的」这种元层面的见解执取。「执此见为最上」——将自己的世界观、宗教观、哲学观视为「最上、最妙、最真实」——这是一切宗教争论的元根源。智者不这样做：智者了解，任何被执取为「最上」的见解，都已经在「见见相斗」的框架中了。「以见相高下」——用见解的高低来排序他人，是这种「最上见」执著的直接结果。

Holding one's view as the highest, saying all other views are inferior — this is called clinging to views; the wise do not do this. If one holds one's own view as superior and argues about it in the world — from this arise disputes, views ranked above and below each other.

The Paramaṭṭhaka targets the meta-level of view-clinging: not just holding a view but holding the view that one's view is the highest. This is the structure of sectarian religious identity — 'my tradition has the truth, others are in error.' The wise person recognises that this structure (ranking views hierarchically from a position of claiming the top) is itself a form of clinging that generates contention. The sutta does not say all views are equally valid; it says the stance of 'mine is highest, theirs are inferior' is what produces the adversarial dynamic.

自见所执持，谓此为清净——因此生我慢，以见故轻他。所见各不同，真伪难分别——以见断定他，以见轻视他——此人以见故，终不得寂静。

「自见所执持，谓此为清净——因此生我慢」：这一颂将「见执」与「慢」（māna，我慢、傲慢）连结。对自己见解的执取自然产生「我比其他人更清净、更有智慧」的傲慢——这是对他人的轻蔑（「以见故轻他」）。「所见各不同，真伪难分别」：从观察者的角度看，各种见解争论不休，真伪确实难以从外部判断——但对「以见断定他、轻视他」的人，经文的评价是明确的：「此人以见故，终不得寂静」。见执是寂静的对立面。

Holding one's own view as purity — from this arises conceit, with views one disparages others. What is seen differs; truth and falsity are hard to distinguish — judging others through views, looking down on others through views — this person, because of views, will never attain stillness.

The link between view-clinging and conceit (māna) is the sutta's sharpest observation: declaring one's view supreme generates a hierarchy with oneself at the top and others below — the structure of contempt. The observation that 'truth and falsity are hard to distinguish from outside' is not relativism but an acknowledgment that competing systems of truth-claim resist external adjudication. What the sutta adjudicates is not the content of views but the quality of holding them: the person who judges and disparages through views will never reach stillness regardless of whether their views happen to be correct.

若见有对待，则非最上见——若见无对待，则无可执取。贤圣于世间，不立最上见——不以见争论，不以见高下。

这两颂是最上八偈的哲学核心：「若见有对待，则非最上见」——任何声称自己是「最上」的见解，都必然将其他见解定位为「较低」，因此它是在一个对待（相对的、比较的）框架中运作的——它有对立面。但「最上见」如果真的是最上的，就不应有对立面（不应有「较低的见解」与之对待）——「若见无对待，则无可执取」：如果一个见解真的无对待，就没有可被执取的固定位置了。贤圣不在世间立「最上见」：圣者的清净不是一个可以被排列在其他见解之上的立场。

A view that has an opposite is not the highest view — a view that has no opposite has nothing to cling to. The noble one does not establish a 'highest view' in the world — does not dispute through views, does not rank views as higher or lower.

This is the Paramaṭṭhaka's most philosophically acute argument: any view that claims to be 'highest' necessarily posits 'lower' views to stand above — it operates within a comparative framework and therefore has an opposite. But a genuinely supreme insight would have no opposite — no 'inferior views' below it. And if a view has no opposite, there is nothing fixed enough to grasp. The noble one, therefore, does not 'establish a highest view': not because they have no insight but because genuine insight does not take the form of a position in a hierarchy of views. This anticipates the logic of later Buddhist philosophy's thoroughgoing critique of metaphysical positions.

无见则无著，无著则寂静——此贤圣之道，不执不争论。

结颂以贤圣之道作收：「无见则无著，无著则寂静」——这与恶意八偈的因果链呼应，但在最上八偈中，「无见」的含义更为深刻：不是没有任何理解，而是不把任何理解固化为「最上见」、「真正清净之见」、「可以用来与他人争论的立场」。「此贤圣之道，不执不争论」：八偈品所有的核心教导在此汇聚——圣者的道路是无执取（anupādāna）与无争论（arana）的，这不是知识上的空白，而是洞见的最高质量：不再需要一个立场来保护自己或压倒他人。

Without views there is no clinging; without clinging there is stillness — this is the path of the noble one: no grasping, no contention.

The closing verse brings together the Aṭṭhakavagga's core teaching in its most concentrated form: the causal chain from non-grasping to stillness, and the identification of this as the characteristic quality of the noble one's path. 'Without views' here reaches its deepest meaning: not cognitive blankness but the absence of the grasping that turns insight into a defended position. The noble one's knowledge does not require a hierarchy of views to stand at the top of — it is complete without opposition. Stillness (upasama) is the result not of winning the argument but of leaving the argument's structure entirely.

总结 · Summary

最上八偈是八偈品中哲学论证最精密的经典之一，针对的是「见执」的元层面：「我的见解是最上的，其余见解都低劣」。经文揭示这种「最上见」的内在矛盾：任何声称「最上」的见解都有对立面（「较低的见解」），因此它在一个对待（相对）框架中运作，不是真正的最上；而如果一个见解真的无对待，就没有可被执取的立场了。对「最上见」的执取产生我慢（傲慢）与对他人的轻蔑，这是以见故争论的根源，也是寂静的对立面。贤圣于世间不立最上见，不以见争论，不以见高下——无执取，无争论，这才是寂静之道。

The Paramaṭṭhaka is the Aṭṭhakavagga's most philosophically precise argument, targeting view-clinging at its meta-level: the claim 'my view is the highest, all others are inferior.' The internal contradiction of this claim is exposed: any view that ranks itself 'highest' operates within a comparative framework (with inferior views below it) and therefore has an opposite — it is not genuinely supreme. A truly supreme insight would have no opposite and therefore no graspable position. Holding the 'highest view' generates conceit and contempt for others, making stillness impossible. The noble one does not establish a highest view in the world — not from ignorance but because genuine understanding does not require a ranked position in a hierarchy of views. Non-grasping and non-contention are the path's mark.
