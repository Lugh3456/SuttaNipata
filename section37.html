第三品·第十一经 · 那罗迦经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第三品·第十经 目录 Contents 第三品·第十二经 →

【序偈：天界的预兆与那罗迦的等待】 那时，诸天王与因陀罗、梵天聚集，宣告：「一位菩萨（Bodhisatta）已降生于人间——他是无上的，是一切有情中最高的，他将在人间的森林，如一头牛王，如一只雄狮，发出他的吼声。」 当仙人阿私陀（Asita）于诸天聚集处听闻此事，来到净饭王（Suddhodana）的宫殿，坐下后，看见了那位孩子——法的继承人，法的宝贝。 阿私陀见到了那孩子，发光耀眼，如火焰燃烧，如太阳在晴空——他充满了喜悦，内心欢欣。 然而，诸天宣告说，这位孩子将证得无上正觉，洞见纯净，怜悯众生，在法轮转动之后——仙人以为自己的老年而悲泣。 「我已年迈，寿命无多——我在这位有功德的人成就正觉之前就会死去，我将听不到他对法的宣说。」 阿私陀回到家中，把这一切告诉他的外甥那罗迦（Nālaka）：「去吧，那罗迦——当你听说觉者出现，就赶快去见他，随他修行！」 那罗迦等待，保守着秘密，直到佛陀在鹿野苑转了法轮——然后他去了，问了关于牟尼行（moneyya-paṭipadā）的问题。 【正文：牟尼行的教导】 【世尊】我将告诉你牟尼行——那是艰难的，难以坚持的：来，我将讲述它。 忍受辱骂，不动摇——不与村民起争执；对谩骂不怒，不傲慢。 就如医生为了受伤者的利益而行走在村庄里——如此，对人对人，心怀善意地游化。 对争论话语感到羞耻，对分裂话语感到羞耻——不说伤害性的话——有戒律，有节制。 就如火焰当中的木头燃烧——牟尼不执取其中的任何部分，不停留，继续行进。 在手持棍棒的人中间，在执着于名誉的人中间——即使被辱骂，牟尼也沉默，不报复任何人。 看见了敌意的危险，理解了和平的获得——聪慧的人忍受，不执着，如地承载一切。 若有人以讥笑或赞美来攻击牟尼——这两者他都平等地承受，不被它动摇——如峰岩不被风所动。 欲望是坑穴，泥土，道路——牟尼知道了它，越过了它，不踏入它——不渴望欲乐，不贪求来世。 这是牟尼行，简要地说——它是不容易的，艰难的——依此而行，牟尼得到安稳。

逐句解释

仙人阿私陀于天界聚集处听闻菩萨降生——来到宫殿见到孩子后，充满喜悦——然而以为自己老迈，将在觉者出现前死去，而悲泣。

那罗迦经的序偈（Pucchā-nidāna，问题的背景）是整部经集中最具叙事性与情感张力的段落之一。老仙人阿私陀（Asita）是传说中在佛陀诞生时曾预言的仙人——他认出了婴儿的大人相，知道他将成为觉者，内心充满喜悦。然而随即的悲泣令人动容：他太老了，他将看不到这一刻的实现，将听不到法轮的转动。阿私陀的悲泣是一种纯粹的精神悲悯——不是为自己的死亡悲伤，而是为无缘亲闻法而悲悼。

The sage Asita, hearing of the Bodhisatta's birth at a divine assembly, came to the palace, saw the child, and was filled with joy — but wept because of his old age: he would die before the awakening, and would not hear the turning of the wheel of Dhamma.

Asita's weeping is one of the most poignant moments in the Sutta Nipata. He recognises the Bodhisatta immediately — the thirty-two marks confirm what the divine announcement declared — and his joy is genuine and immediate. But the recognition of future awakening produces an unexpected grief: he is too old to live to see it. His tears are not self-pity about death itself but sorrow at missing the event of greatest spiritual significance in human history. This combination — joy at the recognition of the extraordinary, grief at being unable to witness its fulfilment — captures something of the relationship between wisdom and limitation that defines human spiritual aspiration.

阿私陀把这一切告诉外甥那罗迦：「去吧，那罗迦——当你听说觉者出现，就赶快去见他，随他修行！」那罗迦等待，直到佛陀转了法轮——然后他去了，问了关于牟尼行的问题。

「那罗迦等待，保守着秘密」（arakkhitā，守护着）——这是那罗迦经叙事中最有意味的细节。那罗迦在舅父的预言之后，隐入了等待：他保守这个秘密，不声张，只是等待着——可能等待了几十年——直到「觉者出现」的消息传来，他立刻前往。这种等待是一种深刻的精神准备：他没有在等待期间去追逐世俗的满足，而是保持内心的预备，以便当时机来临时能够立刻行动。

Asita told his nephew Nālaka everything: 'Go, Nālaka — when you hear of an awakened one, go quickly and follow him.' Nālaka waited, keeping the secret, until the wheel of Dhamma was turned — then he went and asked about the path of the sage.

Nālaka's waiting is the sutta's hidden centre: he receives a prophecy as a young man and keeps it — not broadcasting it, not seeking premature fulfilment — until the moment arrives. This patient, expectant readiness is itself a form of spiritual practice. When the news of the Dhamma's turning arrives, he goes immediately — not requiring persuasion or another confirmation. The entire arc, from Asita's prophetic grief through the waiting to Nālaka's direct question, frames the teaching on the sage's path as something worth waiting a lifetime for.

忍受辱骂，不动摇——不与村民起争执；对谩骂不怒，不傲慢。就如医生为了受伤者的利益而行走在村庄里——如此，对人对人，心怀善意地游化。

「牟尼行」（moneyya-paṭipadā，牟尼的修行方式）的核心是一种在与人接触中的特殊忍耐与善意。「忍受辱骂」——在托钵与弘法中，修行者会遇到各种态度的人；关键是不被其中的负面反应所动摇，不起愤怒，不起傲慢（「我不是这种人」的防御性反应）。「如医生为受伤者」：这个比喻精准地描述了正确的姿态——医生不因患者的痛苦行为（愤怒、不合作）而放弃，而是以专业的善意继续服务，因为目的是治愈而非对等地反应。

Bearing insults without being shaken — not quarrelling with villagers; not getting angry at abuse, not being arrogant. As a physician goes through a village for the benefit of the injured — so, person by person, wander with goodwill.

The path of the sage as described here is not withdrawal from the world but a particular quality of engagement with it: bearing insults without inner disturbance, refusing the reflexive escalations of quarrel, anger, and defensive pride. The physician simile is precise: a doctor encountering a patient's pain, anger, or uncooperativeness does not respond in kind — the goal is healing, which requires consistent goodwill regardless of the patient's condition. Applied to the sage's wandering among villagers, it describes a practice of unconditional positive regard: person by person, maintaining the orientation of goodwill regardless of how one is received.

若有人以讥笑或赞美来攻击牟尼——这两者他都平等地承受，不被它动摇——如峰岩不被风所动。欲望是坑穴，泥土，道路——牟尼知道了它，越过了它。这是牟尼行——它是不容易的，艰难的——依此而行，牟尼得到安稳。

牟尼行的两个顶峰：对讥笑与赞美平等（「如峰岩不被风所动」）——这比仅仅忍受批评更困难，因为赞美同样能动摇人，使人骄慢、自满；以及彻底超越欲望（「知道了它，越过了它」）。「峰岩不被风所动」是整个经集中对平静最有力的自然比喻之一：山峰不因风的方向而倾斜，不因风的强弱而颤抖——不是冷漠，而是扎根。「这是牟尼行——它是不容易的，艰难的」：佛陀对那罗迦没有美化：这条路是真实地困难的，但「依此而行，牟尼得到安稳」——安稳（khema）是真实的目的地。

Whether attacked by mockery or praise — the sage bears both equally, unmoved — like a peak of rock, unmoved by wind. Desire is a pit, a swamp, a road — the sage knows it and crosses it. This is the sage's path — it is not easy, it is difficult — walking thus, the sage attains peace.

The two climaxes of the sage's path: equanimity toward both scorn and praise — the mountain peak simile shows that stability under favour is as important as stability under attack, since praise can destabilise through inflation of the self; and the crossing of desire, which is described as a pit, a swamp, and a road (three images of different kinds of danger and enticement). The closing honesty — 'this is not easy, it is difficult' — is characteristic of the Sutta Nipata's refusal to oversell the path. The promise is real but the effort is genuine. Khema (peace, security) is the destination: not dramatic liberation but the unmovable, rooted ease of a mind that has crossed what it needed to cross.

总结 · Summary

那罗迦经具有经集中最精彩的叙事序偈：老仙人阿私陀在天界聚集处得知菩萨降生，前往宫殿验证，充满喜悦——然后以将看不见法轮转动而悲泣；他将这一切告诉外甥那罗迦，嘱咐他等待。那罗迦保守秘密，等待了数十年，直到佛陀转法轮，立刻前往请教「牟尼行」。佛陀的教导描绘了一条真实艰难的道路：忍受辱骂而不动摇，不与村民争执，如医生般以善意游化，对讥笑与赞美平等承受（如峰岩不被风所动），彻底超越欲望。「这是牟尼行——它是不容易的，艰难的——依此而行，牟尼得到安稳」。这首经是对精神等待（那罗迦的数十年准备）与精神功课（牟尼行的严格要求）的双重颂歌。

The Nālaka Sutta has the Sutta Nipata's richest narrative prelude: the aged sage Asita, learning of the Bodhisatta's birth at a divine assembly, verifies the marks at the palace, fills with joy — then weeps that he will die before the awakening he has recognised. He charges his nephew Nālaka to wait and seek the Buddha when the time comes. Nālaka keeps the secret for decades until the wheel turns, then goes immediately with his question about the path of the sage. The teaching describes a genuinely difficult path: bearing insults without flinching, maintaining goodwill village by village like a physician, receiving both mockery and praise with the equanimity of a mountain peak in wind, crossing desire entirely. 'This is not easy, it is difficult — walking thus, peace is attained.' The sutta honours both the long patient preparation (Nālaka's waiting) and the rigorous ongoing practice (the sage's path) that together constitute the spiritual life.
