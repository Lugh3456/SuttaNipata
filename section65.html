第五品·第十经 · 迦波经

经集 · Sutta Nipata · 彼岸道品

← 第五品·第九经 目录 Contents 第五品·第十一经 →

【迦波问】 告诉我一个岛屿，世尊—— 在洪流中间，在老与死的恐惧中—— 一个岛屿，让我不再陷入苦难—— 告诉我，让我真正了知它。 【世尊答】 迦波，在洪流中间， 在老与死的恐惧中—— 我告诉你那个岛屿—— 没有什么可拥有，没有执取—— 我称之为涅槃， 老与死的灭尽。 了知这个，正念而行的人， 在此世间， 不被魔王所驾驭—— 他不再成为死神的跟随者。

逐句解释

告诉我一个岛屿——在洪流中间，在老与死的恐惧中——一个岛屿，让我不再陷入苦难。

迦波经（Kappa Sutta）以「岛屿」的意象为核心。迦波的问题精准：不是问「如何渡越」，而是问「洪流中间有没有一个稳固的地方」——「在洪流中间」（oghena majjhe）：洪流包括欲望、存在、见解、无明四种洪流（四暴流，cattāro oghā）。迦波不是问彼岸的事，而是问在苦海中间有没有立足之处——这是一个更急迫的问题：此刻我能站在哪里？

Tell me an island, Blessed One — in the middle of the flood, in the fear of aging and death — an island so that I will not fall into suffering — tell me, that I may truly know it.

Kappa's question is more urgent than most in the Pārāyanavagga: not 'how do I cross?' but 'is there an island in the middle?' — a stable place right here, in the midst of the flood. The 'four floods' (kāma, bhava, diṭṭhi, avijjā — sensuality, becoming, views, ignorance) are the ocean of conditioned existence. Kappa is not asking about the far shore but about a refuge that exists within the current experience of crossing — something to stand on now. This temporal urgency shapes the answer.

没有什么可拥有，没有执取——我称之为涅槃，老与死的灭尽。了知这个，正念而行的人，不被魔王所驾驭——他不再成为死神的跟随者。

世尊的答案出人意料：岛屿不是一个地方，而是一种内在状态——「没有什么可拥有，没有执取」（akiñcana anādāna）。「没有执取」本身就是岛屿：不拥有、不执取，这个状态就是涅槃，就是老与死的灭尽之处。「不被魔王所驾驭」（na vasaṃ gacchati maccurājassa）：执取是魔王控制意识的把手；没有执取，魔王就没有地方可以抓住你。「不再成为死神的跟随者」：生死轮回的追随是由执取驱动的——没有执取，追随就不再发生。这个答案把「岛屿」完全内化为「无执取的内心状态」。

Having nothing, not grasping — I call this Nibbāna, the ending of aging and death. Knowing this, mindfully practising — in this world, one is not ruled by Māra — one is no longer a follower of the death-king.

The answer redefines 'island': it is not a location but a quality — having nothing (akiñcana), not grasping (anādāna). Nibbāna is precisely this: the complete release of possession and grasping. The island is not somewhere you travel to; it is a quality of mind that you can establish right here, in the middle of the flood. 'Not ruled by Māra' — Māra (the death-king, the personification of conditioning) rules through grasping; remove the grasping and Māra has no handle. 'No longer a follower of the death-king' — the rounds of rebirth are powered by craving and grasping; the one who genuinely establishes non-grasping is no longer swept along by the current.

总结 · Summary

迦波经以「洪流中间的岛屿」为问题，得到最令人意外的答案：岛屿不是地方，而是内心状态——「没有什么可拥有，没有执取」。这个「无执取」的内心状态就是涅槃，就是老与死的灭尽。能在洪流中间建立这种无执取状态的人，「不被魔王所驾驭」，「不再成为死神的跟随者」。迦波经把涅槃完全定义为内在的「无执取」，而非遥远的彼岸目的地——这是彼岸道品对「岛屿」意象最深刻的运用。

The Kappa Sutta's question — 'tell me an island in the middle of the flood' — receives an answer that radically internalises the island metaphor: the island is not a place but a quality, 'having nothing, not grasping.' Nibbāna is defined as this non-possessing, non-grasping quality of mind — something that can be established right here, in the midst of conditioned existence, rather than reached only by crossing to a distant shore. The one who establishes this quality is no longer ruled by Māra and no longer follows the death-king — because the grasping that powers conditioned existence has been released. The Kappa Sutta is the Pārāyanavagga's most direct equation of Nibbāna with the quality of non-grasping.
