第五品·第四经 · 弥多求经

经集 · Sutta Nipata · 彼岸道品

← 第五品·第三经 目录 Contents 第五品·第五经 →

【弥多求问】 我问你，具知者—— 苦从哪里来？各种各样的苦， 在世间的种种——从哪里来？ 【世尊答】 你问我关于苦的来源—— 我将告诉你，如我所知： 世间种种苦，从执取而来。 不了知的人执取而来—— 痴者接连造作苦—— 因此，了知的人不执取， 不执取则不再出生。 【弥多求问】 那使苦止息的——我们问你这个—— 告诉我，对这个你了知了什么： 如何渡越忧愁与悲恸， 如何渡越苦？ 【世尊答】 我不是通过传统而告诉你—— 通过直接了知，我来说： 舍离渴爱，就不再执取， 不再执取就不再出生。 正念，独居，放弃对世间的喜悦—— 如此行者，不再出生、老—— 这就是牟尼的清净，我告诉你。 【弥多求】 大师的话令我喜悦—— 没有执取，已被解脱—— 瞿昙，你真正了知了一切的来源。 世尊，带给众生祝福， 永远！永远！

逐句解释

世间种种苦，从执取而来。不了知的人执取而来——痴者接连造作苦——因此，了知的人不执取，不执取则不再出生。

弥多求经（Mettagū Sutta）直接问苦的来源。世尊的回答以最简洁的形式给出：苦从执取（upādāna）而来。「不了知的人执取」：执取的直接原因是不了知（亦即无明）。「了知的人不执取」——了知什么？了知执取本身的本质：执取就是苦的来源，而了知这一点就使执取失去了基础。「不执取则不再出生」：在缘起链中，执取是「有」（bhava，存在）的条件，有是「生」（jāti）的条件——切断执取就切断了「再生」（rebirth）的环节。

All the various sufferings in the world arise from grasping. The unknowing person brings about grasping — the fool makes suffering after suffering — therefore the knowing person does not grasp; not grasping, one is not born again.

The direct answer — suffering arises from grasping — is the liberating diagnosis in its most compressed form. 'Grasping' (upādāna) is the specific link in the dependent origination chain between craving (taṇhā) and becoming (bhava): what is craved and then grasped becomes the condition for a new round of becoming. Knowing this — not just intellectually but in the direct way that changes behaviour — removes the basis for grasping. The unknowing person makes grasping after grasping; the knowing person, having seen grasping as the root of suffering, simply does not do it.

我不是通过传统而告诉你——通过直接了知，我来说：舍离渴爱，就不再执取，不再执取就不再出生。正念，独居，放弃对世间的喜悦——如此行者，不再出生、老。

「我不是通过传统而告诉你——通过直接了知，我来说」（na anussavena brūmi — paccanubhūtam etaṃ）：这是彼岸道品中最明确的知识论声明之一。佛陀的教导不是传统（anussava，口耳相传的知识、书本知识），而是直接了知（paṭicca-samuppāda，直接经验过的）。这个区分对彼岸道品来说很关键：弟子们来自婆罗门传统（三吠陀是最高权威），而佛陀宣称的是更高的知识来源：直接了知，而非传统权威。「正念，独居，放弃对世间的喜悦」——三个实践支柱，简洁而完整。

I do not tell you this through tradition — I tell you what has been directly known: abandon craving, and there is no more grasping; not grasping, one is not born again. Mindfulness, solitary dwelling, abandoning the world's delights — one who practises thus is not born or aged again.

'Not through tradition but through direct knowledge' is one of the Pārāyanavagga's most epistemologically significant declarations. The students come from the brahminical tradition in which the Vedas (received, memorised, transmitted text) are the supreme epistemic authority. The Buddha's claim to teach from direct experience rather than received tradition repositions the source of authority from textual transmission to personal realisation. This does not dismiss knowledge but reorders what kind of knowing has liberating power: knowing-through-transmission leaves grasping intact; knowing-through-direct-experience dissolves it.

总结 · Summary

弥多求经以「苦从哪里来？」与「如何止苦？」为双重问题，得到最精炼的双重答案：苦从执取来（执取←无明），止苦靠舍离渴爱（无执取→不再出生）。「我不是通过传统而告诉你——通过直接了知」是弥多求经最重要的认识论声明：佛陀的教导的权威来自直接经验，而非传统传承。弥多求最后发出感叹：「大师的话令我喜悦——没有执取，已被解脱」，是彼岸道品中少数以弟子的领悟宣告（rather than单纯的问答）结束的经典，体现了问道与答道的即时解脱效果。

The Mettagū Sutta pairs its two questions — where does suffering come from? how is it crossed? — with two matching answers: suffering from grasping (grasping from ignorance); crossing by abandoning craving (no grasping → no rebirth). The epistemological declaration — 'not through tradition but through direct knowledge' — distinguishes the liberating knowing from transmitted learning and asserts experience over authority. Mettagū's closing response — 'your words delight me, released without grasping' — makes this one of the Pārāyanavagga's few dialogues in which the student's liberation is announced within the exchange itself, showing the immediate transformative effect of the encounter.
