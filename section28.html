第三品·第二经 · 精进经

经集 · Sutta Nipata

← 第三品·第一经 目录 Contents 第三品·第三经 →

当我在尼连禅河岸边，致力于精进努力，为了获得安稳而修行禅定—— 魔罗前来，说了悲悯的话：「你憔悴了，脸色发黄，死亡就在你身旁。」 「你有一千分之一死的机会，两分之一是活的——活着吧！活着是更好的，活着你才能积累功德。」 「持守梵行，事奉圣火，积累大量的功德——努力有什么用呢？」 「精进的道路是困难的，难以完成的——」魔罗就这样站在佛陀身旁，说了这些话。 【世尊】你是来了，懈怠者的朋友！恶魔，你来这里是为了什么目的？ 我不需要哪怕是最小数量的功德，魔罗应该对那些有功德的人说。 我有信心，精进，还有慧——当我如此精进时，为什么你向我问生死？ 这风甚至能令河流干涸——在我精进之时，我的血液为什么不能干涸？ 当血液干涸时，胆汁与痰液也干涸了——当肌肉萎缩时，心会变得更宁静——我的正念、慧力与禅定更加稳固。 这样生活，经历了极度的感受——我的心不渴求感官欲乐——观见一个众生的纯净。 欲望是你的第一支军队，第二支称为不满，第三是饥渴，第四是渴爱。 第五支军队叫做昏沉与睡眠，第六是恐惧，第七是疑惑，第八是伪善与固执。 利益、名誉、荣耀与错误地获得的名声，以及自我赞叹而轻视他人。 这些是你的军队，黑暗的攻击者——非英雄者无法胜过，胜过了你才能得到幸福。 我背负着芦苇！宁愿死在战场！而不是活着被征服！ 有些沙门和婆罗门被淹没其中，不了解你那支军队的道路——他们不了解有德行者的居所。 魔罗的军队四面环绕，我以正念的大象应战——我将进入那些人不会进入的地方，就像国王受过训练的战象进入战场。 你所制造的那些东西，我的心已经不被欺骗了——对你，以及你的军队，这一战中，我必定取胜！

逐句解释

魔罗前来，说了悲悯的话：「你憔悴了，脸色发黄，死亡就在你身旁。活着吧！活着是更好的，活着你才能积累功德。」

精进经叙述菩萨在尼连禅河（Nerañjarā）岸边苦行精进时与魔罗（Māra）的对抗。魔罗的诱惑策略是值得注意的：他不以欲望诱惑，而以「悲悯」和「理性」说话——「你快死了，活着才能做好事」。这是最微妙的魔罗形式：以合理的理由（活着能积功德）来动摇修行者的精进。任何动摇精进的声音，无论其包装多么合理，都是魔罗的声音。佛陀立刻认出了他：「你是来了，懈怠者的朋友！」

Māra came and said these compassionate words: 'You are gaunt, your colour is poor, death stands near you. Live! Living is better — living, you can accumulate merit.'

Māra's strategy in this sutta is sophisticated: he does not tempt with pleasure but with apparently sensible advice. 'You are dying — save yourself' is framed as concern. 'Living allows merit' is framed as spiritual pragmatism. This is Māra at his most insidious — not offering the obvious distractions of sensual pleasure but supplying rational-sounding justifications for abandoning the effort. The Bodhisatta's immediate recognition — 'you are the friend of the indolent' — is an act of discernment: any voice that counsels retreat from the effort of liberation, however reasonable it sounds, is recognised as adversarial to liberation.

我有信心，精进，还有慧——当我如此精进时，为什么你向我问生死？这风甚至能令河流干涸——在我精进之时，我的血液为什么不能干涸？

菩萨以信（saddhā）、精进（viriya）、慧（paññā）三种力量回应魔罗的「理性」。「这风甚至能令河流干涸」：自然界的强力现象被用来类比精进的力量——风足以令河流干涸，精进足以令血液（生命力）干涸，却不会动摇决心。这不是夸张的英雄主义，而是对一种真实状态的描述：当信、精进与慧三者结合，外在的肉体困苦（憔悴、血液干涸）不仅无法动摇决心，反而成为精进的见证。

I have faith, energy, and wisdom — why do you ask me about life and death? The wind could even dry up a river — why should my blood not dry up in my striving?

The Bodhisatta's three resources — saddhā (faith/confidence), viriya (energy/effort), and paññā (wisdom) — are exactly the qualities that Māra's appeal is designed to undermine: faith in the path, energy to pursue it, and the wisdom to see through sophisticated rationalisation. The river-and-wind simile turns Māra's 'you will die' into confirmation of commitment: yes, the body may be depleted; this is not a reason to stop. The willingness to be physically exhausted in pursuit of liberation is the opposite of the cowardice Māra is advocating. The blood may dry — the resolve will not.

欲望是你的第一支军队，第二支称为不满，第三是饥渴，第四是渴爱。第五支军队叫做昏沉与睡眠，第六是恐惧，第七是疑惑，第八是伪善与固执。利益、名誉、荣耀与错误地获得的名声，以及自我赞叹而轻视他人。

精进经中最著名的段落：魔罗十军的枚举。这十支军队不是外在的军事力量，而是内心的障碍：欲望（kāma）、不满（arati）、饥渴（khuppipāsā）、渴爱（taṇhā）、昏沉与睡眠（thīna-middha）、恐惧（bhaya）、疑惑（vicikicchā）、伪善与固执（makkha-thambha）、利益与名声（lābha-sakkāra-siloka）、自我赞叹与轻视他人（attukkaṃsana-paravambhana）。这十支「军队」是所有修行者在内心战场上需要面对的力量——精进经是对这场内在战争的诗意描述。

Sensual desire is your first army; discontent is called the second; the third is hunger and thirst; the fourth is craving. The fifth is sloth and torpor; the sixth is fear; the seventh is doubt; the eighth is hypocrisy and stubbornness. Gain, renown, and honour wrongly obtained — and self-praise while disparaging others.

Māra's ten armies are the most systematic catalogue of inner obstacles in the Sutta Nipata. Each 'army' is a psychological force that can bring the practitioner's effort to a halt: sensual desire (the pull toward pleasure), discontent (restlessness and dissatisfaction with the path), hunger and thirst (physical needs becoming compelling), craving (the deep drive toward gratification), sloth and torpor (the failure of energy), fear (especially of the unfamiliar depth of practice), doubt (about the teacher, the teaching, the self), hypocrisy and stubbornness (performing practice without inner commitment), and finally the subtle seductions of gain, status, and self-aggrandisement. The list is a psychological map of how liberation can be thwarted from the inside.

我背负着芦苇！宁愿死在战场！而不是活着被征服！……对你，以及你的军队，这一战中，我必定取胜！

菩萨以战士的语言回应：「我背负着芦苇」（eso me uttaro kāso，这是我的最高挑战/我已经准备好）、「宁愿死在战场而不活着被征服」——这是古印度战士在决战前的誓言语言，被用来描述对魔罗的精神抵抗。这不是暴力语言，而是不退转的决心：无论肉体付出什么代价，都不会从精进中退缩。「以正念的大象应战」：正念（sati）被比喻为大象——稳重、力量、不可阻挡。以正念为战具，菩萨进入任何普通人不会进入的深度。

I take up the challenge! Better to die in battle than to live as a conquered one! … Against you and your armies, in this battle, I will certainly prevail!

The language shifts into military heroism — the warrior's vow before a decisive engagement. This is deliberate: the battle against Māra's armies is presented as the ultimate heroism, requiring the same quality of unconditional commitment that a warrior gives to a battle where death is preferable to defeat. The 'elephant of mindfulness' (sati-nāga) is a counterimage to Māra's armies — a single quality, but one of enormous power and stability, capable of moving through terrain that other forces cannot enter. The declaration of certain victory is not boast but the warrior's necessary orientation: entering battle already knowing the outcome, because the quality of commitment has decided it.

总结 · Summary

精进经是经集中最具戏剧性的经典之一，以诗意的战争叙事描述菩萨在尼连禅河岸边精进修行时与魔罗的对抗。魔罗的诱惑是微妙的：不以感官欲乐，而以「悲悯」和「理性」——「你快死了，活着才能做好事」。菩萨立刻识破，以信、精进、慧三种力量回应。经文中最著名的段落是魔罗十军的枚举：欲望、不满、饥渴、渴爱、昏沉睡眠、恐惧、疑惑、伪善固执、名利、自赞毁他——这是对内在修行障碍最系统的描述之一。菩萨以「正念大象」为武器，以宁死不退的决心应战。精进经是对精进（viriya）这一佛教核心美德的最有力的颂歌——精进不是固执，而是看清了魔罗真面目之后的不退转。

The Padhāna Sutta is the Sutta Nipata's most dramatically rendered encounter — the Bodhisatta's battle with Māra during the period of intense striving before enlightenment. Māra's strategy is subtle: not sensual temptation but apparently rational compassion ('you are dying — retreat and live to accumulate merit'). The Bodhisatta's recognition is immediate and his response is triple: faith, energy, wisdom. The sutta's centrepiece is the enumeration of Māra's ten armies — the psychological forces that defeat practitioners from the inside. Against these, the 'elephant of mindfulness' is deployed, and an unconditional vow is made: better death than retreat. The Padhāna Sutta is the Pali Canon's great hymn to viriya — the energy that does not flinch, not because it is reckless but because it has seen clearly what it is fighting and why the fight matters absolutely.
